## Description

Sanjeev Arora is a co-author of [[malladi-2024-finetuning-2305-17333]] and is affiliated with [[princeton-university]]. In this source, he helps connect the empirical success of MeZO with an optimization-theoretic explanation grounded in pretrained language-model geometry.

## Key Contributions

- Co-authored the overall MeZO proposal for forward-only language-model fine-tuning.
- Helped shape the theoretical argument that local landscape structure can make large-scale zeroth-order tuning practical.
- Contributed to positioning MeZO as a memory-efficient alternative to both full fine-tuning and in-context-only adaptation.
