## Description

Dan Klein is a professor at UC Berkeley and a co-author of [[zhao-2021-calibrate-2102-09690]]. He is a prominent researcher in NLP, known for foundational work in parsing, structured prediction, and natural language generation.

## Key Contributions

- Supervised or co-advised the research on contextual calibration for few-shot in-context learning.
- Contributed to the theoretical framing of output distribution shift as the unifying cause of few-shot prompt instability.
