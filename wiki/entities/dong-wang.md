## Description

Dong Wang is a researcher affiliated with [[university-of-illinois-urbana-champaign]] and a co-author of [[unknown-nd-inference]]. In this paper, Wang contributes to the study of how RAG performance changes with effective-context budget and parameter allocation.

## Key Contributions

- Co-authors the formalization of optimal RAG performance under a maximum effective context length.
- Helps evaluate DRAG and IterDRAG across multiple knowledge-intensive QA datasets.
- Supports the computation allocation analysis used to predict strong inference configurations.
