## Description

Charlie Snell is a co-first author of [[gudibande-2023-false-2305-15717]] and is affiliated with [[uc-berkeley]]. In this paper, he helps examine why imitation models can sound persuasive while remaining substantively weaker than ChatGPT.

## Key Contributions

- Co-authored the paper's experimental study of imitation scaling.
- Helped connect qualitative judgments with targeted benchmark failures.
- Contributed to the paper's critique of near-parity claims for public ChatGPT imitators.
