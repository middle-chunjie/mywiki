## Description

Sergey Levine is a co-author of [[gudibande-2023-false-2305-15717]] and is affiliated with [[uc-berkeley]]. In this paper, he is part of the author group testing how much imitation can recover from a weaker open-source base model.

## Key Contributions

- Co-authored the comparison between broad imitation, local imitation, and stronger base models.
- Helped interpret why human preference signals overestimate imitation success.
- Contributed to the argument that imitation does not eliminate the underlying capabilities gap.
