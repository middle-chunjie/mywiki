## Description

RAR-b is a reasoning-oriented retrieval benchmark used in [[unknown-nd-rare-2410-20088]] to test whether RARe improves retrieval on tasks that require more complex inference than standard benchmark collections. The paper evaluates eight parsed RAR-b datasets because exemplar pools must be recoverable from the original task data.

## Key Contributions

- Serves as the paper's main reasoning-heavy out-of-domain evaluation suite.
- Exposes some of RARe's largest gains, including `+2.72` absolute over Promptriever for Llama-3.1-Instruct on average RAR-b performance.
