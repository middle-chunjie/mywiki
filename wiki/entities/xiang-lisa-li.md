## Description

Xiang Lisa Li is a researcher at [[stanford-university]] and a co-author of the DSP paper. In this paper, she contributes to using in-context learning as a programmable interface for retrieval-grounded NLP.

## Key Contributions

- Co-authors a framework that bootstraps demonstrations from end-task labels instead of hand-labeling intermediate reasoning steps.
- Helps position DSP as a bridge between few-shot prompting and retrieval-augmented question answering.
