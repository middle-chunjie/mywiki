## Description

Sanmi Koyejo is a Stanford University researcher and senior co-author of [[schaeffer-2023-emergent-2304-15004]], which argues that many reported emergent abilities of large language models are mirages created by evaluation choices.

## Key Contributions

- Co-authored the paper's argument that metric geometry and finite-sample resolution can explain many emergence claims without invoking abrupt capability phase shifts.
- Supported the cross-domain validation showing similar apparent emergence can be induced in multiple vision architectures.
