## Description

Rylan Schaeffer is a Stanford University researcher and first author of [[schaeffer-2023-emergent-2304-15004]], which argues that many reported LLM emergent abilities are artifacts of evaluation metrics and sampling resolution.

## Key Contributions

- Co-developed the paper's scaling-law-based explanation for metric-induced emergence.
- Helped validate the claim across GPT-3/InstructGPT arithmetic tasks, BIG-Bench meta-analysis, and induced emergence experiments in vision.
