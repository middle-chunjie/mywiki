## Description

Christopher Potts is a researcher at [[stanford-university]] and a co-author of the DSP paper. The paper places him among the authors advancing programmatic retrieval-grounded prompting for knowledge-intensive NLP.

## Key Contributions

- Co-authors DSP's three-stage design of demonstration bootstrapping, iterative search, and grounded prediction.
- Contributes to the framing of composable natural-language transformations as a reusable software abstraction.
