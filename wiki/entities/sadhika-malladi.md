## Description

Sadhika Malladi is a co-author of [[gao-2025-metadata-2501-01956]] and a member of Princeton Language and Intelligence at [[princeton-university]]. In this work, she helps position metadata conditioning as a low-overhead improvement to language-model pre-training.

## Key Contributions

- Co-authored the main experiments showing MeCo reaches the `240B`-token baseline with only `160B` training tokens.
- Helped analyze why validation perplexity and downstream task performance can diverge.
- Contributed to the paper's discussion of steerability and harmful-generation reduction.
