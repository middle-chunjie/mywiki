## Description

Jason D. Lee is a co-author of [[malladi-2024-finetuning-2305-17333]] and is affiliated with [[princeton-university]]. In this work, he contributes to the theoretical and optimization analysis explaining why MeZO can scale to very large language models.

## Key Contributions

- Co-authored the dimension-free per-step analysis tying MeZO's slowdown to local effective rank rather than parameter count.
- Helped formulate the global convergence result under PL-style assumptions and bounded gradient covariance.
- Contributed to the broader argument that adequate pretraining changes the practical optimization regime for zeroth-order methods.
