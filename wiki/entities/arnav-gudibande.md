## Description

Arnav Gudibande is a co-first author of [[gudibande-2023-false-2305-15717]] and is affiliated with [[uc-berkeley]]. In this paper, he helps frame the central claim that broad imitation of ChatGPT mostly transfers style rather than capability.

## Key Contributions

- Co-authored the empirical study comparing broad imitation against task-specific imitation.
- Helped analyze the gap between human preference judgments and benchmark results.
- Contributed to the paper's argument that stronger base pretraining matters more than more imitation data.
