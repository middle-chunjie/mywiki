## Description

Darren Key is a Cornell University researcher and co-author of [[tang-2024-worldcoder-2402-12275]]. In this paper he is part of the team that frames code-synthesized world models as a practical agent architecture for planning and transfer.

## Key Contributions

- Co-authored the WorldCoder architecture that learns Python transition and reward programs from interaction data.
- Helped develop the optimism-constrained learning setup for sparse-reward exploration and instruction following.
- Contributed to the analysis across Sokoban, MiniGrid, and AlfWorld.
