## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2022; re-verify against recent literature in dialogue NLU and open-world intent discovery.

This paper proposes MTP-CLNN, a two-stage framework for new intent discovery (NID) — the task of uncovering novel intent categories from unlabeled user utterances in dialogue systems. Stage 1 (MTP) fine-tunes BERT with a joint cross-entropy loss on external intent datasets and an MLM loss on in-domain data, producing intent-aware utterance representations without requiring in-domain labels. Stage 2 (CLNN) replaces noisy pseudo-labeling with a neighborhood-aware contrastive objective that pulls together utterances with similar nearest-neighbor sets, yielding compact clusters. On three benchmarks (BANKING, StackOverflow, M-CID), MTP-CLNN outperforms prior state-of-the-art by large margins in both unsupervised and semi-supervised settings.

## Problem & Motivation

Practical dialogue systems face the problem of discovering emerging user intents from unlabeled utterances. Existing NID methods either require large labeled in-domain corpora for representation learning or rely on pseudo-labeling for clustering, which propagates noise and degrades performance. Vanilla pre-trained language models (e.g., raw BERT) perform poorly on NID because their representations are not attuned to intent semantics. The paper asks: (1) how to learn semantic utterance representations without abundant in-domain annotations, and (2) how to cluster utterances without noisy pseudo-labels.

## Method

**Stage 1 — Multi-task Pre-training (MTP):**

- Initialize from `bert-base-uncased`.
- Joint loss on two sources:
  - `L_stg1 = L_ce(D_external; θ) + L_mlm(D_all_internal; θ)`
  - Supervised cross-entropy on external public intent dataset (CLINC150, 8 domains, ~18K utterances).
  - Self-supervised MLM on all in-domain data (labeled + unlabeled).
- In semi-supervised NID: replace external CE data with in-domain labeled data for continual pre-training.
- Training until convergence on external data, then early-stopping with patience `20` on in-domain validation.
- Learning rate: `5e-5`.

**Stage 2 — Contrastive Learning with Nearest Neighbors (CLNN):**

- Encode all utterances with MTP model; mine top-`K` nearest neighbors per utterance via inner product (FAISS).
- Neighborhood size `K = 50` for BANKING/M-CID; `K = 500` for StackOverflow; empirical rule: `K ≈ 0.5 × (train_size / num_classes)`.
- Per minibatch of size `M`: for each `x_i` sample one neighbor `x'_i` from `N_i`; apply random token replacement (RTR, prob=`0.25`) to generate augmented views `~x_i`, `~x'_i`.
- Construct `2M × 2M` binary adjacency matrix `A'` (positive if neighbor or same known-intent label).
- Contrastive loss (generalized SupCon):
  - `l_i = -(1/|C_i|) * Σ_{j∈C_i} log [exp(sim(~h_i, ~h_j) / τ) / Σ_{k≠i}^{2M} exp(sim(~h_i, ~h_k) / τ)]`
  - `L_stg2 = (1/2M) Σ_i l_i`
  - Temperature `τ = 0.07`; similarity = dot product on L2-normalized `128-d` MLP-projected embeddings (two-layer MLP from `768-d`).
- Neighborhoods updated every `5` epochs; learning rate `1e-5` (BANKING/M-CID) or `1e-6` (StackOverflow).
- Final clustering: non-parametric `k-means` on stage-2 representations.

**Data augmentation comparison:** Random Token Replacement (RTR) and Stop-Words Replacement (SWR) both outperform dropout, token shuffling, and EDA; RTR used by default.

## Key Results

- **Unsupervised NID** (Table 2): MTP-CLNN vs. strongest prior (SAE-DCN): BANKING NMI +18.86 (81.80 vs 62.94), ARI +30.06, ACC +26.54; StackOverflow NMI +17.37, ARI +32.65, ACC +24.34; M-CID NMI +29.26, ARI +22.19, ACC +26.07.
- **Semi-supervised NID KCR=25%, LAR=10%** (Table 3): MTP-CLNN vs. DAC: BANKING NMI +17.26 (84.11 vs 69.85), ARI +24.13; StackOverflow NMI +25.71, ARI +33.71.
- **MTP alone** (unsupervised) already surpasses all baselines; CLNN adds roughly +5% NMI, +10% ARI, +10% ACC on top of MTP.
- **Label efficiency**: from KCR=75% to KCR=25% on M-CID, MTP drops only 8.55% NMI vs. ~21.58% for DAC.
- **Ablation of MTP** (Table 4): both PUB (external CE) and MLM components are individually necessary; their combination (MTP) gives the best unsupervised NMI/ARI.
- All experiments on single RTX-3090, averaged over 10 seeds.

## Limitations

- Evaluated only on balanced datasets; real-world intent distributions are typically highly imbalanced, and performance under class imbalance is unknown.
- Discovered clusters are assigned numeric labels only; the framework cannot automatically generate human-readable intent names, limiting direct deployment.
- Requires prior knowledge of the number of clusters `K_clusters` (though off-the-shelf methods can estimate it).
- Relies on BERT-base; scaling to larger PLMs or instruction-tuned models is not explored.
