## Summary

The paper presents `nomic-embed-text-v1`, a fully reproducible English text embedding model that targets long-context retrieval while staying compact at `137M` parameters. The training recipe combines long-context masked language modeling, weakly supervised contrastive pretraining over `470M` public pairs filtered down to about `235M`, and supervised contrastive finetuning on `1.6M` labeled examples. Architecturally, the model adapts BERT with RoPE, SwiGLU, FlashAttention, zero dropout, and Dynamic NTK scaling so a model trained at `2048` tokens can run at `8192` tokens during inference. The main claim is not only strong performance, but full auditability: weights, code, and curated data are released, and the resulting model exceeds OpenAI Ada-002 and `text-embedding-3-small` on average MTEB and LoCo scores.

## Problem & Motivation

Most strong open-source embedding models at the time were either limited to short contexts such as `512` tokens, or else were large and expensive enough to be impractical for many retrieval systems. Closed-source long-context models were competitive, but their data curation and training pipelines were opaque. The paper targets this gap by asking whether a relatively small encoder can support long documents, remain reproducible end to end, and still reach competitive retrieval and semantic embedding quality on both short-context and long-context benchmarks.

## Method

- **Three-stage recipe**: train a long-context encoder with masked language modeling, then weakly supervised contrastive pretraining, then supervised contrastive finetuning. The contrastive objective uses cosine similarity `s(q, d)` inside an InfoNCE loss `L = -(1/n) Σ_i log exp(s(q_i, d_i)/τ) / Σ_j exp(s(q_i, d_j)/τ)`.
- **Backbone**: start from BERT-base and replace absolute positional embeddings with [[rotary-positional-embedding]], switch GeLU to SwiGLU, use FlashAttention, set dropout to `0`, and make vocab size a multiple of `64`, yielding a `137M`-parameter encoder called [[nomic-bert-2048]].
- **Sequence length strategy**: all training stages run with `max_seq_len = 2048`; inference extrapolates to `8192` tokens using [[dynamic-ntk-scaling]] with `α = 2`, rather than native 8k training.
- **MLM stage**: pretrain on BooksCorpus plus a 2023 Wikipedia dump packed into `2048`-token chunks, use masking rate `30%`, remove NSP, optimize with AdamW at `lr = 5e-4`, `β1 = 0.9`, `β2 = 0.98`, `weight_decay = 1e-5`, linear warmup over `6%` of steps, global batch size `4096`, gradient accumulation `8`, DeepSpeed ZeRO-2, `bf16` mixed precision, and no gradient clipping.
- **Weakly supervised contrastive pretraining**: curate `470M` public text pairs across `29` datasets, then apply [[consistency-filtering]] by keeping a pair only if its document is among the top-`2` nearest neighbors of the query under `gte-base`; this reduces the corpus to about `235M` pairs. Train with global batch size `16384`, AdamW `lr = 2e-4`, `β1 = 0.9`, `β2 = 0.999`, `weight_decay = 0.01`, warmup `700` steps, inverse-square-root decay, GradCache, and task prefixes such as `search_query`, `search_document`, `classification`, and `clustering`.
- **Long-context data augmentation**: because many public pairs are shorter than `2048` tokens, add title-body Wikipedia pairs and S2ORC abstract-fulltext pairs so the encoder sees examples where semantics are distributed across long spans.
- **Supervised finetuning**: optimize a hard-negative variant of the contrastive loss by adding `H = 7` mined negatives per pair, train for `1` epoch with batch size `256`, AdamW `lr = 2e-5`, warmup `400` steps, linear cooldown, and random sampling among mined negatives to reduce false-negative damage.
- **Supervised data**: finetune on MSMarco, NQ, NLI, HotpotQA, FEVER, MEDI subsets, WikiAnswers, and Reddit, totaling about `1.6M` examples; retrieval datasets mine negatives with `gte-base` by selecting the top `20` non-positive documents for each query.

## Key Results

- Aggregate benchmark table: [[nomic-embed-text-v1]] reaches `62.39` on [[mteb]], `85.53` on LoCo, and `54.16` on Jina Long Context, versus `60.99 / 52.70 / 55.25` for Ada-002 and `62.26 / 82.4 / 58.21` for `text-embedding-3-small`.
- GLUE sanity check for the backbone: [[nomic-bert-2048]] averages `0.84` across 8 GLUE tasks, close to MosaicBERT `0.85` and above JinaBERTBase `0.83`, suggesting the long-context architectural changes do not collapse general encoder quality.
- MTEB: `nomic-embed-text-v1` gets `62.4` average, beating Ada-002 `61.0`, `text-embedding-3-small` `62.3`, Jina v2 `60.4`, and matching or beating several larger open models such as E5 Large v2 `62.3`.
- Jina Long Context benchmark: average scores are `48.5 / 52.1 / 54.2` at sequence lengths `128 / 512 / 8191`, outperforming Jina embeddings base v2 at all three lengths (`44.0 / 47.3 / 51.9`) but remaining below `text-embedding-3-small` and `text-embedding-3-large`.
- LoCo: `nomic-embed-text-v1` scores `85.3` at `2048`, `85.6` at `4096`, and `85.5` at `8192`; the ablated variant reaches `86.9` at `8192`, and both variants clearly exceed Ada-002 `52.7` and `text-embedding-3-small` `82.4`.
- Model efficiency: the system stays in the `137M` parameter class while competing with much larger baselines; at `4096`, it is near E5-Mistral's `87.8` LoCo average despite the latter being `7B` parameters.

## Limitations

- The model is only trained natively up to `2048` tokens and relies on extrapolation for `4096` and `8192`, so long-context behavior still depends on the robustness of Dynamic NTK scaling rather than direct long-context optimization.
- On the Jina Long Context benchmark, the model still trails `text-embedding-3-small` and `text-embedding-3-large`, especially at `8191` tokens, so the open long-context recipe does not fully close the gap to the strongest closed systems.
- The release is English-only, which limits transfer to multilingual retrieval or cross-lingual embedding use cases.
- The main model is finetuned on datasets such as FEVER and HotpotQA that overlap with evaluation ecosystems used in BEIR-style retrieval benchmarking; the paper provides an ablated variant partly to expose this dependence, but comparability to strict zero-shot settings remains imperfect.
