## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2022; re-verify against recent literature.

PaLM studies whether scale, cleaner data mixtures, and better distributed systems can keep improving few-shot generalization in large language models. The paper trains a dense decoder-only Transformer with `540.35B` parameters on `780B` tokens using Google's Pathways system across `6144` TPU v4 chips, combining SwiGLU MLPs, parallel residual blocks, multi-query attention, RoPE, and a `256k` SentencePiece vocabulary. Beyond reporting unusually high training efficiency (`46.2%` model FLOPs utilization), the paper shows state-of-the-art few-shot performance on `28/29` English NLP tasks, strong reasoning gains from chain-of-thought prompting, competitive code and translation ability, and dedicated analyses of memorization, contamination, bias, and toxicity. It is both a scaling paper and a systems paper for dense LLM training.

## Problem & Motivation

The paper asks whether few-shot language understanding and generation had genuinely plateaued after GPT-3-era scaling, or whether better model scale, training data quality, and systems efficiency could still unlock new behavior. Prior large models already showed broad capability, but they remained limited on multi-step reasoning, multilingual generation, and code, while training at larger scale was operationally difficult across multiple accelerator pods. PaLM is motivated by two intertwined goals: build a very large dense autoregressive Transformer that is sample-efficient enough to improve across many tasks, and show that Pathways can train such a model efficiently enough to make the experiment practical.

## Method

- **Architecture**: dense decoder-only [[transformer]] with three scales; the largest PaLM uses `118` layers, `d_model = 18432`, `48` query heads, attention head size `256`, and `540.35B` parameters.
- **Block design**: replaces the standard serialized Transformer block with a parallel formulation `y = x + MLP(LN(x)) + Attention(LN(x))`, which improves large-scale training speed by about `15%`.
- **MLP / attention changes**: uses [[swiglu]] activations, [[multi-query-attention]] for shared key-value projections during decoding, no dense or layer-norm biases, shared input/output embeddings, and [[rotary-positional-embedding]] instead of absolute or relative position embeddings.
- **Tokenization**: trains a lossless [[sentencepiece]] vocabulary with `256k` tokens; numbers are digit-split and out-of-vocabulary Unicode falls back to UTF-8 bytes.
- **Pretraining data**: trains for one epoch over `780B` tokens with a [[pretraining-data-mixture]] of `50%` multilingual social media conversations, `27%` multilingual filtered webpages, `13%` English books, `5%` GitHub code, `4%` multilingual Wikipedia, and `1%` English news.
- **Infrastructure**: PaLM 540B is trained on `6144` TPU v4 chips over two pods; each pod keeps a full parameter copy, partitions weights with `12`-way model parallelism plus `256`-way fully sharded data parallelism, and uses activation rematerialization rather than pipeline parallelism.
- **Optimization**: initializes kernels with fan-in scaling, trains with Adafactor using `lr = 1e-2` for the first `10k` steps then `1 / sqrt(k)` decay, `beta_1 = 0.9`, `beta_2 = 1 - k^-0.8`, global-norm clipping `1.0`, dynamic weight decay `lr^2`, and auxiliary `z_loss = 1e-4 * log^2 Z`.
- **Training schedule**: uses sequence length `2048` and batch growth for 540B from `512` to `1024` to `2048`, corresponding to `1M`, `2M`, and `4M` tokens per step through `255k` training steps.
- **Evaluation recipe**: measures broad [[few-shot-learning]] performance, code, translation, reasoning, multilingual generation, [[memorization]], and [[data-decontamination]]; reasoning results rely on `8-shot` [[chain-of-thought]] prompting and optional external calculator use.

## Key Results

- **English NLP**: PaLM 540B beats prior single-checkpoint pretrained LM SOTA on `24/29` tasks in `1-shot` and `28/29` tasks in few-shot evaluation, with average `63.9` on NLG and `74.7` on NLU.
- **Knowledge / reasoning**: reaches `69.3` on `5-shot` [[mmlu]] versus `67.5` for Chinchilla 70B, and hits `58%` on GSM8K with `8-shot` chain-of-thought plus calculator versus the prior `55%` system that required finetuning plus a verifier.
- **BIG-bench**: `5-shot` PaLM 540B surpasses the average human score on BIG-bench aggregate evaluation and the paper reports SOTA on `44/58` overlapping BIG-bench tasks against previously published large models.
- **Code**: pretraining-only PaLM 540B gets `26.2 pass@1` and `76.2 pass@100` on HumanEval with only about `2.7B` Python tokens; PaLM-Coder 540B improves to `88.4 pass@100`, `82.5 pass@25` on TransCoder, and `82.1 pass@1` on DeepFix.
- **Translation**: few-shot BLEU reaches `44.0` for `en->fr`, `37.4` for `en->de`, `47.5` for `de->en`, and `43.8` for `ro->en`; for `de->en` and `ro->en`, these results exceed the older supervised baselines cited in the paper.
- **Systems efficiency**: training throughput reaches `238.3K` tokens/sec at batch size `2048`, with `46.2%` model FLOPs utilization and `57.8%` hardware FLOPs utilization on `6144` TPU v4 chips.

## Limitations

- The paper documents non-trivial safety and quality risks rather than solving them: toxicity remains high (`0.46` first-sentence toxicity probability on non-toxic prompts for 540B; `0.56` over `128` decode steps), and fairness analysis is limited to English benchmarks.
- Memorization increases with scale: the 540B model exactly reproduces held-out `50`-token continuations for `2.4%` of sampled training spans, and examples repeated more than `500` times are memorized at rates above `40%`.
- Training stability is imperfect; the 540B run experienced loss spikes about `20` times and required checkpoint rollback plus skipping `200-500` batches as an ad hoc mitigation.
- Translation and multilingual generation remain uneven: `en->kk` few-shot BLEU is only `5.1` against a supervised `15.5`, and non-English generation lags English even after finetuning.
- Some benchmark gains are hard to interpret cleanly because parts of the evaluation ecosystem are partially contaminated or prompt-sensitive, and the model still underperforms humans on a substantial fraction of BIG-bench tasks.
