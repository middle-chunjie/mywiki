## Summary

ChatQA proposes a two-stage instruction-tuning recipe for open conversational question answering systems that must answer follow-up questions over provided or retrieved context. Stage 1 builds general assistant behavior from a broad supervised instruction mixture, while stage 2 specializes the model for context-grounded QA using conversational QA data, single-turn QA data, and explicit unanswerable examples. The paper also adapts retrieval for multi-turn conversations by fine-tuning dense retrievers on conversational query-context pairs instead of paying the latency and API cost of query rewriting. Across 10 conversational QA benchmarks, ChatQA-70B reaches an average score of `54.14`, slightly above GPT-4's `53.90`, while human evaluation shows mostly ties with GPT-4 and exposes remaining weaknesses on tabular reasoning and abstention.

## Problem & Motivation

The paper targets a difficult deployment setting where users ask follow-up questions conversationally, answers must stay grounded in long provided or retrieved documents, and systems should abstain when the evidence is missing. Prior expert models were often dataset-specific, while black-box assistants such as GPT-4 were strong but not reproducible. The authors aim to build a white-box family of LLMs that match or exceed proprietary systems on zero-shot conversational QA, improve retrieval for multi-turn questions, and reduce hallucination in unanswerable cases without relying on synthetic supervision from OpenAI models.

## Method

- **Stage-1 supervised fine-tuning**: collect `128K` instruction-following examples from Soda, ELI5, FLAN/chain-of-thought sets, Self-Instruct, Unnatural Instructions, OpenAssistant, Dolly, and private conversational data; unify everything into a `System`/`User`/`Assistant` chat format.
- **Stage-2 context-enhanced instruction tuning**: continue tuning on a blend of conversational QA, contextual single-turn QA, and retained stage-1 data; append the relevant context after the system role and add answer-type-specific instructions before the question or dialogue.
- **HumanAnnotatedConvQA**: build `7k` document-grounded dialogues with an average of `5` user-agent turns, then add `1.5k` unanswerable turns by deleting answer-supporting text spans and training the model to emit `Sorry. I cannot find the answer based on the context`.
- **SyntheticConvQA**: generate another `7k` dialogues from `~1k`-word documents using GPT-3.5-turbo, averaging `4.4` turns, plus `1.5k` synthetic unanswerable turns; mark chunk overlap with the answer using 4-gram recall thresholds of `> 0.5` for "high" and `< 0.1` for "low".
- **Retriever adaptation for multi-turn QA**: fine-tune a single-turn dense retriever on conversational history plus the current question; for synthetic data, choose the gold chunk by the maximum 4-gram recall between each chunk and the agent answer.
- **Model and retrieval setup**: train ChatQA variants on Llama2-`7B`/`13B`/`70B` and in-house GPT-`8B`/`22B`; chunk long documents into `~300` words, retrieve `top-5` chunks for Doc2Dial/QuAC/QReCC and `top-20` for TopiOCQA/INSCIT to match context length.

## Key Results

- **Overall QA quality**: ChatQA-70B reaches `54.14` average over `10` datasets, versus GPT-3.5-turbo `50.37` and GPT-4 `53.90`; ChatQA-13B reaches `50.86`, slightly above GPT-3.5-turbo.
- **Stage-2 tuning matters**: ChatQA-22B improves over GPT-22B-SFT from `38.83` to `53.53` (`+14.70`), and ChatQA-13B improves over Llama2-13B-Chat from `40.34` to `50.86` (`+10.52`).
- **Retriever fine-tuning is competitive with rewriting**: on Dragon, fine-tuning gets average `top-1 = 52.72` and `top-5 = 80.67`, versus rewrite `54.46` and `80.13`; on E5-unsupervised, fine-tuning jumps to `47.79`/`75.00` from rewrite `33.23`/`61.02`.
- **Ablations confirm each ingredient**: removing stage-1 drops average score from `54.08` to `52.18`; removing single-turn QA data drops it to `52.25`; removing conversational QA data drops it further to `48.97`.
- **Human evaluation is more conservative than automatic metrics**: against GPT-4, ChatQA-70B ties on `69.09%` of comparisons, wins `13.81%`, and loses `17.10%`.
- **Unanswerable handling improves but does not fully close the gap**: on QuAC and DoQA unanswerable evaluation, ChatQA-70B with `1.5k` abstention samples scores `77.25`, beating GPT-3.5-turbo's `73.27` but trailing GPT-4's `80.73`.
- **Inference is retrieval-sensitive**: replacing the fine-tuned retriever with original Dragon reduces retrieval-dataset average from `42.31` to `40.50`, and using `top-10` contexts underperforms `top-5`.

## Limitations

- The headline margin over GPT-4 is very small (`54.14` vs. `53.90`) and does not fully survive human evaluation, where GPT-4 still has a slightly higher win rate.
- The model remains weaker on table-heavy reasoning and abstention: GPT-4 leads on avg-table (`70.10` vs. `66.42`) and on the QuAC/DoQA unanswerable average (`80.73` vs. `77.25`).
- Some gains depend on non-public curation choices, especially the private `7k` HumanAnnotatedConvQA dataset and the exact stage-2 blend construction.
- Retrieval robustness is not solved: answer quality still depends on retriever quality, the number of supplied chunks, and train-test mismatch between continuous training contexts and retrieved chunks.
