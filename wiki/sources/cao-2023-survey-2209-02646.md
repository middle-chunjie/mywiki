## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

This survey synthesizes the diffusion-model literature up to roughly 2023, organizing the field around the core DDPM and score-SDE formulations and then tracing four major improvement directions: sampling acceleration, diffusion-process design, likelihood optimization, and bridging arbitrary distributions. It reviews both discrete and continuous-time formulations, including ELBO-based DDPM training, reverse-time SDEs, and probability-flow ODEs, then connects these foundations to conditional generation, latent diffusion, manifold or discrete diffusion, and cross-domain applications. Beyond taxonomy, the paper compiles benchmark tables across datasets such as CelebA-64, ImageNet-64, and CIFAR-10, highlighting the quality-speed frontier through FID, IS, NLL, and NFE. The result is a useful early-stage map of modern diffusion research rather than a new algorithmic contribution.

## Problem & Motivation

Diffusion models had rapidly become a dominant family of deep generative models, but the literature was fragmenting across discrete DDPM variants, score-based SDE formulations, acceleration methods, new forward processes, and an expanding application space in vision, audio, language, and scientific domains. The paper argues that researchers lacked a unified taxonomy for understanding which advances target sampling efficiency, which alter the forward or reverse process, which improve likelihood training, and which generalize diffusion beyond Gaussian-to-data generation. It therefore aims to provide a mathematically grounded survey that connects the original formulations to later improvements and application domains while also summarizing benchmark evidence under explicit numbers of function evaluations.

## Method

- **DDPM core formulation**: the survey presents the forward Markov diffusion as `q(x_{1:T}|x_0) = \prod_{t=1}^{T} q(x_t|x_{t-1})` and the learned reverse chain as `p_{\theta}(x_{0:T}) = p(x_T)\prod_{t=1}^{T} p_{\theta}(x_{t-1}|x_t)`, with `p_{\theta}(x_{t-1}|x_t) = \mathcal{N}(x_{t-1}; \mu_{\theta}(x_t,t), \Sigma_{\theta}(x_t,t))`.
- **Training objective**: it decomposes the DDPM variational bound into `L = L_T + \sum_{t>1} L_{t-1} + L_0`, where `L_{t-1}` is a KL term between the posterior and learned reverse step, then shows the common simplification to the noise-prediction loss `L_simple` on `\epsilon_{\theta}(\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_t}x_0 + \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_t}\epsilon, t)`.
- **Continuous-time view**: the paper recasts diffusion as an SDE `dx = f(x,t)dt + g(t)dw`, gives VP and VE processes, and derives the reverse-time SDE `dx = [f(x,t) - g(t)^2 \nabla_x \log p_t(x)] d\bar{t} + g(t) d\bar{w}` with score network `s_{\theta}(x,t)`.
- **Deterministic sampling view**: it introduces the probability-flow ODE `dx = {f(x,t) - 0.5 g(t)^2 \nabla_x \log p_t(x)} dt`, which shares marginals with the SDE and motivates fast ODE solvers such as DDIM, PNDM, DEIS, and DPM-Solver.
- **Conditional generation**: the survey treats conditional diffusion as modeling `p_0(x|c)` with `s_{\theta}(x,t,c)` and reviews both classifier guidance and classifier-free guidance for labels, text, and self-supervised conditioning signals.
- **Taxonomy of improvements**: enhanced diffusion methods are classified into four families: sampling acceleration, diffusion-process design, likelihood optimization, and bridging distributions.
- **Sampling acceleration subfamilies**: within acceleration, the paper further groups methods into knowledge distillation, training-schedule design, training-free ODE or SDE samplers, and hybrids that merge diffusion with GANs or VAEs.
- **Process-design extensions**: it covers latent diffusion with joint objectives such as `L = L_{Enc}(z_0|x) + L_{Dec}(x|z_0) + L_{DSM}({z_t}_{t=0}^{T})`, plus new forward processes in pixel space and extensions to manifolds, discrete spaces, graphs, and function spaces.
- **Likelihood and transport**: the survey reviews MLE-oriented analyses that connect denoising score matching to likelihood, hybrid objectives such as `L_hybrid = L_DSM + \lambda L_VLB`, and diffusion-inspired bridges between arbitrary source and target distributions.

## Key Results

- The paper organizes diffusion-model advances into `4` top-level algorithm families and discusses `4` major application areas: computer vision, sequential modeling, audio, and AI for science.
- In the sampling-acceleration literature it summarizes, progressive distillation starts with a `2x` acceleration rate, while later methods such as TRACT and consistency models extend the reported acceleration rate to `64` and `1024`, respectively.
- In the CelebA-64 benchmark table, `DDPM++` reaches `FID = 1.90` at `NFE = 1000`, while `NCSN++` reaches `FID = 1.92` with `NLL = 1.97` at the same `NFE`.
- On the same benchmark, `DPM-Solver Discrete` retains `FID = 2.82` at `NFE = 20`, illustrating the quality-speed trade-off captured by the survey's tables.
- Also on CelebA-64, `Progressive Distillation` reports `FID = 3.00` at `NFE = 4`, showing how far the literature had pushed low-step sampling by the time of the survey.

## Limitations

- The survey is anchored in literature available up to about 2023, so many post-2023 developments in text-to-image, video diffusion, controllable generation, and large-scale multimodal systems are necessarily absent.
- Its benchmark synthesis aggregates numbers across different datasets, samplers, and experimental protocols, so the tables are informative but not a perfectly controlled apples-to-apples comparison.
- Much of the concrete benchmark evidence is image-centric, whereas sections on NLP, audio, and AI-for-science are more coverage-oriented and less quantitatively unified.
- As a survey, it contributes taxonomy and synthesis rather than a new model, new dataset, or new empirical evaluation under a single standardized pipeline.
