## Summary

DRAMA is a dense retrieval training framework that transfers the generalization, multilinguality, and long-context priors of large language models into sub-1B retrievers. The paper combines two ingredients in a single-stage contrastive recipe: diverse LLM-generated augmentation data and pruned decoder-only LLM backbones initialized from Llama 3.2/3.1 family models. Augmentation spans cropped-sentence pseudo-queries, LLM-generated queries, and listwise LLM reranking labels over large web and multilingual corpora. Across BEIR, MIRACL, MTEB retrieval subsets, and long-context benchmarks, the resulting 0.1B, 0.3B, and 1B models consistently match or surpass strong encoder baselines while preserving multilingual performance and much better context extrapolation than similarly sized pre-LLM-era encoders.

## Problem & Motivation

Small dense retrievers are efficient, but with limited supervised fine-tuning data they usually generalize worse than LLM-based retrievers, especially for multilingual and long-context retrieval. Directly deploying 7B-8B retrievers is expensive because corpus encoding and query inference scale poorly with model size. The paper therefore asks whether LLM strengths can be distilled into much smaller retrievers without multi-stage pretraining from scratch. Its answer is to move LLM cost to training time by generating richer augmentation data and by pruning decoder-only LLMs into compact bidirectional encoders that retain multilingual and long-context priors.

## Method

- **Training objective**: the retriever is trained with InfoNCE, `` `L(q, D+, DN) = -log exp(Sim(q, D+) / tau) / sum_{Di in {D+ union DN}} exp(Sim(q, Di) / tau)` ``, where negatives are the union of hard negatives and in-batch negatives.
- **Augmentation source 1, sentence cropping**: from corpus `C`, randomly cropped sentences are used as pseudo-queries; an 8B LLM retriever retrieves top-`k = 50` documents, top `1..10` become positives, and ranks `31..50` become hard negatives.
- **Augmentation source 2, synthetic query generation**: Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct generates a query for each document, then the same LLM retriever mines positives and negatives from corpus retrieval results.
- **Augmentation source 3, listwise reranking**: the instruction LLM reranks top-`k = 20` retrieved candidates; reranked top-`1` is the positive and bottom `10` candidates are hard negatives, injecting the instruction model's relevance preferences.
- **Rejected augmentation variant**: direct triplet generation lets the instruction LLM synthesize `(task, query, positive, negative)` tuples without a seed corpus, but the paper finds this pure-synthetic route much less effective.
- **Backbone construction**: Llama-3.2-1B, itself pruned from Llama-3.1-8B, is structurally pruned into `0.1B` and `0.3B` models, then used as a retriever encoder with bi-directional attention enabled during fine-tuning.
- **Pruning objective**: masks over layers, hidden dimensions, heads, and MLP widths are optimized with hard-concrete variables and constrained losses such as `` `L_head = lambda_head * (sum_i z_i^head - H_T) + phi_head * (sum_i z_i^head - H_T)^2` ``, added to the LM loss.
- **Pruning configuration**: the first pruning stage uses about `0.5B` tokens; the recovery pretraining stage uses `26B` tokens over English plus `19` non-English languages. Resulting models support max context length `8192`.
- **Training data mix**: the final recipe mixes sentence-crop, query-generation, and rerank augmentations over English web, multilingual web, and multilingual Wikipedia with sampling ratio `2:1:1`, plus about `2M` supervised E5 fine-tuning instances.
- **Optimization details**: documents are chunked to at most `256` tokens; the 0.1B/0.3B models train with `7` hard negatives per query, the 1B model with `3`; batch sizes are `2048`, `1024`, and `256`; all variants train for `200,000` steps on `32` A100 GPUs. MRL is used so embeddings can be truncated after training.

## Key Results

- On BEIR, `Drama_0.1B` reaches `56.9` nDCG@10, matching ArcticEmb-v2-M (`56.9`) and beating mGTE-Dense (`54.3`) with a similarly small model.
- On BEIR, `Drama_0.3B` reaches `58.0`, outperforming ArcticEmb-v2-L (`57.2`) by `0.8`; `Drama_1B` reaches `59.1`, slightly above Gecko (`58.0`) and MistralE5 (`59.0`).
- On MIRACL, all Drama variants set a stronger multilingual frontier for their scale: `70.4` (0.1B), `71.4` (0.3B), and `71.7` (1B), all above M3-BGE-Dense (`69.2`).
- On multilingual generalization, `Drama_0.3B` scores `54.8` on MTEB-FR, `63.0` on MTEB-ZH, and `55.6` on MTEB-DE, showing that a pruned LLM backbone can stay competitive outside English.
- For long-context retrieval without long-context fine-tuning, Drama obtains `47.1`, `48.8`, and `54.8` average nDCG@10 on MLDR for 0.1B, 0.3B, and 1B models; the 1B variant rises to `62.3` after MLDR fine-tuning.
- On NeedleRetrieval at length `4096`, `Drama_0.1B` scores `48.5` versus `13.8` for E5-v2-base; at length `32768`, `Drama_1B` still scores `19.0`, well above MistralE5's `6.6`.
- Ablation shows all three augmentation types matter: using sentence crops plus generated queries reaches `53.1` on BEIR, while adding listwise rerank labels raises this to `54.5`; rerank data is also the most expensive component at about `10k` GPU hours versus `0.1k` and `1k`.

## Limitations

- Multilingual gains depend on language coverage during pruning; the paper notes that languages included in pruning generalize much better than unseen languages such as Polish.
- The supervised fine-tuning setup intentionally excludes domain-specific retrieval datasets such as finance or medicine, so domain transfer beyond the chosen benchmarks is still underexplored.
- Training uses contexts up to only `256` tokens even though the pruned backbones support much longer windows, so long-context gains rely heavily on extrapolation rather than matched-length supervision.
- The direct triplet-generation augmentation path is not very effective, suggesting that smaller retrievers still benefit more from real-corpus grounding than fully synthetic retrieval data.
- DRAMA is evaluated mainly on retrieval; the paper leaves broader embedding tasks, instruction-following embeddings, and reasoning-intensive retrieval as future work.
