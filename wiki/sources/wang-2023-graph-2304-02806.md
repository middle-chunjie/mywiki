## Summary

The paper proposes Graph Mixture of Experts (GMoE), a sparse graph neural network architecture that increases capacity for structurally diverse graphs without matching dense inference cost. Each layer contains multiple message-passing experts with different hop sizes, and a noisy top-`k` gate routes each node to a small subset of experts so that nodes with similar neighborhoods share specialists. To avoid routing collapse, the training objective adds importance and load-balancing penalties on top of the task loss. Across ten OGB tasks spanning graph, node, and link prediction, GMoE consistently improves GCN/GIN baselines, including `+1.81` ROC-AUC on ogbg-molhiv and `+0.89` Hits@20 on ogbl-ddi, while maintaining comparable FLOPs and similar measured inference latency.

## Problem & Motivation

The paper targets a mismatch between graph diversity and standard homogeneous GNN design. Real-world graphs can vary in neighborhood scale, structure, node type, and edge semantics, but common GCN/GIN-style models force all nodes to share the same aggregation mechanism. The authors argue that stronger training diversity from graph augmentation or pretraining is only useful if the model can adapt its aggregation behavior per node, and that this adaptation must preserve inference efficiency for large-scale applications such as recommendation and molecular screening.

## Method

- **GMoE layer**: replace each backbone layer with `n` experts, where the first `m` experts aggregate over `N_i` (hop-1) and the remaining `n - m` aggregate over `N_i^2` (hop-2). The per-node update is a gated sum of expert message functions `E_o(h_j, e_ij, W)`.
- **Noisy top-`k` routing**: compute routing as `G(h_i) = Softmax(TopK(Q(h_i), k))`, with `Q(h_i) = h_i W_g + ε · Softplus(h_i W_n)` and `ε ~ N(0, 1)`. This selects a sparse subset of experts for each node while preserving differentiable routing scores.
- **Backbone instantiation**: the same recipe is applied to both GCN and GIN, yielding GMoE-GCN and GMoE-GIN by replacing every original layer with its expert-routed counterpart.
- **Anti-collapse regularization**: add importance balancing `L_importance(H) = CV(Importance(H))^2`, where `Importance(H)` sums gate values over the batch, and load balancing `L_load(H) = CV(sum p)^2`, where `p` is the probability that an expert is selected.
- **Training objective**: optimize `L = L_EM + λ (L_load(H) + L_importance(H))`, where `L_EM` is the task loss and `λ` is tuned.
- **Pretraining integration**: combine GMoE with [[graphmae]] using the masked reconstruction loss ``L(H, M) = D(d(f(M · H)), H)``; the paper later compares GIN and GMoE-GIN with and without this pretraining.
- **Hyperparameters**: grid search `n ∈ {4, 8}`, `m ∈ {0, n/2, n}`, `k ∈ {1, 2, 4}`, and `λ ∈ {0.1, 1}`. For pretraining, use batch size `1024`, learning rate `0.001`, weight decay `0`, `100` epochs, and masking ratio `0.25`.
- **Complexity control**: match FLOPs to the single-expert baseline by shrinking hidden width to `s = s_0 / sqrt(k)`, making the selected-expert cost proportional to `k` rather than `n`; the gating cost is negligible because `n << s`.

## Key Results

- On graph classification, GMoE-GCN improves over GCN from `68.87 ± 1.51` to `70.28 ± 1.36` ROC-AUC on ogbg-molbbbp and from `76.06 ± 0.97` to `77.87 ± 1.03` on ogbg-molhiv.
- On graph regression, GMoE-GCN reduces RMSE from `1.114 ± 0.036` to `1.087 ± 0.043` on ogbg-molesol and from `2.640 ± 0.239` to `2.500 ± 0.193` on ogbg-molfreesolv.
- On node and link prediction, GMoE-GCN raises ogbn-protein ROC-AUC from `73.53 ± 0.56` to `74.48 ± 0.58`, ogbn-arxiv accuracy from `71.74 ± 0.29` to `71.88 ± 0.32`, ogbl-ppa Hits@100 from `18.67 ± 1.32` to `19.25 ± 1.67`, and ogbl-ddi Hits@20 from `37.07 ± 0.051` to `37.96 ± 0.082`.
- With GIN backbones, GMoE-GIN improves the average of four graph classification datasets from `69.63` to `70.17` without pretraining and from `70.71` to `71.31` with GraphMAE pretraining.
- Ablations show sparse routing helps accuracy as well as efficiency: on ogbg-molfreesolv, `n = m = 4, k = 1` achieves `2.500 ± 0.193` RMSE versus `2.856 ± 0.118` for dense `k = 4`, and on moltox21, setting `λ = 0.1` improves ROC-AUC from `72.87 ± 1.04` to `75.45 ± 0.58`.
- The complexity analysis is reflected in runtime: on an NVIDIA A6000, inference for `10,000` samples is reported as `30.2 ± 10.6 ms` for GMoE-GCN versus `36.3 ± 17.2 ms` for GCN.

## Limitations

- The evaluation is primarily empirical and centered on relatively standard backbones (`GCN`, `GIN`), so the paper does not establish a strong theoretical account of when expert diversity should help.
- The gains are consistent but often modest, and some settings regress, such as GMoE-GIN without pretraining on ogbg-moltoxcast (`-0.44`) and with pretraining on ogbg-molbbbp (`-1.32`).
- Expert diversity is limited to hop-1 versus hop-2 aggregation; the paper does not explore heterogeneous expert families such as GAT- or GraphTransformer-style aggregation.
- Training converges more slowly than the single-expert baseline because each expert receives fewer updates early in optimization.
- Comparison against stronger contemporary graph models is limited; the authors note broader evaluation as future work.
