## Summary

The paper proposes Debater, an LLM-based dense retriever that improves document embeddings by making the document encoder perform multiple latent reasoning steps before search. Instead of representing each document with one end-of-sequence embedding, Debater inserts a Chain-of-Deliberation over `m` prompt tokens and uses the most query-aligned intermediate step during contrastive training. A self-distillation objective then transfers the best step-level ranking distribution back into the final document embedding so the last token compresses useful cues from earlier reasoning states. Trained on the public E5 corpus and evaluated on BEIR, Debater improves average retrieval quality over prior baselines and remains competitive with larger `7B` retrievers while using MiniCPM backbones as small as `2.4B`.

## Problem & Motivation

The paper targets a limitation of LLM-based dense retrieval: most systems compress each document into a single embedding, which can miss the multiple semantic facets needed to match diverse queries. The authors argue that LLMs already have strong reasoning capacity, but standard dense retrievers do not use it when constructing document representations. Debater is motivated by the idea that a short latent reasoning chain before retrieval can progressively refine a document embedding and improve cross-task zero-shot generalization.

## Method

- **Base retrieval setup**: queries and documents are mapped into one embedding space with `h^q = LLM(q, </s>)[-1]` and similarity `f(q, d) = sim(h^q, h^d)`, where Debater uses cosine similarity.
- **Chain-of-Deliberation**: for each document, the model appends reasoning prompt tokens `t_1, ..., t_m` and produces step-specific embeddings `h_i^d`; the final step is `h_m^d = LLM(d, t_1, ..., t_m)`. The paper sets CoD length to `m = 8` in the main system.
- **Step selection objective**: each step is scored as `f(q, d(t_i)) = sim(h^q, h_i^d)`, and the document-level score becomes `f_max(q, d) = max_{1<=i<=m} sim(h^q, h_i^d)`. Contrastive training minimizes `L_c = -log exp(f_max(q, d^+)) / (exp(f_max(q, d^+)) + sum exp(f_max(q, d^-)))`.
- **Self Distillation**: the teacher distribution `P(d_i|q)` is built from the best-performing step across CoD, while the student distribution `Q(d_i(t_m)|q)` uses only the last-step embedding. Debater minimizes `L_t = KL(P || Q)` so the last token absorbs useful matching signals from earlier reasoning steps.
- **Final objective**: the training loss is `L = L_c + L_t`, combining retrieval supervision and step-to-final distillation.
- **Training details**: Debater is initialized from MiniCPM-`2.4B` and MiniCPM-`4B`, trained for `1000` steps with AdamW, batch size `256`, cosine decay, `3%` warmup, and peak learning rate `2e-4`.
- **Data and negatives**: training uses the public E5 dataset with about `1.5M` samples, with `1` hard negative from E5 plus `7` in-batch negatives for each query.
- **Implementation**: the system is built in OpenMatch with flash-attention and LoRA enabled for efficiency, and evaluated with `nDCG@10` on `14` public BEIR zero-shot datasets.

## Key Results

- On BEIR average nDCG@10, Debater-`2.4B` reaches `0.533` and Debater-`4B` reaches `0.543`, outperforming BM25 (`0.437`), GTR (`0.486`), SGPT (`0.511`), and Cohere Emb-V3 (`0.520`).
- On the CPT subset average, Debater-`4B` reaches `0.582`, higher than Debater-`2.4B` (`0.571`), SGPT (`0.550`), and E5-Mistral-`7B` (`0.571`); Debater-`2.4B` matches E5-Mistral while using roughly `35%` of the parameters.
- The strongest single-dataset gains over E5-Mistral include TREC-COVID (`0.795` vs `0.708`) and FEVER (`0.859` vs `0.731`) for Debater-`2.4B`, while Debater-`4B` further reaches `0.836` on TREC-COVID and `0.857` on FEVER.
- Ablations show both modules matter: on MiniCPM-`2.4B`, average score rises from vanilla `0.504` to Debater `0.522`; on MiniCPM-`4B`, it rises from `0.496` to `0.535`.
- Increasing thinking depth helps up to a point: performance improves clearly up to depth `4`, Debater-`2.4B` plateaus around `8`, and both scales degrade when CoD is extended to about `12`.

## Limitations

- Debater adds extra reasoning steps before producing document embeddings, so it increases encoding cost and may hurt latency-sensitive retrieval deployments.
- The paper shows that deeper CoD can cause overthinking; beyond moderate depths the extra computation does not reliably improve retrieval and can reduce accuracy.
- Experiments focus on BEIR and a smaller low-resource subset for scale analysis, so evidence on very large production corpora and online serving tradeoffs is limited.
- The method refines document embeddings only; query encoding remains standard, so the gains come from one-sided representation improvement rather than a fully deliberative dual encoder.
