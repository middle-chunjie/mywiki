## Summary

The paper proposes ConvSDG, a framework that uses [[large-language-model]]s to synthesize conversational search training data when manually labeled sessions are scarce. It supports two regimes: dialogue-level generation from topic descriptions with [[pseudo-relevance-feedback]] labels for unsupervised training, and query-level paraphrastic augmentation that reuses existing judgments for semi-supervised training. The generated sessions are then used to fine-tune an ANCE-based [[conversational-dense-retrieval]] model over reformulated multi-turn queries. Across CAsT-19/20/21 and TopiOCQA, ConvSDG improves over strong retrieval and rewriting baselines, and in several settings it even beats systems trained with manual relevance judgments. The main contribution is showing that synthetic [[session-data-generation]] can materially improve conversational retrieval with relatively modest generation cost.

## Problem & Motivation

[[conversational-search]] datasets are expensive to build and existing benchmarks contain limited sessions, sparse supervision, and strong long-tail effects. This makes both [[conversational-query-rewriting]] and [[conversational-dense-retrieval]] models hard to fine-tune reliably. The paper asks whether [[data-augmentation]] with LLM-generated sessions can replace or complement manual data, and whether diverse rewrites of the same turn can improve robustness without requiring many new human annotations.

## Method

- **Framework split**: ConvSDG has two generation modes. Dialogue-level generation produces an entire session from a topic description in one pass; query-level augmentation rewrites each existing turn `t` times while preserving intent.
- **Dialogue-level prompting**: the LLM receives `[Instruction, Topic Information]` and generates a full multi-turn conversation with both queries and hypothetical answers, which the authors found more coherent than turn-by-turn generation.
- **Pseudo supervision**: when relevance judgments are unavailable, the method runs off-the-shelf retrieval and samples `3` pseudo-positive passages uniformly from the top-`5` results for each generated turn.
- **Query form selection for PRF**: the paper compares `q + a`, `q + a + topic`, `convq + topic`, and `convq + conva + topic`, and chooses `q + a + topic` as the best formulation for pseudo-label generation.
- **Query-level augmentation**: when judgments exist, the LLM rewrites each input query under the instruction "transform one query into another query with same meaning", and the original turn-level labels are copied to the generated paraphrases for semi-supervised training.
- **Retriever formulation**: conversational dense retrieval reformulates each turn as ``q_n^ref = q_1 ∘ ... ∘ q_{n-1} ∘ q_n`` and scores a passage with ``S(q_n^ref, p) = F_Q(q_n^ref)^T · F_P(p)`` using ANCE encoders.
- **Training objective**: only the query encoder is updated while the passage encoder is frozen, optimized with a contrastive objective ``L = exp(S(q, p+)) / (exp(S(q, p+)) + Σ_{p- in C-} exp(S(q, p-)))``.
- **Implementation details**: generation uses ChatGPT `gpt-turbo-3.5-4k` with default hyperparameters; ANCE uses max lengths `64` for queries, `384` for passages, and `512` for concatenated sessions, batch size `16`, and `5` training epochs.
- **Inference**: dense retrieval uses [[approximate-nearest-neighbor-search]] over a Faiss index after conversational retriever fine-tuning.

## Key Results

- **Dialogue-level augmentation without relevance judgments**: ConvSDG reaches `MRR 59.5`, `NDCG@3 32.1`, `Recall@100 33.5` on [[cast-19]]; `MRR 37.9`, `NDCG@3 25.3`, `Recall@100 34.9` on [[cast-20]]; and `MRR 50.2`, `NDCG@3 33.2`, `Recall@100 37.5` on [[cast-21]].
- **Hardest benchmark gains**: on CAsT-21 in the unsupervised setting, the paper reports relative improvements of `19.2%` in MRR and `17.7%` in NDCG@3 over the second-best method.
- **TopiOCQA performance**: dialogue-level ConvSDG achieves `MRR 21.4`, `NDCG@3 19.9`, `Recall@10 37.8`, and `Recall@100 58.0` on [[topiocqa]], outperforming BM25 and zero-shot ConvDR by large margins.
- **Query-level augmentation with relevance judgments**: on CAsT-21, ConvSDG obtains `MRR 47.2`, `NDCG@3 30.8`, and `Recall@100 36.8`, corresponding to relative gains of `25.5%`, `22.0%`, and `17.2%` over the second-best method in that setting.
- **Efficiency against prior generation**: compared with CONVERSER's `230k` generated turns, ConvSDG uses only `1,704` turns in the dialogue-level CAsT setting and still produces stronger results.

## Limitations

- Dialogue-level generation depends on LLM coherence; if the synthetic sessions drift off-topic or mishandle conversational phenomena, the retriever can be trained on unrealistic trajectories.
- The unsupervised setting relies on [[pseudo-relevance-feedback]], which can introduce false positive labels; the paper explicitly notes that better pseudo-label construction remains open.
- Query-level augmentation assumes that rewrites preserve search intent and label validity, but the authors observe distribution shift and inconsistent gains until enough generated data are added.
- The study uses a single generator family (ChatGPT 3.5) and a single dense retriever backbone ([[ance]]), so generalization to stronger LLMs, different retrievers, or cost-quality tradeoffs is not fully characterized.
