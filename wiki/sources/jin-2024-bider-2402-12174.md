## Summary

BIDER studies a central failure mode of retrieval-augmented LLMs: retrieved passages are often long, noisy, and misaligned with the specific evidence a generator actually needs. The paper defines this missing target as Key Supporting Evidence (KSE) and proposes a three-stage training pipeline: synthesize oracle KSE from retrieved documents, distill a seq2seq refiner that maps retrieval results to KSE, and then align the refiner with downstream LLM preferences using reinforcement learning. Using BART-Large as the refiner and LLaMA2-7B as the generator, BIDER improves average task performance from `0.363` to `0.390` across five knowledge-intensive benchmarks while reducing generator input from `759` to `90` tokens on average. The main contribution is to make evidence quality, compactness, and ordering explicit optimization targets inside RAG.

## Problem & Motivation

Standard RAG pipelines feed top retrieved documents directly into the generator, but those documents can contain redundant or distracting text and may still miss the evidence structure most useful for answering. The paper frames this as knowledge inconsistency between retrieved text and the knowledge actually required by the LLM. Prior refinement methods often depend too heavily on unstable generator feedback, sentence ranking, summarization, or perplexity heuristics, which can discard crucial facts while keeping noise. BIDER is motivated by the need to construct a better training target than raw retrieval output, then learn a refiner that can present compact evidence in a form the downstream LLM can use more effectively.

## Method

- **Problem setup**: given question `q`, gold answer `o`, retrieved documents `D_q = {d_i}_{i=1}^K`, and fixed retriever `R` plus generator `G`, BIDER learns a mapping `F(D_q)` that makes generator outputs stronger than naive `q + D_q` prompting.
- **Stage 1, nugget extraction**: form a fact query `f = concat(q, o)` and retrieve top sentence nuggets by semantic similarity, `S = TopK_{s in D_q}(sim(s, f))`, where `sim` is computed with the `E5` embedding model; the positive nugget set size is `K = 7`.
- **Stage 1, nugget refinement**: iteratively build candidate pool `P` with gain `kappa_i = sim(s_i, f) - (1 / |P|) * sum_{s_j in P} sim(s_i, s_j)`, balancing usefulness against redundancy. After each addition, a `T5-XXL` NLI model estimates support for answering the question.
- **Refinement stopping rule**: stop when support exceeds `lambda_max = 0.5` or marginal gain falls below `lambda_min = 0.01`, which is intended to avoid both over-selection and accumulation of redundant evidence.
- **Stage 1, nugget cleaning**: keep the first selected nugget, then score later nuggets by their effect on answer likelihood under the generator, `tau'_i = log(G(o | q + s_i) / G(o | q))`, normalize to `tau_i`, and drop nuggets with `tau_i < lambda_lm = 0.05`.
- **Supervised distillation**: treat refinement as a seq2seq task rather than pure ranking. The input is `q + D_q` with document separators; the target is the ordered KSE string from the synthesis stage. The refiner backbone is `BART-Large`, trained with cross-entropy loss.
- **Preference alignment**: optimize the refiner with clipped PPO so that generated evidence better matches downstream LLM information preferences. The objective includes clipped policy loss, value loss, and entropy bonus, with action space over output tokens.
- **Reward design**: the reward is delayed until sequence end, `R_t = 0` before `EOF`, then `F1(a_pred, o) - F1(a_ori, o)` at termination, where `a_pred` uses refined evidence and `a_ori` uses original retrieved evidence. This targets net answer improvement rather than text similarity alone.
- **Implementation details**: AdamW with learning rate `5e-5`, batch size `32`, PPO decoding settings `top_k = 10` and `top_p = 0.95`, HuggingFace Transformers plus `PFRL`, BM25 retrieval over the December 2018 Wikipedia dump, `SimLM` reranking over top `100` documents, and `LLaMA2-7B` as generator.

## Key Results

- Across five datasets, BIDER improves average score from `0.363` to `0.390` while reducing average generator input from `759` to `90` tokens, roughly an `88%` compression relative to the original prompt.
- On `NQ`, BIDER reaches `0.403` EM with `77` tokens versus `0.356` with `725` tokens for the original prompt.
- On `TriviaQA`, BIDER reaches `0.523` EM with `98` tokens versus `0.480` with `787` tokens.
- On `FEVER`, BIDER reaches `0.524` accuracy with `93` tokens versus `0.517` with `805` tokens; gains exist but are much smaller than on QA and dialogue.
- On `HotpotQA`, BIDER reaches `0.386` F1 with `113` tokens versus `0.376` with `770` tokens, indicating improvement but also showing the method is less dominant on complex multi-hop QA.
- On `Wizard of Wikipedia`, BIDER reaches `0.122` F1 with `69` tokens versus `0.086` with `710` tokens, the largest relative gain among the reported tasks.
- Ablations show both major components matter: removing preference alignment drops `NQ` from `0.403` to `0.373`, while removing knowledge synthesis drops it further to `0.340`; similar degradation appears on `TriviaQA` (`0.523` to `0.518` and `0.504`).
- Preference alignment increases gold-answer coverage and front-loads useful evidence: on `NQ`, `% Gold in Out.` rises from `48.1` to `51.1`, and average gold position improves from `1.33` to `1.19`.
- Under weaker retrieval quality, BIDER still helps substantially: with plain BM25 on `NQ`, performance rises from `0.257` to `0.325` while using only `80` tokens.
- End-to-end latency on `NQ` drops from `1.33s/query` without BIDER to `1.18s/query` with BIDER (`0.10s` refiner time, `1.08s` generator time), about a `10%` speedup.

## Limitations

- The paper reports weaker gains on more complex settings such as [[hotpotqa]], suggesting that evidence refinement alone does not fully solve multi-hop reasoning.
- Training is not fully generator-agnostic: the method requires separate optimization for each dataset and downstream generator, which limits portability.
- The full pipeline depends on supervision signals that are unavailable in many production settings, including gold answers or evidence for KSE synthesis and generator access for reward computation.
- Experiments use only a December 2018 Wikipedia corpus, so robustness to broader real-world sources and writing styles is not established.
- The method improves compactness and answer quality, but it does not address retriever failures that omit necessary evidence entirely.
