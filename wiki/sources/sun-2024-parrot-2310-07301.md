## Summary

Parrot targets the multi-turn weakness of open-source chat models by improving the quality of instruction-tuning data rather than only scaling response models. The paper trains a question-asking model, Parrot-Ask, on real user-ChatGPT logs so it can generate human-like follow-up questions with contextual references and topic shifts, then pairs those questions with `gpt-3.5-turbo` responses to build the `40K`-dialogue Parrot-40K corpus. A final Parrot-Chat model fine-tuned on this data outperforms other open-source `13B` chat models on Alpaca-Eval, MT-Bench, and the authors' longer-horizon MT-Bench++ benchmark, while using fewer supervised examples than strong baselines such as Vicuna and UltraLM. The core claim is that human-like multi-turn supervision, especially with longer sessions, is a primary bottleneck for robust instruction following.

## Problem & Motivation

The paper starts from an empirical gap: many open-source chat models remain competitive on short instruction-following benchmarks but degrade substantially as dialogue length grows, especially beyond roughly six turns. The authors argue that this is largely a data problem. Existing automatic pipelines either generate short and shallow conversations, produce repetitive or gratitude-heavy exchanges, or rely on scarce ShareGPT-style logs that underrepresent long, realistic user sessions. The motivation behind Parrot is therefore to model the user side of conversation directly, so the collected supervision better matches how humans actually ask follow-up questions, shift topics, and refer back to prior context.

## Method

- **Parrot paradigm**: first train a question generator `Parrot-Ask`, then let it converse iteratively with `gpt-3.5-turbo` to collect multi-turn supervision, and finally fine-tune a response model `Parrot-Chat` on the resulting corpus.
- **Ask-model objective**: for a dialogue `X = (X_q^1, X_a^1, ..., X_q^T, X_a^T)`, optimize only question tokens with `` `L_ask = Σ_i log p(x_i | X_{q,<i}, X_{a,<i}), x_i ∈ X_q` ``. The first user turn is treated as context and excluded from loss computation.
- **Parrot-Ask training setup**: initialize from `LLaMA2-13B`, train on `70K` ShareGPT sessions with `max_length = 4096`, `3` epochs, AdamW, initial `` `lr = 3e-5` ``, cosine decay, warmup over `0.1` epoch, `8 × A100-80G`, total batch size `32`, and gradient accumulation `8`.
- **Data collection**: build Parrot-40K in two halves. One `20K` subset extends short ShareGPT sessions with fewer than `3` turns; another `20K` subset starts from UltraChat opening questions to broaden topic coverage. Iteration stops at `10` turns or when `gpt-3.5-turbo` returns meaningless answers.
- **Chat-model objective**: train Parrot-Chat with the standard assistant loss, masking system and user tokens and optimizing only answer tokens via `` `L_assistant = Σ_i log p(x_i | X_{q,<i}, X_{a,<i}), x_i ∈ X_a` ``.
- **Evaluation pipeline**: compare against Baize, UltraLM, Vicuna, ChatGPT, and GPT-4 using Alpaca-Eval, MT-Bench, and a new `8`-turn MT-Bench++ benchmark formed by extending each MT-Bench dialogue with `6` manually written follow-ups.
- **Ablation design**: isolate the effects of data source quality and session length by training on Parrot-20K variants and by truncating Parrot-40K sessions to `1`, `3`, `5`, or full-turn versions.

## Key Results

- **Dataset quality**: Parrot-40K reaches `8.71` average turns, `3551` average tokens, `5.5` topics, and `11.6` contextual references per dialogue, exceeding Baize (`4.54` turns), UltraChat (`3.85`), and ShareGPT (`6.67`).
- **Human-likeness**: annotators judge `81.1%` of Parrot-generated questions as resembling real user questions, versus `36.8%` for iterative self-chatting.
- **Main benchmark wins among open-source 13B models**: Parrot-Chat scores `83.42` on Alpaca-Eval, `6.81` on MT-Bench, and `6.56` on MT-Bench++, beating Vicuna v1.5 (`80.74`, `6.57`, `6.39`) with only `40K` supervised dialogues.
- **Data-quality ablations**: Parrot-20K(S) improves over ShareGPT-20K from `80.12/6.47/6.18` to `81.69/6.70/6.26` on Alpaca-Eval / MT-Bench / MT-Bench++; Parrot-20K(U) improves over UltraChat-20K from `67.66/6.09/6.17` to `78.23/6.33/6.36`.
- **Long-turn robustness**: models trained on `1`, `3`, or `5` turns degrade more quickly on MT-Bench++, while full-session Parrot-Chat remains more stable after turn `5`, matching the paper's claim that long training dialogues matter.

## Limitations

- The method still depends on proprietary models: `gpt-3.5-turbo` is used to generate responses during data construction, and GPT-4 is used as an automatic judge for benchmark evaluation.
- Parrot-Ask does not generate the initial question; the first turn still comes from existing human or synthetic seed data, leaving fully autonomous session bootstrapping unresolved.
- The asking model is not controllable enough to enforce explicit constraints such as required intents or context-reference structures.
- Even with better long-turn behavior, Parrot-Chat remains materially below closed-source GPT-3.5-Turbo and GPT-4 on the reported benchmarks.
- The evidence is concentrated on `13B`-scale chat models and benchmark-style evaluation, so transfer to other model sizes or real deployment settings is not established.
