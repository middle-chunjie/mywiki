## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

The paper introduces RHGN, a knowledge-graph entity alignment model designed to explicitly handle heterogeneity across graphs instead of treating all neighbors and relations uniformly. Its core idea is to separate relation and entity semantics through a relation-gated convolution, propagate evidence across seed-aligned entities via cross-graph embedding exchange, and construct soft supervision for relation matching with a relation alignment module. On four OpenEA benchmarks, RHGN consistently beats prior triple-based, neighbor-based, and relation-enhanced baselines, with the largest gains on EN-DE and DBpedia-YAGO. The work is useful as a structured-graph alignment method that combines relation-aware message passing with cross-graph information propagation under limited supervision.

## Problem & Motivation

Entity alignment aims to recover 1-to-1 equivalent entities across different knowledge graphs, but prior embedding methods struggle with heterogeneity. The paper isolates two concrete failure modes: neighbor heterogeneity, where aligned entities have different local neighborhoods across graphs, and relation heterogeneity, where semantically similar relations are expressed by different labels. Earlier GNN-based methods also tend to mix entity and relation signals too directly, which can over-smooth entity representations and inject noise. RHGN is motivated by the need to keep relations informative without collapsing them into entity space, while still shortening the path for cross-graph semantic propagation.

## Method

- **Problem setup**: two KGs are defined as `G1 = (E1, R1, T1)` and `G2 = (E2, R2, T2)` with a seed alignment set `S' ⊂ E1 × E2`; the goal is to recover a 1-to-1 entity alignment.
- **Graph data preprocessing**: RHGN augments each triple set with inverse relations using `r_inv = W_inv r` and `T' = T ∪ {(t, r_inv, h) | (h, r, t) ∈ T}` to make directional evidence bidirectional.
- **Cross-graph construction**: aligned seed entities are exchanged to build cross graphs `G1_cross` and `G2_cross`, replacing aligned entities across graphs so information can propagate through shorter paths.
- **Relation-gated convolution (RGC)**: entity updates use `e_i^(k+1) = tanh(Σ_{j∈N(i)} W_e^k (e_j^k ⊗ sigmoid(r_ij^k)))`, where relations act as gates on neighbor messages instead of being directly merged into entity embeddings.
- **Relation updates**: relation embeddings are evolved by `r_ij^(k+1) = W_r^k r_ij^k`; RGC weights are shared across the two graphs at each layer to reduce semantic drift.
- **Cross-graph embedding exchange (CEE)**: at each layer, the original graph consumes cross-graph embeddings and vice versa, i.e. `E^(k+1) = RGC(E_cross^k, R^k, G^k, W^k)` and `E_cross^(k+1) = RGC(E^k, R_cross^k, G_cross^k, W^k)`.
- **Soft relation alignment (SRA)**: a relation label embedding is computed as `r' = concat[(1/|H_r|) Σ_{e_i∈H_r} e_i, (1/|T_r|) Σ_{e_j∈T_r} e_j]`, and soft labels are generated by `y_ij = I(cos(r_i', r_j') > γ)` with `γ = 0.5`.
- **Entity alignment loss**: a contrastive objective minimizes distances for positive pairs and enforces a margin `λ = 1.5` for negatives, with balance weight `α1 = 0.1` and `25` negative samples per aligned pair.
- **Relation alignment loss**: relation matching is cast as multi-label classification over `x_ij = cos(r_i, r_j)` and trained with a soft-margin loss; the total objective is `L = L1 + α2 L2` with `α2 = 10`.
- **Implementation details**: the model uses `4` RGC layers, hidden size `256`, batch size `256`, learning rate `0.001`, Adam optimization, Xavier initialization, early stopping patience `25`, max `1000` epochs, and `5`-fold cross-validation. Every `25` epochs it resamples `25` hard negatives with CSLS and `100` head/tail entities for soft relation labels.

## Key Results

- On OpenEA `EN_FR_V1`, RHGN reaches `Hits@1 = 0.500`, `Hits@5 = 0.739`, and `MRR = 0.603`, improving over the strongest baseline IMEA (`0.458 / 0.720 / 0.574`).
- On `EN_DE_V1`, RHGN gets `0.704 / 0.859 / 0.771`, outperforming IMEA's `0.639 / 0.827 / 0.724` by `+6.5` Hits@1 points and `+4.7` MRR points.
- On `D_W_V1`, RHGN reports `0.560 / 0.753 / 0.644`, exceeding IMEA's `0.527 / 0.753 / 0.626`; on `D_Y_V1`, it reaches `0.708 / 0.831 / 0.762` versus IMEA's `0.639 / 0.804 / 0.712`.
- In convolution ablations on `EN_FR_V1`, RGC (`0.500 / 0.739 / 0.603`) beats GCN (`0.391 / 0.612 / 0.488`), GAT (`0.362 / 0.577 / 0.457`), R-GCN (`0.468 / 0.708 / 0.572`), and CompGCN (`0.473 / 0.726 / 0.584`).
- The best depth is `4` layers; `1` layer performs poorly because cross-graph information cannot propagate far enough, while deeper models add noise.
- Error analysis shows RHGN performs better for entities with more neighbors on `EN_FR_V1`, with `Hits@1` rising from `0.445` for degree `1` to `0.601` for degree `> 9`.

## Limitations

- The model still processes the whole graph and therefore scales poorly to very large KGs; the paper explicitly notes that compute and time grow with graph size and suggests chunking methods such as Cluster-GCN.
- Relation reasoning is limited to single relations; multi-hop relation paths are not modeled directly, even though the authors argue such paths carry richer semantic alignment cues.
- The evaluation is restricted to four OpenEA structural benchmarks and excludes settings with attributes, names, and descriptions, so external validity to richer real-world KGs is only partially established.
- Soft relation labels are induced from entity embeddings, so relation supervision quality depends on the current entity space and may degrade when seed alignments or neighborhoods are weak.
