## Summary

GeAR extends dense retrieval with a lightweight generation pathway so that a single model can score whole documents and also surface query-relevant fine-grained content inside them. The architecture combines a BERT-based bi-encoder for global document-query similarity, a fusion encoder with cross-attention for token-level interaction, and a causal text decoder trained to generate answers or relevant sentences conditioned on the query-document pair. The paper also constructs a 5.8M-triple Relevant Information Retrieval dataset with LLM-assisted query synthesis from Wikipedia. Across QA-style retrieval and keyword-style retrieval settings, GeAR improves over retrained dense retrievers and stronger reranker baselines on local information retrieval, while preserving standard bi-encoder efficiency for global retrieval and offering more interpretable retrieval outputs.

## Problem & Motivation

Standard bi-encoder retrievers compress the document-query relationship into a single scalar similarity, which is efficient but hard to interpret and weak at exposing which local spans actually answer a query. This is especially limiting for long documents and tasks such as answer sentence selection, search highlighting, fine-grained citation, and retrieval-augmented generation, where users need both the right document and the right in-document evidence. GeAR is motivated by the question of whether a retriever can keep the global efficiency of dense retrieval while also learning query-conditioned local understanding, without falling back to expensive chunking or heavyweight cross-encoder reranking.

## Method

- **Task formulation**: the paper defines a global-local retrieval objective with `f(q, D) -> d` for document retrieval and `f(q, d) -> u` for fine-grained unit retrieval, where `u` is typically a sentence or answer-bearing span inside `d`.
- **Training data**: QAR uses `30M` samples from PAQ; RIR uses `5.8M` synthetic `(query, document, unit)` triples built from Wikipedia by selecting sentences of `8-20` tokens whose first word and subject are not pronouns, rewriting them into queries with `Llama-3.1-70B-Instruct`, then filtering with `BGE` at similarity `>= 0.5`.
- **Bi-encoder backbone**: GeAR initializes `E_d(.)` and `E_q(.)` from `bert-base-uncased` and uses mean pooling for document/query embeddings, preserving standard dense-retrieval scoring for global retrieval.
- **Fusion encoder**: a fusion encoder shares most parameters with the query encoder and inserts lightweight cross-attention modules so document embeddings from `E_d(.)` can interact with query embeddings at every layer.
- **Text decoder**: a `110M`-parameter causal decoder, triggered by a special `[Decode]` token, consumes fusion embeddings and autoregressively generates query-conditioned fine-grained information; in QAR the generation target is the answer, while in RIR it is the relevant sentence.
- **Training objective**: GeAR optimizes `L_GeAR = L_CL + alpha * L_LM`, where `L_CL` is contrastive learning for global query-document alignment and `L_LM` is token-level cross-entropy for generation; the reported setting uses `alpha = 0.25`.
- **Supervision design**: following MoCo/BLIP-style training, the model adds a momentum bi-encoder with momentum coefficient `0.995`, queue size `57600`, and soft-label weight linearly ramped from `0` to `0.4` in the first `2` epochs.
- **Optimization details**: training runs for `10` epochs on `16 x AMD MI200 64GB` GPUs with max sequence length `512`, batch size `48` for QAR and `16` for RIR, `AdamW`, weight decay `0.05`, warmup `1000` steps, learning rate `1e-6 -> 1e-5 -> 1e-6` under a cosine schedule.
- **Inference modes**: global retrieval uses only the bi-encoder with no extra cost versus a classic bi-encoder; local retrieval ranks sentences using fusion-encoder cross-attention weights; generation activates the decoder only when textual explanations or answers are requested.

## Key Results

- **Global retrieval**: on SQuAD / NQ / TriviaQA / PAQ / RIR, GeAR reaches `R@5/M@5 = 0.887/0.766`, `0.762/0.574`, `0.664/0.400`, `0.952/0.872`, and `0.964/0.910`, and the paper reports a `3.5%` relative improvement in average Recall@5 over retrained `BGERT`.
- **Local retrieval**: on SQuAD / NQ / TriviaQA / PAQ / RIR, GeAR reaches `R@1/M@1 = 0.814/0.878`, `0.761/0.865`, `0.510/0.797`, `0.884/0.965`, and `R@3/M@3 = 0.933/0.897`, outperforming `BGE-Reranker-L` by an average relative `12.9%`.
- **Generation contribution matters**: removing `L_LM` drops local retrieval substantially, e.g. NQ `R@1 0.761 -> 0.582`, PAQ `0.884 -> 0.649`, and RIR `R@3 0.933 -> 0.891`, while average local recall rises from `0.663` at `alpha = 0` to `0.781` at `alpha = 0.25`.
- **Generation quality**: GeAR obtains PAQ `EM/F1 = 87.5/91.9` and RIR `Rouge-1/L = 87.6/87.3`; it is competitive on in-domain generation but still trails `Llama 3.3 70B` on several QA benchmarks.
- **Efficiency**: for local retrieval, inference cost is about `1.28x` BGE on CPU and `1.65x` BGE on a single `H20`, still far below `BGE Reranker-large`, which requires `8 x H20` and `8.38x` BGE time.

## Limitations

- The synthetic RIR training data is large but still less comprehensive than mature retrieval corpora, so robustness in broader and more semantically diverse retrieval settings remains uncertain.
- GeAR is capped at `512` input tokens, matching typical retrieval chunks but limiting direct use on longer documents unless combined with later long-context retrieval extensions.
- The decoder is intentionally lightweight (`110M` parameters) and optimized as an auxiliary module rather than a general-purpose generator, so it does not match stronger decoder-only LLMs on harder generation tasks.
- Local retrieval is not free: although much cheaper than cross-encoder reranking, the fusion encoder still adds a moderate constant-factor inference overhead over a same-size bi-encoder.
