## Summary

This paper proposes Parametric RAG (P-RAG), a retrieval-augmented generation framework that injects retrieved document knowledge into a language model's parameters instead of only concatenating passages into the prompt. Each corpus document is offline converted into a lightweight LoRA-style parameter block attached to feed-forward network layers after document rewriting and QA augmentation. At inference time, the system follows a Retrieve-Update-Generate workflow: retrieve top documents, merge their low-rank updates, and answer with the temporarily updated model. Across 2WikiMultihopQA, HotpotQA, PopQA, and ComplexWebQuestions, P-RAG consistently improves over strong in-context RAG baselines, especially on larger models, while reducing online latency by 29%-36% relative to standard single-round RAG in the reported LLaMA-3-8B setup.

## Problem & Motivation

The paper starts from a limitation of conventional RAG: retrieved passages are injected only through the input context, so longer prompts increase inference cost and often degrade knowledge utilization in complex reasoning tasks. The authors argue that this mismatch is structural because LLMs primarily store internal knowledge in model parameters, whereas in-context RAG only changes attention-time activations. Their goal is therefore to design a retrieval pipeline that preserves the flexibility of external knowledge access while moving retrieved information closer to the model's parametric memory, without requiring full supervised fine-tuning for every query.

## Method

- **Overall pipeline**: Parametric RAG builds an offline parametric corpus `K_P = {p_i | p_i = f_phi(d_i)}` and uses an online Retrieve-Update-Generate workflow instead of prompt-only knowledge injection.
- **Document augmentation**: each document is rewritten once and paired with `m = 3` generated QA pairs, forming an augmented resource `D_i = {(d_i^k, q_i^j, a_i^j)}` that exposes the same facts through multiple phrasings and explicit questions.
- **Parametric document encoding**: each document gets a document-specific LoRA update on feed-forward weights, with `W' = W + A B^T`, `A in R^(h x r)`, `B in R^(k x r)`, and `r << min(h, k)`. Only the low-rank parameters are trainable; the base model remains frozen.
- **Training objective**: for each augmented triplet, the model optimizes next-token loss on the concatenated sequence `x = [d_i^k ⊕ q_i^j ⊕ a_i^j]`, minimizing `sum -log P_(theta + Delta theta)(x_t | x_<t)` so the document facts are internalized into `Delta theta = {A, B}`.
- **Update step at inference**: after retrieving top-`k = 3` documents, the method merges their adapters with `Delta W_merge = alpha * sum_(j=1)^k A_j B_j^T`, then applies `W' = W + Delta W_merge` to the feed-forward layers before generation.
- **Implementation details**: LoRA is inserted only into FFN matrices, not QKV projections; learning rate is `3e-4`, epochs `= 1`, scaling factor `alpha = 32`, rank `r = 2`, dropout `= 0`, and decoding is greedy.
- **Retriever and corpus**: the system uses BM25 implemented with Elasticsearch over the DPR-style Wikipedia corpus; experiments use Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct, LLaMA-3.2-1B-Instruct, and LLaMA-3-8B-Instruct.
- **Efficiency claim**: offline preprocessing costs about the equivalent of decoding `12|d|` tokens per document, while online inference avoids the `t|d|` prompt expansion term that standard in-context RAG pays for retrieved documents.

## Key Results

- On **LLaMA-3-8B**, P-RAG improves total F1 over Standard RAG from `0.3372` to `0.3932` on 2WikiMultihopQA, from `0.2277` to `0.3563` on HotpotQA, from `0.1613` to `0.2413` on PopQA, and from `0.3545` to `0.4541` on ComplexWebQuestions.
- The combined setting is stronger still: on **LLaMA-3-8B**, Combine Both reaches `0.4258` on 2WikiMultihopQA, `0.4559` on HotpotQA, `0.3059` on PopQA, and `0.4728` on ComplexWebQuestions.
- P-RAG also shows large gains on **Qwen-1.5B**, e.g. total 2WikiMultihopQA F1 rises from `0.2431` to `0.3025`, HotpotQA from `0.2073` to `0.2165`, PopQA from `0.0999` to `0.1885`, and CWQ from `0.2823` to `0.3280`.
- Warm-up LoRA initialization improves over random initialization; for **LLaMA-3-8B**, HotpotQA rises from `0.3563` to `0.4499`, PopQA from `0.2413` to `0.2952`, and CWQ from `0.4541` to `0.5591`.
- Runtime analysis on **LLaMA-3-8B** shows P-RAG answering 2Wiki questions in `2.34s + 0.32s` versus `3.03s` for Standard RAG (`1.29x` speedup), and CWQ questions in `2.07s + 0.32s` versus `2.82s` (`1.36x` speedup).
- Storage overhead is non-trivial: for LLaMA-3-8B with `32` layers, hidden size `4096`, FFN size `14336`, and `r = 2`, each document requires about `2.36M` extra parameters or roughly `4.72 MB` at 16-bit precision.

## Limitations

- The approach shifts cost offline rather than removing it: document parameterization is expensive, roughly equivalent to decoding `12|d|` tokens per document during preprocessing.
- Parametric representations are model-specific; the authors explicitly note poor portability across different base LLM families.
- Storage remains much larger than raw text, reaching roughly `4.72 MB` per document in the LLaMA-3-8B configuration.
- Some benefits depend on engineering quality: the current implementation adds `0.32s` LoRA merge/load latency even though the theoretical FLOP overhead is below `1%`.
- P-RAG is not uniformly best on every subtask; for example, on LLaMA-3-8B 2Wiki inference questions, FLARE reports `0.3086` versus P-RAG's `0.2471`.
- The paper is an under-review preprint and explores only LoRA-based FFN injection, leaving other parameter-efficient mechanisms and more universal parameterizations to future work.
