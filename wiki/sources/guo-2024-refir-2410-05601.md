## Summary

ReFIR proposes a training-free retrieval-augmented framework for diffusion-based large restoration models (LRMs) to reduce hallucinated textures under severe image degradation. The paper first probes SUPIR and argues that ControlNet mainly reconstructs global structure while the UNet decoder restores high-frequency details, motivating texture transfer only in the decoder. ReFIR retrieves semantically similar high-quality images by nearest-neighbor search in an embedding space, then injects external knowledge through separate attention, spatial adaptive gating, and AdaIN-based distribution alignment between target and reference denoising chains. Applied to SeeSR and SUPIR without finetuning, ReFIR improves both fidelity and perceptual metrics on CUFED5, WR-SR, and RealPhoto60, while keeping parameter count unchanged at the cost of higher memory and latency.

## Problem & Motivation

Large diffusion-based restoration models can synthesize realistic images, but under heavy degradation they may hallucinate scene details that are inconsistent with the original content because their restoration behavior is bounded by internal knowledge stored in frozen model weights. The paper argues that simply scaling parameters or training data is expensive, and instead adapts the retrieval-augmented generation idea to image restoration: retrieve content-relevant high-quality images as external knowledge, then design an interaction mechanism that lets the low-quality denoising chain use helpful textures without corrupting spatial layout.

## Method

- **Mechanistic observation**: probing SUPIR suggests ControlNet mainly performs denoising structure reconstruction, while the UNet decoder restores high-frequency textures; ReFIR therefore injects retrieved knowledge only into decoder self-attention.
- **Retriever**: define a fixed high-quality database `D` and a non-parametric retriever `R` with `R: I_LQ, D -> I_R`, where `|I_R| = k`; nearest neighbors are found by cosine similarity over compact features from models such as `VGG16`, `ResNet`, or `CLIP`.
- **Separate attention**: build a target chain `C_T` for the low-quality image and a source chain `C_S` for the retrieved reference; compute `O_intra = Attention(Q_T, K_T, V_T)` and `O_inter = Attention(Q_T, K_S, V_S)` instead of concatenating keys/values, to avoid domain preference toward `C_T`.
- **Spatial adaptive gating**: flatten target and source latents `h_T, h_S in R^(C x HW)`, compute pixel-wise cosine similarities `sim in R^(HW x HW)`, sum each row, then min-max normalize to obtain a mask `M in [0, 1]` for pixel-level fusion.
- **Fusion rule**: combine internal and retrieved knowledge as `O_fuse = (1 - sM) ⊗ O_intra + sM ⊗ O_inter`, where `s` is a user-controlled scalar and `⊗` is element-wise multiplication.
- **Distribution alignment**: correct domain shift between target and reference chains via `O'_fuse = AdaIN(O_fuse, O_intra)`, matching the fused output's mean and variance to the original target-chain attention output.
- **Implementation details**: the paper uses one reference image by default, bicubic upsampling before the LRM, reflective padding to match chain sizes, fixed random seeds, and evaluates both SeeSR and SUPIR under their original hyperparameter settings.
- **Appendix extension**: for multiple references, replace scalar `s` with a normalized weight vector `s = {s_1, ..., s_k}` with `sum_n s_n = 1`, derived from cosine similarities between `I_LQ` and each retrieved image.

## Key Results

- On CUFED5, `SeeSR + ReFIR` improves over `SeeSR` from `19.94` to `20.32` PSNR and from `142.92` to `134.62` FID; on WR-SR it improves PSNR from `21.73` to `21.86` and FID from `65.78` to `61.22`.
- On CUFED5, `SUPIR + ReFIR` improves over `SUPIR` from `18.97` to `19.00` PSNR, from `4.5624` to `4.2085` NIQE, and from `168.26` to `148.69` FID; on WR-SR FID drops from `75.85` to `71.82`.
- On RealPhoto60, `SeeSR + ReFIR` improves NIQE from `4.7432` to `4.4566`, MUSIQ from `55.54` to `57.13`, and CLIPIQA from `0.6575` to `0.6732`; `SUPIR + ReFIR` improves NIQE from `3.5076` to `3.4593` and MUSIQ from `59.84` to `60.49`.
- Ablations on CUFED5 show all three components matter: removing spatial adaptive gating worsens NIQE from `4.2085` to `4.3893`, and removing distribution alignment worsens FID from `148.69` to `153.05`.
- Decoder-only injection works best: FID is `148.69` for decoder-only injection versus `168.26` with no injection and `168.26` for encoder-only injection.
- Parameter counts stay unchanged at `2.04B` for SeeSR and `3.87B` for SUPIR, but GPU memory rises from `24.4G` to `40.9G` for SeeSR and from `37.3G` to `51.4G` for SUPIR; latency rises from `76.5s` to `170.7s` and from `146.4s` to `322.8s` on `2048 x 2048` inputs with an `80G NVIDIA A100`.

## Limitations

- Performance depends strongly on retrieval quality; the paper shows random high-quality references cause severe degradation, reaching `223.47` FID in one ablation.
- The retriever is intentionally simple and semantic, relying on cosine similarity over pretrained image features rather than a restoration-specific retrieval model.
- ReFIR is training-free but not free computationally: it keeps parameters fixed while increasing memory and inference time because two denoising chains interact.
- Evaluation focuses mainly on real-world super-resolution and a limited set of LRMs, so generalization to broader restoration tasks is not yet established.
