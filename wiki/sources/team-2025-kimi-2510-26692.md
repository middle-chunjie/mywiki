## Summary

Kimi Linear proposes a hybrid attention architecture for large language models that combines Kimi Delta Attention (KDA), a fine-grained gated delta-rule linear attention, with periodic full Multi-Head Latent Attention (MLA) layers. The paper argues that long-horizon agentic inference, reinforcement-learning scaling, and million-token decoding make quadratic attention and linearly growing KV caches a practical bottleneck. KDA introduces channel-wise forget gates, specialized DPLR-style transitions, and a hardware-aware chunkwise algorithm so the model can preserve more expressivity than prior linear attention while remaining efficient. In matched 1.4T-token comparisons, the 48B-total / 3B-active Kimi Linear model outperforms full MLA on short-context, long-context, and RL-style evaluations, while cutting KV-cache usage by up to `75%` and improving 1M-context decoding throughput by up to `6.3x`.

## Problem & Motivation

Modern LLM deployments increasingly emphasize long-context decoding, tool-use trajectories, and reinforcement-learning-based test-time scaling, all of which amplify the inference cost of standard full attention. The paper targets the gap between efficient linear attention and high-quality softmax attention: prior linear variants reduce asymptotic cost but usually lose retrieval fidelity and short-context quality, while full attention retains quality at the expense of quadratic compute and a growing KV cache. The goal is therefore to build a hybrid architecture that preserves the quality of full attention while materially improving memory efficiency and long-context throughput.

## Method

- **KDA update rule**: replace scalar gating in Gated DeltaNet with channel-wise decay, using `S_t = (I - β_t k_t k_t^T) Diag(α_t) S_{t-1} + β_t k_t v_t^T` and `o_t = S_t^T q_t`, so each feature dimension has its own forgetting rate.
- **Chunkwise parallelization**: derive a hardware-efficient formulation with WY-style packing and UT transform, enabling inter-chunk recurrence plus intra-chunk parallelism; the FLOPs analysis uses fixed chunk size `C = 64`.
- **Neural parameterization**: compute `q_t`, `k_t`, and `v_t` with `ShortConv -> Swish`, then apply `L2Norm` to `q_t` and `k_t`; use `d_k = d_v = 128` for all experiments.
- **Gating and output**: parameterize the forget gate `α_t` and output gate with low-rank projections, keep the delta learning rate as `β_t = sigmoid(W_β x_t)`, and apply head-wise `RMSNorm` before the output projection.
- **Hybrid backbone**: interleave KDA and full `[[multi-head-latent-attention]]` layers in a uniform `3:1` ratio, choosing layerwise hybridization instead of headwise mixing for training stability and simpler infrastructure.
- **Positional design**: use `[[no-position-encoding]]` in MLA layers so KDA carries the main positional and recency bias, instead of combining RoPE with the hybrid stack.
- **Model scale**: the main matched comparison uses a MoE model with `48B` total parameters, `3B` activated parameters, `8 / 256` experts active per forward pass, and a dense first layer.
- **Training and post-training**: pretrain matched baselines on `1.4T` tokens; compare against MLA and GDN-H under identical SFT and RL settings, including RLVR-style math training and PTX stabilization.

## Key Results

- On 1.4T pretraining evaluation, Kimi Linear beats MLA / GDN-H on major short-context benchmarks: `HellaSwag 82.9`, `MMLU 73.8`, `MMLU-Pro 51.0`, `TriviaQA 71.7`, `GSM8K 83.9`, `CEval 79.5`, `CMMLU 80.8`.
- After identical SFT, Kimi Linear remains strongest overall, reaching `MMLU 77.0`, `MMLU-Pro 67.4`, `GPQA-Diamond 62.1`, `AIME 2025 21.3`, `PolyMath-en 43.6`, and `LiveCodeBench v6 26.0`.
- On long-context benchmarks at `128k`, Kimi Linear attains the best average score `54.5`, ahead of MLA `52.2` and GDN-H `51.2`, with standout wins on `RULER 84.3`, `MRCR 29.6`, `HELMET-ICL 90.0`, `RepoQA 68.5`, and `Long Code Arena (Lib) 37.1`.
- Efficiency gains are large at long context: up to `75%` less KV-cache usage, `3.98x` acceleration at `128k` RULER settings, and `1M`-context TPOT of `1.84 ms` versus `11.48 ms` for MLA (`6.3x` faster under larger-batch memory reallocation assumptions).
- Scaling-law analysis reports roughly `1.16x` compute efficiency over matched MLA baselines, and the extended `5.7T` run reaches `RULER@1M = 94.8` for Kimi-Linear-Instruct.

## Limitations

- The architecture is still hybrid rather than purely linear: the paper explicitly keeps periodic full MLA layers because pure linear attention remains weak on exact long-context retrieval and copying.
- Some benchmarks remain mixed even when the overall average improves; for example, Kimi Linear trails MLA on `LongBench V2` (`35.0` vs. `36.1`) and `Frames` (`58.8` vs. `60.5`), and does not lead `EvalPlus`.
- The work is a technical report / preprint with substantial in-house infrastructure, internal evaluation tooling, and proprietary training corpora, so independent reproduction at the reported scale is still difficult.
- Many claims about RL convergence and deployment efficiency are demonstrated under the authors' own stack and kernels, so external systems results may depend on implementation quality.
