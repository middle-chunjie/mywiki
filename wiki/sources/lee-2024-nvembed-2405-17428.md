## Summary

NV-Embed turns a decoder-only LLM into a generalist embedding model by combining bidirectional attention during contrastive training, a latent-attention pooling layer, two-stage instruction tuning, and transparent public-data curation. Starting from Mistral-7B, the model first trains on retrieval tasks with in-batch negatives and curated hard negatives, then mixes retrieval with classification, clustering, and semantic similarity tasks without in-batch negatives. The latent layer attends from token states to a trainable latent dictionary before mean pooling, aiming to avoid the information dilution of simple averaging and the recency bias of EOS pooling. NV-Embed-v1 reached the top of MTEB in May 2024, and the improved NV-Embed-v2 reclaimed the top spot in August 2024 with stronger retrieval, clustering, and out-of-domain long-document retrieval performance.

## Problem & Motivation

Decoder-only embedding models were starting to outperform bidirectional encoders, but they still inherited two architectural mismatches from autoregressive pretraining: causal masking suppresses full-sequence representation learning, and EOS-only pooling overweights the last token. The paper targets a more reproducible alternative to proprietary-data embedding systems by asking whether a public-data recipe on top of [[mistral-7b]] can produce a single embedding model that works well for retrieval, clustering, classification, and semantic similarity. A second motivation is practical: retrieval needs in-batch negatives, while many non-retrieval tasks do not, so a single training regime may underperform unless task differences are handled explicitly.

## Method

- **Backbone conversion**: initialize from [[mistral-7b]] and replace the decoder-only causal mask with bidirectional attention during contrastive training so every token can attend to the full sequence.
- **Latent-attention pooling**: after the decoder, apply a trainable latent dictionary with `Q in R^(l x d)`, `K = V in R^(r x d)`, and `O = softmax(QK^T)V`; the paper uses `r = 512`, latent width `d = 4096`, `8` attention heads, then an MLP with GELU and final mean pooling.
- **Instruction format**: retrieval-style queries are rewritten as `` `Instruct:{task_definition} Query:q+` ``, while instruction tokens are masked out of the pooled embedding; documents keep no instruction prefix.
- **Two-stage training**: stage 1 trains on retrieval datasets with in-batch negatives plus curated hard negatives; stage 2 blends retrieval and non-retrieval tasks and disables in-batch negatives to avoid false negatives in classification and clustering batches.
- **Hard-negative mining**: positives and negatives are curated with a positive-aware recipe using [[e5-mistral-7b-instruct]] as teacher and threshold `` `max_negative_score_threshold = pos_score * 0.95` ``.
- **Optimization**: end-to-end LoRA fine-tuning with rank `16`, alpha `32`, dropout `0.1`, Adam, warm-up `50` steps, linear decay, learning rate `2e-5` in stage 1 and `1.5e-5` in stage 2.
- **Batching and sequence limits**: batch size `128`, each query paired with `1` positive and `7` hard negatives, BF16 training, maximum length `512`, and `<BOS>/<EOS>` added to both queries and documents.
- **Data recipe**: public retrieval datasets, public classification/clustering/STS datasets, positive-aware hard-negative mining, additional retrieval corpora, and `120,000` synthetic examples spanning `60,000` synthetic tasks generated with Mixtral-8x22B-Instruct-v0.1.
- **Compression appendix**: the paper also studies pruning from `8B` to `3.5B`, `INT8`/`FP8` quantization, and LoRA-based recovery after removing the latent-attention block.

## Key Results

- [[mteb]] average across `56` tasks: `69.32` for NV-Embed-v1 and `72.31` for NV-Embed-v2, with the latter ranked No. 1 on August 30, 2024.
- Category scores for NV-Embed-v2 on [[mteb]]: Retrieval `62.65`, Rerank `60.65`, Clustering `58.46`, Pair Classification `88.67`, Classification `90.37`, STS `84.31`, Summarization `30.70`.
- Two-stage training beats single-stage training without in-batch negatives on average (`72.31` vs. `71.94`) and especially on retrieval (`62.65` vs. `61.37`).
- Positive-aware hard-negative mining raises retrieval from `59.22` to `61.52`; adding extra retrieval data lifts it to `62.28`; adding synthetic data reaches `62.65`.
- Example-based labels for multi-class classification and clustering outperform label-text supervision, improving overall average from `70.82` to `72.31`.
- On [[air-benchmark]], NV-Embed-v2 reaches `52.28` average nDCG@10 on QA (second place) and `74.78` average Recall@10 on Long-Doc (first place).
- Pooling ablation shows latent attention improves retrieval over mean pooling in the v2 setup (`62.65` vs. `61.82`), while self-attention brings little benefit.

## Limitations

- The recipe is demonstrated on an English-centric benchmark stack; the paper does not establish comparable multilingual or cross-backbone transfer.
- Some training sources, such as MSMARCO, overlap with benchmark families used in [[mteb]], following common practice but weakening a strict zero-shot interpretation.
- The main system still depends on substantial data curation, hard-negative mining, and synthetic generation, so the overall recipe is more involved than the architecture alone.
- The strongest out-of-domain gains are reported on [[air-benchmark]], but the QA track still trails BGE-en-ICL (`52.28` vs. `52.93`).
- Compression results are appendix-only and require pruning plus LoRA recovery after removing the latent-attention block, so deployment trade-offs are not the paper's primary validated contribution.
