## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2022; re-verify against recent literature.

This paper scales instruction tuning along three axes: model size, task count, and chain-of-thought (CoT) supervision. The authors assemble a Flan mixture of `1,836` tasks from `473` datasets and `146` task categories, then finetune T5, PaLM, cont-PaLM, and U-PaLM models from `80M` to `540B` parameters. Across held-out benchmarks, larger models and broader instruction mixtures consistently improve zero-shot, few-shot, and reasoning performance, while adding only `9` CoT datasets is enough to preserve and improve held-out CoT ability. The headline result is Flan-PaLM 540B, which reaches `75.2%` on five-shot MMLU with CoT plus self-consistency and is preferred by human raters over PaLM on `79%` of challenging open-ended prompts.

## Problem & Motivation

Prior instruction-finetuning results showed gains on unseen NLP tasks, but it was unclear whether those gains would continue to scale with much larger model sizes and much larger task mixtures. The paper also identifies a mismatch between standard instruction tuning and reasoning evaluations: finetuning only on non-CoT instruction data can degrade chain-of-thought performance even when general task performance improves. The motivation is therefore twofold: build a single post-training recipe that improves usability and generalization across zero-shot, few-shot, and reasoning settings, and quantify whether instruction finetuning remains compute-efficient relative to expensive pretraining.

## Method

- **Data mixture**: instruction finetuning uses `1,836` tasks from `473` datasets and `146` task categories, combining four mixtures: Muffin (`80` tasks), T0-SF (`193`), NIV2 (`1,554`), and CoT (`9` datasets).
- **Prompt formats**: the training mixture includes zero-shot and few-shot exemplars, with and without CoT rationales; for the CoT subset, the authors manually write `~10` instruction templates per dataset.
- **Reasoning supervision**: the CoT mixture contains `74,730` examples over nine datasets, intended to preserve multi-step reasoning while keeping the same checkpoint usable for non-CoT tasks.
- **Model families**: the recipe is applied to encoder-decoder and decoder-only backbones, including T5 (`80M` to `11B`), PaLM (`8B`, `62B`, `540B`), cont-PaLM (`62B`), and U-PaLM (`540B`).
- **Optimization**: all models are finetuned with Adafactor and a constant learning rate; training uses sequence packing with end-of-sequence boundaries and attention masking across packed-example boundaries.
- **Checkpoint selection**: a single checkpoint per model is chosen using periodic held-out evaluation every `2k` to `10k` steps, then reused across all ablations for that model family.
- **Flan-PaLM 540B hyperparameters**: `batch size = 32`, `dropout = 0.1`, `learning rate = 1e-3`, `steps = 21k`; finetuning consumes about `0.2%` of pretraining compute, roughly `512` TPU v4 chips for `37` hours.
- **Mixture balancing**: task sampling is capped per mixture to avoid domination by very large datasets, with caps `30,000` (Muffin), `20,000` (T0-SF), `100,000` (CoT), and `5,000` (NIV2); later runs use updated mixture weights after ablations showed T0-SF was especially helpful.
- **Evaluation protocol**: the normalized average is the macro-average over six normalized scores: `MMLU-direct`, `MMLU-CoT`, `BBH-direct`, `BBH-CoT`, `TyDiQA-direct`, and `MGSM-CoT`.
- **Open-ended generation evaluation**: PaLM 540B and Flan-PaLM 540B generate `5` samples with `temperature = 0.7`; outputs are filtered by log-probability heuristics and judged by human raters on `190` prompts.

## Key Results

- Flan-PaLM 540B improves the held-out normalized average from `49.1` to `58.5`, a `+9.4` absolute gain over PaLM 540B.
- On five-shot MMLU, Flan-PaLM 540B reaches `72.2%` with direct prompting and `75.2%` with CoT plus self-consistency, versus `69.3%` for PaLM 540B.
- On one-shot TyDiQA, Flan-PaLM 540B improves from `52.9%` to `67.8%`, a `+14.9` absolute gain over PaLM 540B.
- On MGSM, Flan-PaLM 540B improves from `45.9%` to `57.0%` with CoT prompting and to `72.0%` with CoT plus self-consistency.
- Scaling task count from `0` to `1,836` raises the normalized average by `+15.5` for PaLM `8B`, `+10.4` for `62B`, and `+9.4` for `540B`.
- Human raters prefer Flan-PaLM over PaLM on `79%` of `190` open-ended prompts, with an additional `~10%` preference boost on prompts that include a CoT trigger.
- Finetuning remains cheap relative to pretraining: Flan-PaLM 540B uses about `0.2%` of pretraining compute while improving broad benchmark performance and zero-shot usability.

## Limitations

- Gains from scaling the instruction mixture show diminishing returns after roughly `282` tasks, so simply adding more tasks may not keep yielding large improvements.
- The CoT component is small (`9` datasets) and may bias reasoning behavior toward the particular rationale styles present in those datasets.
- The strongest model is still not state of the art on every specialized benchmark: the paper notes weaker-than-best results on BBH algorithmic tasks and TyDiQA relative to specialized finetuned systems.
- The study is centered on benchmark generalization and a limited human evaluation rather than long-term deployment behavior, robustness, or post-2022 alignment methods.
- Because this is a 2022 preprint in a fast-moving LLM area, later post-training methods, newer evaluation suites, and stronger base models may change the practical frontier.
