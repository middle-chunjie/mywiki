## Summary

This paper studies retrieval granularity as an inference-time design choice for dense retrieval and retrieval-augmented QA. Instead of indexing Wikipedia as passages or sentences, it introduces propositions: minimal, self-contained fact units generated by a distilled Propositionizer. Using FACTOIDWIKI, an English Wikipedia dump segmented into `41.4M` passages, `114.2M` sentences, and `256.9M` propositions, the authors evaluate SimCSE, Contriever, DPR, and GTR on Natural Questions, TriviaQA, WebQuestions, SQuAD, and EntityQuestions. Proposition indexing consistently improves passage recall, especially for unsupervised retrievers and long-tail entities, and also improves downstream FiD and LLaMA-2-7B QA under fixed token budgets. The core claim is that finer retrieval units increase question-relevant information density without retraining the retriever.

## Problem & Motivation

Dense retrievers are usually trained and deployed with a fixed corpus segmentation choice, but the paper argues that retrieval unit granularity is an under-studied inference-time variable. Passage indexing can bury the answer inside irrelevant context, while sentence indexing still leaves unresolved coreference and multi-fact clutter. The authors therefore ask whether a finer but still self-contained unit can improve both retrieval and downstream QA, especially under limited prompt budgets and on tasks the retriever was not explicitly trained for.

## Method

- **Retrieval units**: compare indexing Wikipedia by passages, sentences, and propositions, where a proposition is a minimal, stand-alone fact expression that cannot be further split without losing atomicity.
- **Proposition definition**: each proposition must encode one distinct meaning unit, be minimal, and include enough context to be interpretable independently.
- **FACTOIDWIKI construction**: segment a 2021-10-13 English Wikipedia dump into `41,393,528` passages (avg `58.5` words), `114,219,127` sentences (avg `21.0` words), and `256,885,003` propositions (avg `11.2` words).
- **Propositionizer**: train a passage-to-proposition generator with two-step distillation. First prompt `GPT-4` to create a seed set of `42k` passage-to-proposition pairs, then fine-tune `Flan-T5-large` on that seed set.
- **Quality analysis**: manually inspect 50 passages; proposition errors are rare but measurable. For GPT-4 generations, not-faithful / not-minimal / not-stand-alone rates are `0.7% / 2.9% / 4.9%`; for Propositionizer they are `1.3% / 2.0% / 3.1%`.
- **Passage retrieval evaluation**: for sentence- or proposition-level indexing, aggregate to passage scores with `score(q, p) = max_{u in p} sim(q, u)` after retrieving fine-grained units and mapping them back to unique source passages.
- **Models and tasks**: test four dual-encoder retrievers (`SimCSE`, `Contriever`, `DPR`, `GTR`) on five open-domain QA datasets; evaluate passage Recall@`k`, FiD exact match, and retrieval-augmented `LLaMA-2-7B` exact match under token budgets `l in {100, 500}`.

## Key Results

- **Average passage retrieval gains**: proposition indexing improves average Recall@20 over passage indexing by `+10.1` for unsupervised retrievers and `+2.7` for supervised retrievers.
- **Unsupervised retrievers benefit most**: SimCSE average Recall@20 rises from `49.1` to `61.7`; Contriever rises from `62.8` to `70.5`.
- **Supervised retrievers still improve on average**: DPR average Recall@20 rises from `69.5` to `72.2`; GTR rises from `76.6` to `79.2`, with especially strong gains on SQuAD and EntityQuestions.
- **Long-tail entity queries**: on EntityQuestions, Contriever Recall@20 improves from `56.3` to `70.1`, and DPR Recall@5 improves from `47.5` to `59.4`, indicating larger gains on rarer entities.
- **Reader-based QA**: with FiD on top-20 retrieved passages, average EM improves from `24.9` to `32.4` for SimCSE, `33.7` to `38.7` for Contriever, `36.6` to `38.8` for DPR, and `40.5` to `42.6` for GTR.
- **RAG with fixed token budgets**: using propositions instead of passages at `EM@500` improves averages by `+4.1` for SimCSE, `+3.2` for Contriever, `+2.7` for DPR, and `+2.8` for GTR with `LLaMA-2-7B`.

## Limitations

- The study only uses English Wikipedia, so the granularity findings may not transfer directly to multilingual or domain-specific corpora.
- The retriever comparison is centered on popular bi-encoder / dual-encoder models; late-interaction or generative retrievers are not evaluated.
- Proposition generation adds preprocessing cost and greatly increases index size, from `41.4M` passages to `256.9M` propositions.
- Generated propositions are high quality but not perfect: a small fraction are unfaithful, non-minimal, or not fully stand-alone.
- The paper studies fixed granularities rather than adaptive per-query segmentation or hybrid indexing strategies.
