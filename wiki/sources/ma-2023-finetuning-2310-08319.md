## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

The paper studies whether a modern large language model can improve the full multi-stage retrieval pipeline instead of only acting as a prompted reranker. It fine-tunes LLaMA-2 as both a dense bi-encoder retriever (`RepLLaMA`) and a pointwise reranker (`RankLLaMA`) on MS MARCO passage and document ranking, then evaluates zero-shot transfer on BEIR. The core design uses the final `</s>` token representation as the sequence embedding for retrieval and a linear head over the same token for reranking, with LoRA, FlashAttention, and gradient checkpointing to make 7B-scale contrastive training feasible. Across passage retrieval, document retrieval, reranking, and zero-shot transfer, the LLaMA-based pipeline consistently beats smaller baselines and shows that longer-context decoder-only LLMs can represent full documents without segmentation heuristics.

## Problem & Motivation

Prior multi-stage text retrieval systems benefited heavily from BERT- and T5-style pretrained models, but most retrievers and rerankers still used much smaller backbones than contemporary LLMs. Prompt-based LLM reranking methods had shown promise, yet they only addressed reranking, were slow because of multi-pass generation, and could not directly exploit supervised labels. The paper asks whether directly fine-tuning an open LLM for both retrieval and reranking can improve end-to-end effectiveness while preserving the efficiency advantages of dense retrieval and pointwise scoring. A second motivation is long-context handling: because LLaMA-2 is pretrained for longer sequences, the authors test whether whole-document representation can replace MaxP-style segmentation and pooling.

## Method

- **Pipeline**: the system is a two-stage retrieval stack with a dense retriever `RepLLaMA` followed by a pointwise reranker `RankLLaMA`, both initialized from `[[llama-2-7b]]`; a `13B` variant is also tested for reranking.
- **Retriever representation**: for an input sequence `t_1 ... t_k`, RepLLaMA appends `</s>` and uses the last hidden state of that token as the embedding, i.e. `V_T = Decoder('t_1 ... t_k </s>')[-1]`.
- **Retriever scoring and loss**: relevance is computed by inner product `Sim(Q, D) = <V_Q, V_D>`, and training uses InfoNCE over one positive plus hard negatives and in-batch negatives: `L = -log(exp(Sim(Q,D+)) / (exp(Sim(Q,D+)) + Σ exp(Sim(Q,D-))))`.
- **Retriever training details**: passage and document retrievers use `LLaMA-2-7B`, embedding dimension `4096`, batch size `128`, and exact flat indexes instead of ANN during evaluation; passage training samples `15` hard negatives per query, document training samples `7`.
- **Reranker formulation**: RankLLaMA concatenates query and document as `query: {Q} document: {D} </s>` and predicts a scalar score with a linear head over the final `</s>` representation: `Sim(Q, D) = Linear(Decoder(input)[-1])`.
- **Reranker negatives**: hard negatives are mined from top-ranked results of the paired retriever, so RankLLaMA is optimized for the actual upstream candidate distribution rather than generic BM25 outputs.
- **Efficiency techniques**: both models use `[[low-rank-adaptation]]`, `[[flash-attention]]`, and gradient checkpointing to make billion-parameter contrastive fine-tuning fit on `16 x 32GB` V100 GPUs.
- **Passage retrieval setup**: training uses roughly `500k` MS MARCO passage examples; hard negatives mix BM25 and CoCondenser results; inference retrieves top `1000` passages and reranks top `200`.
- **Document retrieval setup**: training uses roughly `300k` MS MARCO document examples; models consume the first `2048` tokens by default without segmentation, covering about `77%` of documents fully; inference retrieves top `1000` documents and reranks top `100`.
- **Index sizes and context handling**: pre-encoding yields a `135GB` flat index for passages and `49GB` for documents; a `4096`-token reranker improves effectiveness slightly over `2048`, but doubles document-reranker training time from about `4` to `8` days.

## Key Results

- On MS MARCO passage retrieval, `RepLLaMA` reaches `41.2` MRR@10, `99.4` Recall@1k, `74.3` nDCG@10 on DL19, and `72.1` on DL20, beating `bi-SimLM` (`39.1` MRR@10) and `GTR-XXL` (`38.8` MRR@10).
- On passage reranking, `RankLLaMA` reaches `44.9` MRR@10 and the `13B` version reaches `45.2`; the `7B` model also scores `75.6` nDCG@10 on DL19 and `77.4` on DL20, outperforming `RankT5` by `1.5` MRR@10.
- Compared with prompted LLM rerankers, the `RepLLaMA` plus `RankLLaMA` pipeline matches or exceeds `GPT-4` reranking on TREC passage ranking, with `+0.4` nDCG@10 on DL19 and `+7.3` on DL20, while allowing parallel pointwise scoring.
- On BEIR zero-shot transfer, `RepLLaMA` averages `55.1` nDCG@10 across `13` public datasets and `RankLLaMA-7B` improves this to `56.6`; `13B` is nearly identical at `56.5`.
- On MS MARCO document retrieval, `RepLLaMA` reaches `45.6` MRR@100 and `98.9` Recall@1k without segmentation, beating `CoCondenser-MaxP` (`42.5` MRR@100).
- On document reranking, `RankLLaMA` reaches `50.3` MRR@100, `67.7` nDCG@10 on DL19, and `67.4` on DL20, exceeding `MORES+` (`49.3` MRR@100).
- In the ablation, full fine-tuning overfits relative to LoRA: passage retriever training MRR@10 is `46.6` vs `40.8`, but dev MRR@10 is only `41.6` vs `41.2`, and LoRA is better on DL19 (`74.3` vs `72.8`) and DL20 (`72.1` vs `69.9`).
- Increasing document reranker length from `512` to `2048` improves MRR@100 from `48.5` to `50.3`; going to `4096` adds only `+0.3`, indicating diminishing returns.

## Limitations

- The work is evaluated as a `2023` preprint on LLaMA-2-era models; newer retrieval-specialized LLM backbones and later long-context architectures may change the ranking of design choices.
- Retrieval evaluation uses exact flat search rather than practical ANN methods such as `[[nearest-neighbor-search]]` with HNSW, so production latency and effectiveness tradeoffs are not measured directly.
- Supervised training relies mainly on MS MARCO passage and document labels; zero-shot BEIR transfer is promising, but the training distribution is still fairly narrow for broad retrieval claims.
- The paper does not combine the retriever with reranker distillation or KL-style soft-label training, which the authors explicitly note could further improve RepLLaMA.
- Long-context gains plateau: moving document reranking from `2048` to `4096` tokens gives only a small benefit while roughly doubling training time.
