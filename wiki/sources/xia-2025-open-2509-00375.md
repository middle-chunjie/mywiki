## Summary

The paper argues that verifiable deep-research QA should be modeled as a hierarchical reasoning problem rather than as standard single-hop or flat multi-hop retrieval, and introduces InfoSeek to synthesize such tasks automatically at scale. InfoSeek builds a research tree over entities and claims with a dual-agent Planner/Browser loop, converts the tree into natural-language questions, and filters the resulting data for difficulty and verifiability. On top of this dataset, the authors train InfoSeeker, a compact search agent that uses parallel multi-query search, a Refiner Agent, supervised fine-tuning from accepted trajectories, and GRPO-based reinforcement learning. The resulting 3B model is reported to beat several stronger open and closed baselines on BrowseComp-Plus while retaining strong general search QA performance.

## Problem & Motivation

Existing QA datasets such as Natural Questions and HotpotQA are too shallow to represent deep-research behavior, while more recent synthetic deep-research datasets are often closed, small, shortcut-prone, or weakly structured. The paper's motivation is to define a verifiable task class that requires hierarchical decomposition, multi-step evidence gathering, and integration of interdependent constraints, then use that formalization to synthesize open, scalable training data for search-capable LLM agents. The downstream goal is not only a better benchmark, but also a data pipeline that can train compact models to perform nontrivial deep-research workflows.

## Method

- **HCSP formalization**: the paper defines deep-research QA as a hierarchical constraint satisfaction problem with `H(x) = \bigcap_{i=1}^{k} S(c_i) \cap \bigcap_{j=1}^{m} H(y_j)`, unifying flat CSPs and multi-hop reasoning as special cases.
- **Research tree construction**: InfoSeek represents a problem as a tree of entities or facts, then grows it with four actions: root initialization, parent blurring with extra constraints, depth extension through dependency links, and question generation after structural checks.
- **Dual-agent synthesis loop**: a Planner chooses which vertex and action to apply, while a Browser reads webpages or Wikipedia, extracts hyperlinks or atomic claims, and records evidence traces for each expansion step.
- **Quality assurance**: difficulty filtering rejects questions that `Qwen2.5-32B-Inst` can answer directly, while verifiability filtering uses `Gemini 2.5 Flash` over gold pages plus distractors to remove ambiguous or unsolved questions.
- **InfoSeeker inference workflow**: the trained agent alternates explicit thinking with parallel query generation, wraps searches in `<search>...</search>`, and summarizes top-`k` retrievals through a Refiner Agent into compact `<information>...</information>` blocks before emitting the final `<answer>`.
- **SFT stage**: rejection sampling retains only successful executable trajectories from `Qwen2.5-72B` and a preview InfoSeeker model; round-1 SFT uses `2` epochs, `lr = 1e-5`, `weight decay = 0.01`, and context length `16384`.
- **RL stage**: the authors optimize with GRPO, where advantages are normalized as `A_{i,t} = (R_i - mean(R)) / std(R)`, using round-1 settings `batch size = 256`, `max turns = 10`, `rollout size = 5`, `temperature = 0.8`, top-`5` retrieval contents, and a binary reward `R in {0, 1}` for correct format plus answer.

## Key Results

- InfoSeek produces `52,138` synthesized samples at a reported curation cost of `$571.8`, with most problems concentrated in `4-6` reasoning vertices.
- The difficulty filter is strong enough that `Qwen2.5-32B-Inst` answers only `2%` of candidate questions directly; `Qwen2.5-72B` with CoT still fails on `92.7%` overall, rising from `88.1%` on 3-vertex problems to `94.1%` on 7+-vertex problems.
- On standard QA benchmarks, InfoSeeker-3B reaches an average of `43.5`, including `42.7` on NQ, `44.6` on HotpotQA, and `52.0` on 2WikiMultihopQA.
- On BrowseComp-Plus, InfoSeeker-3B reaches `16.5%` accuracy with `8.24` search calls, beating Gemini 2.5 Flash (`15.5%`), GPT-4.1 (`14.6%`), Qwen3-32B (`3.5%`), and Search-R1-32B (`3.9%`), while remaining below Gemini 2.5 Pro (`19.0%`) and far below GPT-5 (`55.9%`).
- Training on InfoSeek matters materially: the same backbone trained on `NQ+HQA` reaches only `3.0%` on BrowseComp-Plus versus `16.5%` when trained on InfoSeek.
- The trajectory pipeline yields `24K` valid SFT trajectories from the first sampling round and `3,450` higher-quality round-2 trajectories after additional filtering.

## Limitations

- The paper's formal construction can still fail through underdetermined or overdetermined trees, and Section 2.4 acknowledges both as inherent risks in tree-based HCSP synthesis.
- Verifiability and shortcut filtering depend on strong external LLM judges, so residual labeling errors or missed shortcuts may remain even after QA.
- The RL reward is intentionally simple, assigning `1` only when both format and extracted answer are correct, which leaves search efficiency, citation quality, and intermediate reasoning quality largely unoptimized.
- The evaluation focuses on verifiable QA-style deep-research tasks rather than longer-form report writing or broader open-ended research assistance, so the reported gains may not transfer directly to full long-form research agents.
