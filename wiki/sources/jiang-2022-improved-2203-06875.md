## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2022; re-verify against recent literature.

The paper introduces PromCSE, a universal sentence embedding framework that freezes a pretrained encoder, injects trainable soft prompts at every Transformer layer, and adds an energy-based hinge auxiliary loss when supervised hard negatives are available. The central argument is that full-model contrastive tuning over-parameterizes pretrained language models and hurts robustness under domain shift, while prompt-only adaptation better preserves general linguistic knowledge. The authors also reinterpret NT-Xent as a special case of energy-based learning, which motivates a margin-based objective over the hardest negative. On seven standard STS benchmarks, unsupervised PromCSE-BERTbase improves average Spearman from `76.25` to `78.49` over SimCSE, and on the out-of-domain CxC-STS benchmark it improves from `67.5` to `71.2`. Supervised PromCSE plus EH reaches `82.13` on BERTbase and `85.10` on RoBERTa-large.

## Problem & Motivation

Contrastive sentence embedding methods such as SimCSE achieve strong in-domain STS performance, but the authors argue that fully tuning large PLMs can overfit the training distribution and break under domain shift. They also argue that the standard NT-Xent objective is too soft in supervised settings because it does not explicitly enforce a margin between positives and the hardest contradiction-style negatives. The paper therefore targets two linked issues: robustness of universal sentence embeddings when the evaluation domain changes, and stronger pairwise discrimination when supervised hard negatives are available.

## Method

- **Prompt-only encoder adaptation**: freeze the PLM and prepend trainable soft prompts `P^j = {p_1^j, ..., p_k^j}` at every Transformer layer instead of tuning all model weights.
- **Layer-wise prompt formulation**: the hidden state follows Eq. (1), where `h_i^j = e_i^j` for input tokens at `j = 0`, `h_i^j = p_i^j` for prompt positions `i <= k`, and otherwise `h_i^j = Trans(h^{j-1})_i`.
- **Sentence representation**: add an MLP on top of the final `[CLS]` state during training; for unsupervised evaluation, discard the MLP and use the `[CLS]` output directly, following SimCSE.
- **Unsupervised contrastive loss**: construct positives by encoding the same sentence twice with different dropout masks and optimize `L_CL = -log exp(sim(h_i, h_i^+) / tau) / sum_j exp(sim(h_i, h_j^+) / tau)` with cosine similarity and `tau = 0.05`.
- **Supervised contrastive loss**: use NLI premise-entailment pairs as positives and contradiction hypotheses plus in-batch samples as negatives, yielding Eq. (8) over `h_i`, `h_i^+`, and `h_j^-`.
- **Energy-based view**: define `E(W, Y_i, X_i) = -sim(f(X_i), f(Y_i))`, showing NT-Xent is a special case of an energy-based negative log-likelihood with inverse temperature `beta = 1 / tau`.
- **Energy-based Hinge loss**: supplement supervised training with `L_EH = [m + sim(h_i, h_i_hat) - sim(h_i, h_i^+)]_+`, where the nearest in-batch or contradiction negative approximates the most offending incorrect answer.
- **Joint objective**: optimize `L = L_CL + lambda * L_EH` with `lambda = 10`; ablations choose margin `m = 0.2` as the best supervised setting.
- **Training setup**: unsupervised PromCSE trains on `1M` English Wikipedia sentences for `1` epoch; supervised PromCSE trains on `MNLI + SNLI` for `10` epochs with seed `42`, max length `32`, Adam plus linear decay, and evaluation every `125` steps on STS-B dev.
- **Main hyperparameters**: unsupervised BERTbase uses `batch = 256`, `lr = 3e-2`, `prompt length = 16`; supervised BERTbase uses `256`, `1e-2`, `12`; RoBERTabase uses `512`, `1e-2`, `10`; RoBERTa-large uses `512`, `5e-3`, `10`. Experiments run on `4 x NVIDIA 3090 Ti`.
- **Prompt design findings**: multi-layer prompts work much better than input-layer-only prompts, and performance saturates when prompt length reaches roughly `k = 12`.

## Key Results

- **Standard STS, unsupervised**: PromCSE-BERTbase reaches average Spearman `78.49`, improving over SimCSE-BERTbase `76.25` by `+2.24`, matching DiffCSE `78.49`, and trailing PromptBERT `78.54` by `0.05`.
- **Standard STS, supervised BERTbase**: PromCSE-BERTbase + EH reaches `82.13`, compared with reproduced SimCSE `81.53`, SimCSE + EH `81.94`, and PromCSE without EH `81.81`.
- **Standard STS, supervised RoBERTa-large**: PromCSE-RoBERTa-large + EH achieves the best reported average `85.10`, above SimCSE-RoBERTa-large `83.76` and SimCSE-RoBERTa-large + EH `84.08`.
- **Domain-shifted CxC-STS, unsupervised**: PromCSE-BERTbase scores `71.2 ± 1.1`, outperforming SimCSE `67.5 ± 1.2` by `+3.7`, DiffCSE `70.1 ± 1.1` by `+1.1`, and PromptBERT `70.0 ± 1.1` by `+1.2`.
- **Domain-shifted CxC-STS, supervised**: PromCSE-BERTbase + EH reaches `74.0 ± 1.0`, above supervised SimCSE `73.0 ± 1.1`, SimCSE + EH `73.2 ± 1.0`, and even MURAL `73.9 ± 0.4`.
- **Alignment / uniformity analysis**: unsupervised PromCSE improves alignment from `0.238` to `0.117` versus unsupervised SimCSE; supervised PromCSE + EH improves uniformity to `-3.397` versus supervised SimCSE `-3.246`.
- **Ablation signals**: input-layer-only prompting collapses average STS to `68.35`, while layer-shared multi-layer prompts still achieve `77.64`; the best EH margin is `m = 0.2`, giving average STS `81.94`.

## Limitations

- The method improves STS under both unsupervised and supervised settings, but the paper reports no improvement on supervised transfer tasks in Appendix C.
- The EH term depends on hard negatives from labeled NLI data; the paper does not solve automatic hard-negative retrieval or generation for unlabeled training.
- Evaluation is concentrated on seven STS tasks plus one domain-shifted caption benchmark, so robustness claims outside sentence similarity remain under-tested.
- Because the method relies on pretrained language models, it may inherit upstream biases from pretraining data.
