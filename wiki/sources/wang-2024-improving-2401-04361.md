## Summary

The paper studies robustness in knowledge-grounded dialogue (KGD), arguing that existing systems degrade under realistic noise in dialogue context and knowledge graphs, such as misspellings, abbreviations, incomplete triples, and erroneous facts. It proposes EnCo, an entity-based contrastive learning framework that creates semantically faithful positive samples through entity-guided paraphrasing and truncated knowledge, while constructing hard negative samples by deleting or replacing entities in both the context and the aligned KG. Built on a BART-based KGD backbone with TransR knowledge encoding and context-knowledge fusion, EnCo achieves new state-of-the-art automatic metrics on KdConv, DuConv, and DuRecDial, improves robustness under eight noisy test conditions, and remains strong in few-shot regimes, though it still trails plain BART on perplexity.

## Problem & Motivation

Knowledge-grounded dialogue models usually optimize response quality on clean benchmark data, but deployment settings introduce noisy dialogue contexts and imperfect knowledge graphs. The paper argues that this mismatch is especially problematic for KGD because the model must jointly reason over user utterances and external triples, so perturbations such as misspellings, abbreviations, missing triples, or wrong entities can easily derail response generation. The authors therefore target robustness as a first-class objective, aiming to make the encoder distinguish semantic-irrelevant perturbations that should preserve responses from semantic-relevant perturbations that should change them.

## Method

- **Task setup**: given dialogue context `C = {u_1, ..., u_{n-1}}` and relevant triple set `K = {(h_1, r_1, t_1), ..., (h_m, r_m, t_m)}`, the model generates response `u_n`.
- **Positive samples**: EnCo builds `⟨C^+, K^+⟩` by paraphrasing each utterance with an entity-aware BART paraphraser while preserving entity spans using special markers `[Ent]` and `[\Ent]`; knowledge is truncated by randomly removing `lambda%` triples with `lambda` sampled from `0` to `15`.
- **Paraphrase model**: the paraphraser uses BART-large with standard Transformer encoder-decoder structure, `12` encoder layers, `12` decoder layers, `16` attention heads, and hidden size `1024`; it is trained on filtered ParaZh-22M pairs, reduced from `22M` to about `6.5M` examples after keeping only sentence pairs with identical entity sets.
- **Negative samples**: EnCo builds `⟨C^-, K^-⟩` using entity-guided augmentation. Each detected entity is modified with probability `0.3`; conditioned on modification, deletion / relevant replacement / irrelevant replacement are chosen with probabilities `0.1 / 0.8 / 0.1`. The same edit is applied to the KG to avoid easy context-KG mismatch shortcuts.
- **KGD backbone**: the response model has a context encoder with `N_e = 12` stacked Transformer encoder layers, a TransR-based knowledge encoder with entity/relation embedding size `200`, a context-knowledge fusion module with `m = 8` attention heads, and a decoder with `N_d = 12` Transformer decoder layers initialized from Chinese BART-large.
- **Fusion and decoding**: each knowledge triple embedding is fused with the encoded context through multi-head attention `Attn_h(h_{k_i} <- H_C^{N_e})`; the decoder then performs parallel cross-attention over `H_C^{N_e}` and the fused knowledge representation `H_K^c`.
- **Training objective**: the generative loss is `L_ce = L_van + L_pos`, where `L_van` is cross-entropy on vanilla samples and `L_pos` is cross-entropy on positive samples. Contrastive learning adds `L_ctr`, yielding final loss `L = L_ce + alpha L_ctr` with `alpha = 1.0`.
- **Optimization details**: for each training sample, the model creates `5` positive and `5` negative samples, trains with batch size `8` on `2 x 32GB` Tesla V100 GPUs, uses Adam with `1000` warmup steps, tunes learning rate in `{1e-5, 2e-5, 3e-5, 5e-5}`, and reports the best setting at `5e-5` for `20` epochs averaged over `3` runs.

## Key Results

- **KdConv**: EnCo sets the best automatic scores across all three domains, e.g. music `BLEU-1/2/3/4 = 39.39/30.18/25.11/20.81`, travel `46.61/40.58/37.02/34.14`, and film `31.43/21.77/16.48/13.17`, beating KRP-DS on BLEU and DISTINCT with reported `p < 0.01`.
- **DuConv**: EnCo reaches `BLEU-1/2/3/4 = 40.10/29.69/24.89/21.30` and `DISTINCT-1/2/3/4 = 6.88/25.66/38.50/49.17`, improving over KRP-DS (`39.06/28.64/23.96/20.66`) and BART+Know (`38.91/28.50/23.71/20.10`).
- **DuRecDial**: EnCo achieves `BLEU-1/2/3/4 = 40.05/29.55/24.68/21.26` and `DISTINCT-1/2/3/4 = 2.78/17.49/31.48/43.88`, again outperforming KRP-DS and BART+Know.
- **Robustness under noise**: on DuConv, EnCo keeps the strongest BLEU under all eight perturbation settings, e.g. vanilla `40.10/29.69`, word deletion `36.03/25.76`, ChatGPT noises `34.07/22.62`, entity deletion `37.42/27.02`, and entity replacement `36.11/26.18`, all above BART+Know.
- **Ablations**: removing positive-sample supervision drops DuConv BLEU from `40.10/29.69/24.89/21.30` to `36.59/28.01/23.54/18.73`; removing contrastive loss drops it to `36.02/27.19/23.21/18.44`, showing both components matter.

## Limitations

- The study is confined to three Chinese KGD benchmarks and a BART-large backbone, so it does not test whether the robustness gains transfer to larger decoder-only LLMs or non-Chinese settings.
- Robustness evaluation is based on synthetic perturbations such as `30%` deletions/replacements and prompt-generated noisy contexts, which approximate but do not fully cover naturally occurring user and KG errors.
- EnCo improves BLEU and diversity but not perplexity: plain BART still achieves the best PPL on DuConv and DuRecDial, indicating a trade-off between robustness-oriented knowledge use and token-level likelihood.
- The method assumes entity extraction quality from TexSmart and aligned KG edits; errors in NER or entity typing could propagate into both positive and negative sample construction.
