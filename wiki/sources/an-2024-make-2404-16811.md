## Summary

This paper proposes information-intensive training to make long-context [[large-language-model]]s actually use evidence placed anywhere in a 32K window rather than mostly at the edges. The method synthesizes long-context QA examples by shuffling many short segments and forcing answers to depend on one highly specific segment or on multiple distant segments, then applies this recipe to Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.2 to produce [[film-7b]]. The resulting model substantially reduces the [[lost-in-the-middle]] effect on the paper's VaL probing suite across document, code, and structured-data contexts. It also improves real long-context benchmarks such as NarrativeQA, Qasper, HotpotQA, and MuSiQue, while largely preserving short-context performance, suggesting that targeted synthetic supervision can transfer beyond the synthetic training distribution.

## Problem & Motivation

The paper studies why long-context models with nominal 32K windows still fail to retrieve or reason over information placed in the middle of the prompt. The authors hypothesize that standard pretraining and supervised alignment induce a [[position-bias]]: next-token loss is dominated by nearby prefixes, and instruction-following data often places decisive signals at the beginning of the prompt. If that hypothesis is right, simply extending context length is insufficient; the model also needs explicit supervision showing that crucial evidence can appear at arbitrary positions and may require cross-segment integration.

## Method

- **Training objective**: construct long-context QA triples where the answer depends on randomly placed evidence, then perform [[instruction-tuning]] so the concatenated context and question form the instruction and loss is applied only to the answer tokens.
- **Fine-grained awareness data**: sample a `~128`-token segment `s_i` from C4 realnewslike, prompt [[gpt-4-turbo]] to generate a highly specific QA pair, and build a long context with `L_i = concat(Shuffle(s_i, [r_j]))`, where `[r_j]` are distractor segments.
- **Integration/reasoning data**: split a source text into multiple `~128`-token segments `[s_i]`, prompt for a QA pair that requires at least two segments, and synthesize `L_i = concat(Shuffle([s_i], [r_j]))`; this makes [[multi-hop-reasoning]] supervision explicit over distant spans.
- **Length balancing and data mixture**: reject-sample distractors so context length is roughly uniform from `4K` to `32K`; final training data contains `1.1M` fine-grained long-context examples (`~63%`), `300K` integration/reasoning examples (`~17%`), `150K` short-context QA examples (`~9%`), and `200K` OpenOrca instruction examples (`~11%`).
- **Backbone and optimization**: start from [[mistral-7b-instruct-v0.2]], use global batch size `128`, one epoch, `~14K` steps, cosine decay with max learning rate `1e-6`, and `3%` warm-up.
- **Systems setup**: train on `16` nodes of `8 x 80GB` A100 GPUs with PyTorch FSDP full sharding and CPU offload; total cost is about `300` GPU-days.
- **Evaluation design**: introduce VaL probing with `~32K` contexts and `~3K` examples per task, covering document sentence retrieval (bi-directional), code function retrieval (backward), and database entity retrieval (forward), in addition to LongBench-style real tasks and standard short-context benchmarks.
- **Training-strategy analysis**: test `4K` sliding-window variants and RoPE-base changes; increasing the [[rotary-positional-embedding]] base from `1.0e6` to `1.0e7` improves probing robustness, while sliding windows sharply hurt long-distance retrieval.

## Key Results

- VaL probing overall: [[film-7b]] reaches `85.9` average score with `13.9` min-max gap, versus `47.3 / 56.2` for [[mistral-7b-instruct-v0.2]] and `79.0 / 32.1` for [[gpt-4-turbo]].
- By probing task, FilM-7B scores `85.4 / 6.1` on document, `83.3 / 18.7` on code, and `89.0 / 16.8` on database retrieval, strongly reducing middle-position collapse relative to the backbone.
- On real long-context tasks, average performance improves from `30.6` to `39.9`; notable gains include NarrativeQA `23.5 -> 26.9`, Qasper `33.8 -> 42.2`, MultiFQA `45.9 -> 56.0`, HotpotQA `42.4 -> 62.1`, 2WikiMQA `24.3 -> 47.0`, and MuSiQue `20.8 -> 39.0`.
- Short-context capability is mostly preserved: the abstract reports MMLU `59.3 -> 59.2` after long-context training.
- RoPE-base analysis on `20%` of training data shows overall VaL probing improving from `80.3 / 23.6` at `theta = 1.0e6` to `84.9 / 14.3` at `theta = 1.0e7`.
- Sliding-window training fails to preserve very long-distance retrieval: with a `4K` window, performance drops dramatically once the query-evidence distance exceeds the window size.

## Limitations

- The core supervision is synthetic and depends on [[gpt-4-turbo]] plus C4 realnewslike, so quality, diversity, and bias are partly inherited from external data generation choices.
- The main gains are demonstrated within a `32K` regime; the paper does not show whether the same recipe scales to much longer windows or to interactive agent settings.
- Improvements are uneven across downstream tasks: gains on QMSum (`24.8 -> 25.1`), GovReport (`33.3 -> 33.8`), and MultiNews (`26.8 -> 26.9`) are marginal.
- The paper argues for a [[position-bias]] explanation, but this remains a hypothesis supported by intervention and evaluation rather than a direct mechanistic analysis.
- The sliding-window ablation suggests the method does not overcome architectural constraints by itself; models with limited effective receptive fields still fail at long-distance retrieval.
