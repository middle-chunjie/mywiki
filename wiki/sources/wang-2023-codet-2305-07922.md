## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

CodeT5+ proposes an open family of code large language models built on a flexible encoder-decoder backbone that can be activated as encoder-only, decoder-only, or full seq2seq depending on the downstream task. The paper combines two-stage pretraining over `51.5B` code tokens and `1,929,817` text-code pairs with a mixture of span denoising, causal LM, contrastive, matching, and bimodal generation objectives, then scales to `2B`, `6B`, and `16B` parameters by freezing pretrained CodeGen decoders and training only a shallow encoder plus cross-attention. Across more than 20 benchmarks, CodeT5+ improves retrieval, completion, summarization, math programming, and retrieval-augmented generation, while the instruction-tuned `16B` model reaches `35.0` `pass@1` on HumanEval.

## Problem & Motivation

The paper targets a structural mismatch in prior code LLMs. Encoder-only models are strong at understanding tasks such as retrieval, while decoder-only models dominate generative tasks such as code synthesis and completion; unified encoder-decoder models cover both classes but often trail specialized baselines on at least one side. The authors also argue that pretraining-task mismatch is a second bottleneck: span denoising alone does not prepare models well for autoregressive generation, and retrieval-style tasks need stronger cross-modal alignment than standard denoising objectives provide. CodeT5+ is therefore designed to make model components compositional and to narrow the pretrain-finetune gap with a broader set of objectives spanning code-only and text-code data.

## Method

- **Backbone and operating modes**: CodeT5+ keeps an [[encoder-decoder-architecture]] but exposes three task modes: encoder-only for [[code-understanding]], decoder-only for [[code-completion]], and full encoder-decoder for [[code-generation]], [[code-summarization]], and [[retrieval-augmented-generation]].
- **Stage-1 unimodal code pretraining**: train on code-only data with three equally weighted objectives: span denoising, seq2seq causal LM, and decoder-only causal LM. Span denoising masks `15%` of tokens with sentinel tokens, uses whole-word masking, and samples span lengths with mean `3`.
- **Seq2seq causal LM**: sample a pivot uniformly from `10%` to `90%` of the sequence, prepend `[CLM]` to the source prefix, and predict the suffix. The decoder-only variant feeds only `[CLM]` to the encoder and generates the full sequence, explicitly strengthening [[autoregressive-language-model]] behavior.
- **Stage-2 bimodal pretraining**: continue training on text-code pairs using text-code contrastive learning, text-code matching, and dual causal generation (`text -> code` and `code -> text`) with equal weights.
- **Contrastive objective**: map encoder `[CLS]` states to normalized `256`-dimensional embeddings and score pairs with `s_{i,j}^{t2c} = h_i^{t\\top} h_j^c` and `s_{i,j}^{c2t} = h_i^{c\\top} h_j^t`; optimize the symmetric contrastive loss `L_tcc` with learned temperature `τ`. A [[momentum-encoder]] queue stores past embeddings to enlarge the negative pool.
- **Matching objective**: prepend `[Match]` to code, append `[EOS]`, use the decoder's last `[EOS]` embedding as a fused text-code representation, and train a binary classifier with loss `L_tcm`. Hard negatives are mined from the momentum queue via similarity scores, so [[hard-negative-mining]] is part of the training recipe rather than a post-hoc trick.
- **Cross-modal causal generation**: use `[CDec]` for `text -> code` generation and `[TDec]` for `code -> text` generation, yielding the full second-stage loss `L = L_tcc + L_tcm + L_t2c + L_c2t`.
- **Compute-efficient scaling**: the `2B`, `6B`, and `16B` models use a shallow-encoder/deep-decoder design. The encoder is initialized from CodeGen-mono `350M`, the decoder from CodeGen-mono `2B/6B/16B`, and only the encoder plus the top `L = 1` cross-attention layer remain trainable. Added trainable cross-attention parameters are only `36M`, `67M`, and `151M` respectively.
- **Pretraining data**: curated GitHub code covers `37,274,876` files in 9 languages and `51.5B` tokens after filtering to permissive licenses and file lengths `50-2000` tokens; the bimodal stage uses CodeSearchNet's `1,929,817` function-level text-code pairs across 6 languages.
- **Optimization details**: stage 1 runs `10k` warmup steps on denoising, then `100k` joint steps with peak learning rate `2e-4`, batch sizes `2048` (denoising) and `512` (CLM), and maximum lengths `512` (denoising), `768/600` (seq2seq CLM source/target), and `1/1024` (decoder-only CLM source/target). Stage 2 runs `10` epochs with batch size `256`, peak learning rate `1e-4`, and code/text lengths `420/128`. Training uses AdamW with weight decay `0.1`, FP16 mixed precision, DeepSpeed ZeRO-2/3, and `16 x A100-40G` GPUs.
- **Instruction tuning**: the authors further align the model with more than `20k` synthetic instruction examples for up to `3` epochs, producing InstructCodeT5+.

## Key Results

- **HumanEval zero-shot**: InstructCodeT5+ `16B` reaches `35.0` `pass@1`, `54.5` `pass@10`, and `77.9` `pass@100`; with CodeT-style test generation it rises to `42.9 / 67.8 / 78.7`.
- **Math programming**: CodeT5+ `770M` gets `87.4` `pass@80` on MathQA-Python and `73.8` `pass@100` on GSM8K-Python, outperforming several much larger models.
- **Code summarization**: CodeT5+ `770M` achieves overall BLEU-4 `20.15` on CodeSearchNet, above CodeT5 `220M` at `19.55` and UniXcoder `125M` at `19.30`.
- **Code completion**: in decoder-only mode, CodeT5+ `770M` reaches `44.86` EM / `74.22` edit similarity on PY150 and `37.90` / `72.25` on JavaCorpus; CodeT5+ `220M` improves over CodeT5 `220M` by `+6.45` EM on PY150 and `+10.37` EM on JavaCorpus.
- **Text-to-code retrieval**: CodeT5+ `770M` obtains MRR `77.4` on CodeSearchNet overall, `74.0` on CosQA, and `44.7` on AdvTest, beating UniXcoder by more than `3` absolute points on all three benchmarks.
- **Retrieval-augmented generation**: CodeT5+ `220M` as a unified retriever-generator reaches Java `11.66` EM / `33.83` BLEU-4 / `40.60` CodeBLEU and Python `11.83 / 31.14 / 36.39`, outperforming REDCODER-EXT.
- **Ablation evidence**: removing stage-1 causal LM drops CodeT5+ `770M` from `44.9` to `36.2` EM on PY150 and from `87.4` to `72.3` `pass@80` on MathQA-Python; removing matching reduces retrieval overall MRR from `77.4` to `74.8`.

## Limitations

- The paper is benchmark-heavy and remains centered on function-level code tasks; it does not evaluate broader software engineering workflows such as repository-level planning, debugging, or multi-file editing.
- Although strong among open models in 2023, the main HumanEval result (`35.0` `pass@1`) still trails much larger or closed systems such as GPT-4 (`67.0`) and code-davinci-002 (`47.0`).
- The instruction-tuning corpus is synthetic and explicitly noisy; the paper notes at least one incorrect target in the illustrated optimization example, so alignment gains rely on robustness to label noise.
- The pretraining corpus is deduplicated only by exact match after whitespace normalization, so residual near-duplicates may remain.
- Retrieval-augmented generation is evaluated with short input budgets (`max source length = 600`) and performance saturates around `k = 5`, suggesting context-length limits constrain further gains.
