## Summary

The paper targets complex [[knowledge-graph-question-answering]] under the information-retrieval pipeline, where answer quality is heavily constrained by subgraph quality. It argues that prior IR-KGQA systems retrieve question-relevant facts independently and therefore miss the structural dependencies that make those facts jointly valid evidence. The proposed Evidence Pattern Retrieval (EPR) framework represents these dependencies as graph patterns over topic entities, retrieves atomic relation/entity patterns with dense retrieval, enumerates candidate evidence patterns, and ranks them with a cross-encoder before downstream reasoning. On ComplexWebQuestions, EPR+NSM reaches `60.6` Hits@1 and `61.2` F1, improving IR baselines by large margins, while remaining competitive on WebQuestionsSP. The main contribution is showing that explicit structural evidence modeling materially improves complex KGQA subgraph extraction.

## Problem & Motivation

IR-style KGQA avoids gold logical-form annotation, but it relies on extracting a question-specific subgraph before answer reasoning. Existing subgraph extraction methods usually emphasize whether individual relations or facts are relevant to the question, while under-modeling how these facts must connect to justify an answer. The paper's core motivation is that noisy but locally plausible relations can mislead downstream reasoning unless the system explicitly represents the structural dependency among topic entities, intermediate variables, and answer-supporting relations.

## Method

- **Task decomposition**: the pipeline keeps the standard IR-KGQA factorization `SG_q = Ext(G, q, Pr_phi)` and `A_pred = {e in SG_q | Pr_psi(e in A_q | q, SG_q) > theta}`, but focuses on improving `Pr_phi` through better [[subgraph-extraction]].
- **Evidence pattern formulation**: an evidence pattern `pat(SG, q)` is a variable-substituted graph where entities not mentioned in the question are replaced by variables, and subgraph extraction becomes `argmax_{SG subset G} sim(pat(SG, q), q)`.
- **Atomic pattern decomposition**: EPR indexes two kinds of [[atomic-pattern]]s, entity-relation APs (ER-APs) and relation-relation APs (RR-APs), so that larger [[evidence-pattern]]s can be built from atomic adjacency structures instead of enumerating the full graph space.
- **Dense retrieval stage**: RR-APs are retrieved with a [[bi-encoder]] over BERT `[CLS]` vectors, using `V_q = BertCLS_1(q)`, `V_p = BertCLS_2(p)`, and `sim(p, q) = V_q^T · V_p`; the system builds a [[faiss]] index over `2,366,590` RR-APs from [[freebase]].
- **Training data construction**: pseudo evidence patterns are built from `1`- or `2`-hop paths between topic entities and a sampled answer; each positive AP gets `19` negatives, with half created by corrupting a relation or link tag and half sampled globally from KG APs.
- **Candidate EP construction**: starting from ER-APs, the system iteratively expands patterns with ER-APs or RR-APs under a size limit `tau`, where `tau = 5` on [[complexwebquestions]] and `tau = 3` on [[webquestionssp]]; valid patterns must cover all topic entities and obey constrained endpoint structures.
- **Ranking stage**: candidate EPs are serialized as Freebase-labeled triplet sequences and scored with a BERT [[cross-encoder]] using `sim(P, q) = Linear(BertCLS([q; P]))`; positives are candidates covering the most answers.
- **Implementation**: both retriever and ranker use `bert-base-uncased`; AP retrieval uses vector dimension `768`, batch size `16`, `5` epochs, learning rate `2e-5`, while EP ranking uses dimension `768`, batch size `2`, `10` epochs, and learning rate `1e-5`.
- **Inference trade-off**: the number of retrieved RR-APs is controlled by `K`, grid-searched from `20` to `200` with step `10`; larger `K` improves coverage but increases EP enumeration cost combinatorially on harder questions.

## Key Results

- On [[complexwebquestions]], `EPR_{K=100} + NSM` reaches `60.6` H@1 and `61.2` F1, improving over the strongest prior IR F1 result `48.0` (`UniKGQA + UniKGQA`) by `+13.2` and over `KD-COT` H@1 `55.7` by `+4.9`.
- On [[webquestionssp]], `EPR_{K=100} + NSM` reaches `71.2` H@1 and `70.2` F1, matching the best reported IR F1 (`70.2` from `UniKGQA + UniKGQA`) while improving over `SR + NSM` by `+1.7` H@1 and `+6.1` F1.
- Average answer-cover statistics show that better extraction transfers downstream: on CWQ, average H@1 is `59.1`, cover rate `77.9`, and ratio `75.9%`; on WebQSP, average H@1 is `70.5`, cover rate `80.2`, and ratio `87.9%`.
- Increasing retrieved APs helps complex datasets more than simpler ones: CWQ cover rate ranges from `62.1%` to `82.7%` with runtime `0.31s` to `2.68s`, whereas WebQSP cover rate ranges only from `74.3%` to `81.3%` with runtime `0.33s` to `0.40s`.
- The ranking model is not highly data-hungry: on CWQ, using `20%` of ranking data already gives `59.6` F1 versus `61.2` with full data; on WebQSP, `64.0` F1 at `20%` rises to `70.2` with full data.
- Unseen relations remain a major failure mode: with unseen relations, performance drops to `17.4` H@1 / `19.7` F1 on CWQ and `36.1` H@1 / `35.4` F1 on WebQSP.

## Limitations

- The method still suffers from combinatorial explosion during brute-force EP enumeration as `K` grows, especially on CWQ.
- Retrieval quality degrades sharply on questions containing unseen relations, indicating limited generalization in the atomic-pattern retriever.
- A large fraction of errors come from non-entity or numerical evidence (`54%` on CWQ, `30%` on WebQSP), which the current IR-KGQA formulation and answer reasoner do not model well.
- On WebQSP, the method is competitive rather than clearly state of the art, so the benefit is strongest on more complex questions.
- The system depends on pseudo evidence patterns instead of gold structural supervision, which may cap ranking quality.
