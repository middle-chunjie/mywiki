## Summary

The paper proposes Future-KL Influenced Policy Optimization (FIPO), a reinforcement-learning method for eliciting deeper reasoning from base large language models without adding a critic. Starting from the observation that GRPO-style outcome rewards broadcast the same advantage to every token, FIPO computes a discounted cumulative future log-probability shift and uses it to re-weight token-level advantages. The method adds masking and clipping to suppress unstable negative-advantage outliers, then plugs the resulting weight into a DAPO-style clipped objective. On Qwen2.5-32B-Base trained with the public DAPO-17K math dataset, FIPO increases AIME 2024 Avg@32 from 50.0% to 56.0%, reaches a 58.0% peak during training, and expands average reasoning length from roughly 4,000 to over 10,000 tokens.

## Problem & Motivation

GRPO-style RL for reasoning models is efficient because it avoids a value network, but its outcome-based reward only gives a sequence-level signal and therefore applies essentially uniform credit to all tokens in a sampled trajectory. The authors argue that this coarse credit assignment creates a length-performance ceiling: the model learns short or moderately long successful traces, yet fails to keep extending reasoning when only a few critical logical pivots actually determine correctness. FIPO is motivated by the need to recover denser token-level supervision inside an ORM/GRPO pipeline, so that reasoning length can continue to scale without switching back to full PPO with a learned critic.

## Method

- Define token-level policy drift as `Δlog p_t = log π_θ(o_t | q, o_<t) - log π_θ_old(o_t | q, o_<t)` and treat it as the atomic signal for reasoning-improvement dynamics.
- Aggregate future influence with `FutureKL_t = Σ_{k=t}^T Δlog p_k`, then interpret the sum as a sample-based estimate of how the current token affects the future trajectory's policy preference.
- Mask unstable negative outliers with `M_k = 1_{A<0}(π_θ(o_k|o_<t) / π_old(o_k|o_<t) ≤ c)` so that tokens breaching the Dual-Clip threshold `c` are excluded from the Future-KL accumulation.
- Apply a soft decay window: `FutureKL_t = Σ_{k=t}^T M_k · γ^(k-t) · Δlog p_k`, with `γ = 2^(-1/τ)` and effective horizon `τ = 32`, so near-future tokens matter more than distant ones.
- Convert Future-KL into a bounded multiplicative weight with `f_t = clip(exp(FutureKL_t), 1 - ε_f_low, 1 + ε_f_high)` and re-weight the standard advantage as `Ã_t = Â_t · f_t`; for the 32B model the paper uses `f_t ∈ [1.0, 1.2]`.
- Optimize a DAPO-style clipped loss `J_FIPO(θ)` where the PPO/GRPO importance ratio `r_{i,t}` is multiplied by the Future-KL weight `f_{i,t}` inside the token-level clipped surrogate objective.
- Training follows DAPO settings on the public `DAPO-17K` math dataset using the verl framework: `512` prompts per batch, `16` sampled responses per prompt, `8,192` total samples per iteration, and a larger mini-batch of `1,024` samples (`64` prompts) for stability instead of the baseline `512`.
- Response length is capped at `20,480` tokens, with an overlong penalty beyond `16,384`; evaluation repeats each benchmark `32` times with temperature `1.0` and `top-p = 0.7`.
- The implementation uses a chunked algorithm for Future-KL accumulation to reduce peak memory from naive `O(L^2)` storage to `O(B·L + L·K)` while keeping the same analytical result.

## Key Results

- On AIME 2024, FIPO improves `Avg@32` from `50.0%` to `56.0%`, `Cons@32` from `60.0%` to `73.0%`, and `Pass@32` from `80.0%` to `83.0%` over the DAPO baseline.
- On AIME 2025, FIPO improves `Avg@32` from `38.0%` to `43.0%`, `Cons@32` from `47.0%` to `50.0%`, and `Pass@32` from `63.0%` to `67.0%`.
- The paper reports a peak AIME 2024 `Pass@1` of `58.0%` during training, with convergence around `56.0%`, outperforming reported comparisons to DeepSeek-R1-Zero-Math-32B (`~47.0%`) and o1-mini (`~56.0%`).
- Average chain-of-thought length grows from roughly `4,000` tokens under DAPO to more than `10,000` tokens under FIPO, while the median response length rises from about `200` tokens early in training to over `10,000`.
- In the 7B ablations, the best reported configuration reaches `40.0%` on AIME 2024 and `19.0%` on AIME 2025 with filtered influence weights in `[0.8, 1.2]`; a larger decay horizon `τ = 256` reaches `42.0%` on AIME 2024 but drops to `16.0%` on AIME 2025.
- Stability analyses show smoother policy KL growth, lower and steadier gradient norms, and a sustained increase in entropy relative to DAPO, consistent with broader exploration rather than early length stagnation.

## Limitations

- The method increases computation because successful training deliberately elicits trajectories longer than `10,000` tokens, raising both training and inference cost.
- Experiments are concentrated on mathematical reasoning benchmarks, so transfer to coding, symbolic logic, or open-ended domains is not demonstrated.
- The training data is intentionally restricted to the public `DAPO-17K` dataset, leaving scaling behavior on larger or cleaner RLVR corpora untested.
- The main study centers on clean base models such as Qwen2.5 rather than already distilled long-CoT models, so interaction with distilled reasoning priors is unclear.
- Future-KL introduces `O(B·L^2)` temporal aggregation in time even with chunked memory optimization, so the algorithm remains heavier than standard GRPO.
- The paper explicitly notes that RL self-evolution still trails teacher-driven distillation in supervision density, leaving a gap to stronger distilled reasoning systems.
