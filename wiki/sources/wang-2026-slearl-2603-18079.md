## Summary

SLEA-RL proposes a reinforcement-learning framework for multi-turn LLM agents that retrieves prior experiences at every decision step instead of once per task. The method combines a self-evolving experience library, observation-cluster-indexed retrieval, and step-level credit assignment so that guidance adapts as the environment state changes. Retrieved strategies and warnings are injected during both training and inference, which lets the policy learn under the same augmentation regime it will use at test time. On Qwen2.5-based agents, SLEA-RL reaches 93.5% success on ALFWorld and 76.3% success on WebShop, outperforming strong GRPO-style and experience-augmented baselines, and it also posts the best average score on seven search-augmented QA benchmarks.

## Problem & Motivation

Standard multi-turn RL for LLM agents treats each rollout as isolated, so the policy cannot directly reuse structured experience from prior successes and failures. Existing experience-augmented approaches partially address this by retrieving memories once from the initial task description, but this becomes stale in long-horizon settings where the observation changes after every action. The paper argues that both retrieval and credit assignment should be state-dependent: the useful guidance for an agent browsing categories on WebShop is different from the guidance needed when comparing products or checking out later in the episode. SLEA-RL is designed to supply experience conditioned on the current observation and to assign finer-grained learning signal to decisions made under similar states.

## Method

- **Agent formulation**: the agent follows `\pi_\theta(a_t \mid o_{\le t}, d, c)` in environment `\mathcal{E}`, where `d` is the task description and `c` is retrieved experience context; the objective is `\max_\theta \mathbb{E}_{\tau \sim \pi_\theta}[\sum_{t=0}^{T} \gamma^t r_t]`.
- **Experience library**: maintain `E = E^{+} \cup E^{-}` with entries `e = (s_e, l_e, z_e)`, where `s_e` is a textual strategy/warning, `l_e \in {principle, pattern, example}` is the abstraction level, and `z_e` is the source-trajectory quality score. The implementation uses per-level capacities `C = 100` for strategy entries and `50` for warning entries.
- **Observation clustering**: assign each observation to a cluster `c(o_t)` if `sim(o_t, rep(c_i)) \ge \delta`, otherwise create a new cluster; the paper uses similarity threshold `\delta = 0.85` and stores cluster-specific pools `E^{+}_{c_i}` and `E^{-}_{c_i}`.
- **Step-level retrieval**: if a cluster already has experiences, retrieve `\varepsilon_t = TopK(E^{+}_{c(o_t)}, k^{+}) \cup TopK(E^{-}_{c(o_t)}, k^{-})` with `k^{+} = 2`, `k^{-} = 1`; otherwise fall back to library-wide semantic retrieval over entries satisfying `sim(o_t, e) > \delta`. Retrieved text is capped at `200` tokens.
- **Warmup and gating**: retrieval starts only after `W = 5` epochs and when the library size exceeds `C_{min} = 10`, to avoid amplifying low-quality early experiences.
- **Self-evolving library update**: trajectories are partitioned at threshold `\eta` into `\mathcal{T}^{+} = \{\tau_j : r_j > \eta\}` and `\mathcal{T}^{-} = \{\tau_j : r_j \le \eta\}`. Only the top/bottom `K_{traj} = 5` trajectories are analyzed, yielding at most `K_{strat} = 10` strategies and `K_{warn} = 5` warnings per evolution step.
- **Step-level credit assignment**: discounted return is `\hat{R}^{(i)}_t = \sum_{k=t}^{T} \gamma^{k-t} r^{(i)}_k`. Within each observation cluster, returns are normalized to obtain `A_{step}`, and the final advantage is `\hat{A} = A_{episode} + w \cdot A_{step}` with `w = 1.0`.
- **Policy optimization**: use a clipped GRPO-style objective with per-step importance ratio `\rho_t^{(i)}`; training hyperparameters include learning rate `1e-6`, batch size `16`, group size `G = 8`, gradient accumulation `4`, clipping `\epsilon = 0.2`, and KL coefficient `\beta = 0.01`. Search-QA experiments use `E5` retrieval with `G = 5`.

## Key Results

- On Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, SLEA-RL reaches `93.5%` overall success on ALFWorld and `87.6` WebShop score / `76.3%` WebShop success, beating GiGPO's `90.8%`, `84.4`, and `72.8%`.
- Relative to GiGPO on ALFWorld subtasks, SLEA-RL improves Heat from `83.7%` to `93.8%` (`+10.1`) and Pick2 from `79.2%` to `90.4%` (`+11.2`), showing the largest gains on harder long-horizon settings.
- On Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct, SLEA-RL achieves `87.5%` ALFWorld success and `88.7` / `75.4%` on WebShop, including a `+10.4%` WebShop-success gain over GiGPO (`65.0%`).
- On search-augmented QA, SLEA-RL posts the best average score `60.9`, above IGPO's `58.7`, with strong multi-hop results such as `70.5` on 2Wiki and `77.2` on MuSiQue.
- Ablations show step-level retrieval outperforms task-level retrieval on WebShop (`87.6` vs. `84.8` score; `76.3%` vs. `72.0%` success), and removing step-level advantage drops performance to `87.8%` on ALFWorld and `70.8%` WebShop success.
- Experience-library capacity matters: on WebShop, `C = 100` performs best (`87.6` / `76.3%`), compared with `C = 25` (`84.5` / `71.8%`) and `C = 200` (`87.1` / `75.6%`).

## Limitations

- Retrieval relies on string-similarity clustering with hand-tuned thresholds such as `\delta = 0.85`; semantically similar but lexically different observations may still be mismatched.
- The method adds an auxiliary LLM-based extraction step once per training batch, so the library is not free to maintain even if per-step retrieval itself is lightweight.
- Evaluation is concentrated on ALFWorld, WebShop, and search-QA tasks with Qwen2.5 backbones; transfer to other agent architectures and environments is not fully established.
- Gains are not uniform across all datasets: on Bamboogle, SLEA-RL scores `33.2`, far below IGPO's `74.9`, suggesting the approach can underperform when the benchmark's reasoning structure mismatches the library dynamics.
- The library uses heuristic admission, capacity, and novelty controls instead of a learned retrieval index, so long-run stability under larger-scale training remains an open systems question.
