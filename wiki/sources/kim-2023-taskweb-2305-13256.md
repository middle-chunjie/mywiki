## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

This paper studies how to choose source tasks that best help an unseen NLP target task when only a few target examples are available. It introduces TASKWEB, a benchmark of roughly `25,000` pairwise transfer experiments spanning `22` NLP tasks, `3` architecture families, multiple model sizes, and `3` adaptation methods. Based on an empirical finding that positive transfer behaves transitively more often than it behaves commutatively, the paper proposes TASKSHOP, a task-selection method that combines known transfer scores with a few-shot target-side scorer through pivot tasks. TASKSHOP improves both source-task ranking quality and top-task identification, and its selected small multi-task training sets outperform substantially larger baselines in zero-shot evaluation.

## Problem & Motivation

Large multi-task NLP models often benefit from more tasks, but it is unclear which source tasks are actually helpful for a new target task, especially when the target has only a handful of labeled examples. Exhaustively measuring pairwise transfer for every new target is expensive, and prior task-selection methods either ignore directionality or rely only on surface similarity. The paper asks whether explicit knowledge of inter-task transfer structure can support better single-task and multi-task source selection for unseen targets.

## Method

- **TASKWEB benchmark**: build pairwise transfer matrices over `22` NLP tasks across `~25,000` source-target runs, covering T5 (`small/base/large`), GPT-2 medium, and RoBERTa-base, with full fine-tuning plus parameter-efficient settings such as adapters and BitFit.
- **Pairwise transfer protocol**: train on full source task `s`, then fine-tune on `1,000` random examples of target task `t`, and compare against directly fine-tuning on the same `1,000` target examples; each transfer is repeated over `8` random seeds.
- **Transfer metrics**: measure average percentage change `PC(s,t) ∝ (p(m_{s→t}) - p(m_t)) / p(m_t)` and positive-transfer rate `PM(s,t) ∝ 1[p(m_{s→t}) > p(m_t)]`, combining magnitude and consistency across seeds.
- **Structural analysis**: test whether transfer is commutative (`A → B` implies `B → A`) and transitive (`A → B` and `B → C` help predict `A → C`), finding the latter much more useful for positive transfers.
- **TASKSHOP estimator**: for source `s` and unseen target `t`, use pivots `p ∈ S \\ {s}` and a few-shot scorer `F` to compute `TS(s,t) = λ · (1 / |S \\ {s}|) Σ_p ((T(s→p) + F(p→t)) / 2) + (1 - λ) · F(s→t)`.
- **Few-shot target access**: assume only `n ≤ 32` target examples are available when ranking source tasks; `F` is instantiated with Retrieval-of-Experts or an LLM-similarity scorer.
- **Multi-task extension**: select top-`k` source tasks, sample `2,000` prompted examples per selected task, and fine-tune LM-adapted T5-3B for `5` epochs with Adam, learning rate `1e-4`, and batch sizes `4` to `16`.
- **Evaluation design**: use leave-one-out evaluation so the target task's pairwise scores are hidden during selection, approximating an unseen-task setting while still allowing gold rankings from TASKWEB.

## Key Results

- TASKWEB covers `22` tasks and about `25,000` transfer experiments across `7` training setups.
- Only `97 / 210` unique task pairs exhibit commutativity, so transfer direction matters.
- Positive-transfer transitivity strengthens with thresholding: the probability of observing positive `A → C` rises from `88%` to `97%` when intermediate thresholds increase from `0.01` to `0.04`.
- In single-task selection, `TASKSHOP_ROE` reaches mean `NDCG = 71.54` versus `64.52` for RoE and `56.69` for LLM similarity.
- In the same setting, `TASKSHOP_ROE` lowers mean `Regret@5` to `2.66` versus `4.11` for RoE and `3.67` for LLM similarity.
- In multi-task zero-shot evaluation, `TASKSHOP_ROE` achieves mean `60.69`, beating `T5-3B + all tasks` at `58.16` and `T0-3B` at `52.88`.
- Using top-`5` selected source tasks gives the best overall mean (`60.69`) compared with top-`1` (`54.28`), top-`3` (`59.03`), top-`10` (`59.36`), and top-`21` (`58.16`).

## Limitations

- The benchmark only covers `22` English tasks and omits long-form, multilingual, and domain-specific tasks such as legal or biomedical NLP.
- Extending TASKWEB to a new task still requires `|T|` or `|M| × |T|` additional fine-tuning runs depending on whether the model family is fixed.
- Multi-task experiments only study models up to `3B` parameters, so the transfer patterns may not extrapolate cleanly to much larger LLMs.
- The multi-task recipe fixes uniform sampling at `2,000` examples per source task; different sampling or curriculum strategies may change the conclusions.
- The formulation assumes relatively discrete task boundaries, which may be less appropriate for modern instruction-tuning corpora with mixed or fuzzy task definitions.
