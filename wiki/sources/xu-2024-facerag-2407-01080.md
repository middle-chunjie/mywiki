## Summary

Face4RAG studies factual consistency evaluation for Chinese retrieval-augmented generation by arguing that prior RAG factuality benchmarks are too tied to the error distribution of a single underlying model. The paper contributes both a benchmark and an evaluation pipeline: Face4RAG combines a synthetic dataset organized by an explicit error typology with a real-world dataset generated by six LLMs, while L-Face4RAG augments standard segment-level checking with logic-preserving answer decomposition and a second-stage logic consistency analysis. The central empirical claim is that logical fallacies are a non-trivial failure mode in RAG answers and that existing evaluators under-detect them. Across synthetic, real-world, and external English factuality benchmarks, the proposed method substantially improves accuracy over prior fine-grained factual consistency evaluators.

## Problem & Motivation

Existing factual consistency evaluation methods for RAG are typically benchmarked on outputs from a particular LLM, so their measured performance can be overly coupled to one model's error distribution. The authors argue this is inadequate because different LLMs produce different mixtures of hallucination, knowledge, and logical errors, and prior pipelines mostly ignore logical fallacies introduced by decomposition-based evaluation. The paper therefore aims to build a benchmark that is less dependent on any single generator and to design an evaluator that can detect mismatched logical structure, not just unsupported or incorrect local facts.

## Method

- **Benchmark design**: Face4RAG combines a synthetic dataset and a real-world dataset for Chinese RAG factual consistency evaluation. The synthetic split contains `1299` answer-level samples and `6737` segment-level samples; the real-world split contains `1200` answer-level samples and `6143` segment-level samples.
- **Error typology**: the benchmark defines `3` top-level categories and `9` explicit error types: hallucination, `4` knowledge errors (`KCont.`, `KInve.`, `KConf.`, `KConc.`), and `4` logical fallacies (`LOver.`, `LCaus.`, `LConf.`, `LIncl.`), plus `LOth.` as an extra catch-all logical category during synthetic generation.
- **Synthetic dataset construction**: negative samples are generated from [[webcpm]] with prompts targeted at each error type; positive samples are expanded by synonym replacement and paraphrasing. The paper then applies human refinement with `12` annotators using a two-step segment-level factuality protocol.
- **Real-world dataset construction**: the authors collect `200` question-reference pairs and prompt `6` LLMs, giving `200 × 6 = 1200` answers from [[gpt-4]], [[gpt-3-5-turbo]], [[baichuan2]], [[chatglm3]], [[qwen]], and Alpaca2 (CH).
- **Logic-preserving answer decomposition**: L-Face4RAG decomposes answers only when adjacent spans lack strong semantic or logical coupling, replaces pronouns with explicit referents when needed, and preserves original sentence structure as much as possible to reduce decomposition-induced distortion.
- **Fact consistency stage**: for each segment, the evaluator performs informational-point extraction, supporting-context identification, and point-wise consistency checking. Operationally, a segment is factual only if every extracted informational point is supported.
- **Logic consistency stage**: the second stage identifies the relevant context, reconstructs the logical structure of both answer and reference, and compares relations such as causality, conditions, and inclusion. In compact form, `y_answer = 1 iff ∀ s_i, fact(s_i) = 1 ∧ logic(s_i) = 1`.
- **Implementation details**: all evaluated baselines are translated into Chinese prompts for the target task, GPT-4 is used as the evaluator in the proposed pipeline, and deterministic decoding is enforced with `temperature = 0`.

## Key Results

- On the synthetic Face4RAG benchmark, L-Face4RAG reaches `93.38%` overall accuracy, versus `78.52%` for RefChecker and `76.37%` for RAGAS with GPT-4.
- On the real-world benchmark, L-Face4RAG achieves `87.75%` overall accuracy, outperforming the strongest baseline in the table, RAGAS with GPT-4, at `72.92%`. Per-model scores are `90.0` on Baichuan2, `88.0` on ChatGLM3, `81.5` on GPT-3.5, `86.0` on GPT-4, `93.5` on Alpaca2 (CH), and `87.5` on Qwen.
- The paper reports that among inconsistent real-world answers, hallucination, knowledge error, and logical fallacy account for `73.78%`, `28.31%`, and `6.96%`, respectively, motivating explicit logic-aware evaluation.
- On external English factuality benchmarks, L-Face4RAG obtains an average score of `84.2` and is reported as SOTA on `6/7` datasets.
- Ablations on the synthetic benchmark show strong dependence on the proposed components: replacing logic-preserving decomposition drops overall accuracy from `93.38%` to `76.44%`, removing chain-of-thought drops it to `79.60%`, and removing logic evaluation drops it to `88.99%`.
- The largest per-error gains are on logical fallacies: for example, LCaus. accuracy is `92.86%` for L-Face4RAG versus `32.14%` with conventional decomposition and `35.71%` without logic evaluation.

## Limitations

- The benchmark is centered on Chinese RAG and its custom prompts, so the primary benchmark evidence does not directly establish equivalent coverage for other languages or retrieval settings.
- The proposed evaluator relies on GPT-4 as the core judge, which means reported gains may partly depend on proprietary-model capability and inference cost rather than only pipeline structure.
- The synthetic split is generated from a predefined error typology, so rare factuality failures outside the designed taxonomy may still be underrepresented even with the added `LOth.` bucket.
- The real-world benchmark uses `200` questions and `6` LLMs, which is broader than prior single-model setups but still a limited slice of the evolving RAG model landscape.
