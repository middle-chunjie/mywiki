## Summary

This paper studies a practical failure mode of [[retrieval-augmented-generation]]: retrieved evidence is often imperfect and may conflict with an LLM's [[parametric-knowledge]], causing standard RAG to underperform even retrieval-free generation. The authors propose Astute RAG, a training-free method for [[black-box-language-model]]s that first elicits internal passages from the model, then performs source-aware iterative [[knowledge-consolidation]] over internal and external evidence, and finally selects an answer by comparing the reliability of competing evidence groups. Across Claude, Gemini, and Mistral models, Astute RAG improves accuracy under both realistic web retrieval and worst-case noisy retrieval, with especially strong gains when knowledge conflicts are frequent and retrieval quality is low.

## Problem & Motivation

Standard RAG assumes retrieved passages are helpful, but realistic web retrieval often returns irrelevant, misleading, or malicious passages. This is particularly damaging when retrieved content conflicts with the model's own internal knowledge, because the generator may follow the wrong source or be confused by inconsistent evidence. The paper argues that robustness at the post-retrieval stage is underexplored: prior methods improve retrieval, filter context, or route between RAG and no-RAG, but do not explicitly compare and consolidate internal and external knowledge. The goal is therefore to make RAG more resilient to imperfect retrieval while preserving the grounding benefit of reliable external evidence.

## Method

- **Setup**: given query `q`, retrieved passages `E = [e_1, ..., e_n]`, and black-box LLM `M`, Astute RAG augments external evidence with internally generated passages `I = [i_1, ..., i_m]`.
- **Adaptive internal generation**: the model generates up to `m <= \hat{m}` internal passages using prompt `p_gen`; the default is `\hat{m} = 1`. The prompt includes constitutional guidance so passages should be accurate, relevant, and hallucination-free rather than merely diverse.
- **Initial merged evidence**: internal and external passages are concatenated as `D_0 = E \oplus I`, with source indicators `S_0 = [\mathbbm{1}_{d \in E}]` so later reasoning remains provenance-aware.
- **Iterative source-aware consolidation**: for iteration `j`, the model applies `\langle D_{j+1}, S_{j+1} \rangle = M(p_con, q, \langle D_0, S_0 \rangle, \langle D_j, S_j \rangle)` to group consistent information, expose conflicts, and discard irrelevant content. Main experiments use `t = 1`.
- **Answer finalization**: the answering prompt `p_ans` compares consolidated evidence groups by source, cross-source confirmation, frequency, and completeness, then outputs the most reliable answer rather than blindly trusting either retrieval or internal memory.
- **Experimental setup**: each short-form QA instance uses `10` retrieved passages from Google Search; inference uses temperature `0` and maximum output length `1024` tokens.

## Key Results

- Controlled analysis shows imperfect retrieval is widespread: roughly `70%` of retrieved passages do not directly contain the true answer.
- With Claude 3.5 Sonnet, `19.2%` of examples exhibit [[knowledge-conflict]] between answers with and without RAG.
- Among conflicting cases, internal knowledge is correct on `47.4%` of instances and external knowledge is correct on `52.6%`, motivating explicit consolidation rather than choosing one source globally.
- On Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Astute RAG reaches `61.7` overall accuracy versus `55.5` for vanilla RAG and `58.8` for the best listed baseline (InstructRAG).
- On Gemini 1.5 Pro, Astute RAG reaches `59.2` overall accuracy versus `53.7` for vanilla RAG and `57.6` for the best listed baseline (Self-Route).
- On the conflicting subset, Astute RAG selects the correct answer in roughly `80%` of cases.
- Efficiency remains favorable: compared with vanilla RAG, the method adds less than `5%` token cost while yielding more than `11%` improvement.
- Adaptive generation behaves as intended: with `\hat{m} = 1`, average generated passages are `0.69`; with `\hat{m} = 2`, they rise to `1.24` and overall accuracy improves from `61.71` to `62.00`.
- Source-awareness matters: on Gemini, Astute RAG scores `59.2` overall versus `58.6` without source labels.

## Limitations

- The method depends on strong instruction-following and reasoning ability from advanced LLMs; weaker models may not consolidate conflicting evidence reliably.
- Experiments focus on relatively short retrieved contexts; the paper does not test whether the same prompting strategy scales to much longer inputs or larger evidence sets.
- Astute RAG is still an inference-time method that increases API calls and token usage, even if the overhead is modest.
- The method improves the post-retrieval stage only; it does not fix upstream retrieval quality, corpus quality, or poisoning vulnerabilities at the retriever level.
