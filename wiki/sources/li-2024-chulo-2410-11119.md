## Summary

ChuLo proposes a chunk-level representation pipeline for long document understanding that preserves full-document coverage while reducing the sequence length seen by the downstream transformer. The method first splits a document into fixed-size non-overlapping chunks, then extracts document-level keyphrases with a PromptRank-inspired Semantic Keyphrase Prioritization algorithm, and finally builds each chunk embedding as a weighted average that upweights keyphrase tokens. These chunk embeddings are processed by a BERT-based chunk attention module for document classification and by a BERT-decoder variant for token classification. Across HP, LUN, Eurlex57K, Inverted Eurlex57K, CoNLL-2012, and GUM, the paper reports consistent gains over truncation, sparse-attention, and prior chunking baselines, with especially large improvements on long-input settings.

## Problem & Motivation

Transformer models handle long-range dependencies well, but standard self-attention scales quadratically with sequence length, making unrestricted long documents expensive to encode directly. Common workarounds such as truncation, sparse attention, and naive chunking either drop information or weaken cross-document context, which is especially harmful for token-level tasks where every token may need full-document evidence. The paper targets a middle ground: preserve all tokens, compress each chunk into a compact representation, and bias that representation toward semantically central phrases so the downstream model sees less noise and less information loss.

## Method

- **Document chunking**: tokenize document `D = (t_0, t_1, ..., t_{l_D-1})`, split it into non-overlapping fixed-length chunks `C_D = (C_0, ..., C_{m-1})`, with `m = ceil(l_D / n)` and padding for the last chunk; `n` is a task-dependent hyperparameter.
- **Semantic Keyphrase Prioritization (SKP)**: extract candidate phrases with POS-pattern matching, segment the document to fit the encoder, score each candidate via a PromptRank-style prompted probability and a positional penalty, then sort by `s_i = r_i * Σ_j p_{ij}` and keep top-`n = 15` phrases.
- **Prompt-based phrase scoring**: for candidate phrase `k_i`, build prompt `"The * mainly discusses k_i"` and compute `p_{ij} = (1 / l_P^α) * Σ_g log p(t_g | t_{<g})` over each document segment before aggregating across segments.
- **Chunk representation**: mark keyphrase tokens as `T_k` and others as `T_nk`, then compute chunk embedding `c = (Σ_t w_t * t) / (Σ_t w_t)` with `w_t = a` for `T_k` and `w_t = b` for `T_nk`, where `a > b` emphasizes key content.
- **Backbone and training**: use BERT-base as the chunk attention backbone, initialize a Transformer-based chunk attention module, and attach classification heads for document tasks plus a BERT-decoder module for token classification.
- **Optimization**: train with AdamW, early stopping patience `10`, maximum `100` epochs, learning-rate search per experiment, and `top-n = 15` keyphrases on all datasets.
- **Dataset-specific hyperparameters**: chunk size `n` is `10` on HP, `50` on LUN, `5` on EURLEX57K and Inverted EURLEX57K, `20` on CoNLL, and `50` on GUM; keyphrase-token weight `a` is `0.8` except `0.5` on LUN, while non-keyphrase weight `b = 0.1`.
- **Warm-up and batching**: learning rate is `5e-5` throughout; warm-up is linear with `10%` steps for HP, LUN, CoNLL, and GUM, cosine with `5%` steps for EURLEX variants; batch sizes range from `2` on CoNLL to `32` on LUN.

## Key Results

- Document classification: ChuLo reaches `0.9538` on HP, `0.6440` on LUN, `0.7332` micro-F1 on EURLEX57K, and `0.7244` micro-F1 on Inverted EURLEX57K.
- On LUN, ChuLo beats the next-best reported baseline (BERT at `0.5797`) by `0.0643` absolute accuracy and outperforms Longformer (`0.5552`) despite using chunk embeddings instead of raw `4096`-token inputs.
- On longer LUN documents, ChuLo scores `0.5911` for inputs `>1024` tokens and `0.7959` for inputs `>2048`, exceeding Longformer (`0.4062`, `0.5306`), GPT-4o (`0.7143` at `>2048`), and Gemini 1.5 Pro (`0.6531` at `>2048`).
- On token classification, ChuLo reports `0.9334` micro-F1 on CoNLL-2012 and `0.9555` on GUM, compared with Longformer at `0.5560` and `0.9427`, and BigBird at `0.5553` and `0.9418`.
- Under very long CoNLL inputs, ChuLo remains stable: `0.9325` for documents `>2048`, `0.9287` for `>4096`, and `0.9206` for `>8192`, whereas Longformer drops to `0.5268`, `0.3156`, and `0.3116`.
- Ablations show PromptRank-style keyphrase extraction outperforms YAKE on LUN (`0.6440` vs `0.5951`), sentence embeddings hurt performance on both HP and LUN, and BERT is a better chunk-attention backbone than RoBERTa or Longformer.

## Limitations

- The method still depends on a separate keyphrase extraction stage and several tuned hyperparameters (`chunk size`, token weights, warm-up schedule), so the pipeline is more complex than plain fine-tuning.
- Evaluation is limited to classification-style long-document tasks; the paper does not test generation, retrieval, or broader multimodal long-context settings.
- Comparisons against GPT-4o and Gemini 1.5 Pro are zero-shot prompt-based, so they are not strictly matched to the fine-tuned supervised baselines.
- The strongest gains come from benchmark suites with relatively small test sets in some splits, such as HP and the long-document subsets, which limits confidence about robustness across domains.
