## Summary

MV-Adapter studies parameter-efficient video-text retrieval by freezing a pretrained CLIP dual encoder and learning only small multimodal adaptation branches. The method adds bottleneck-style video and text adapters after transformer FFNs, then specializes the video side with a temporal adaptation module that models frame sequences and dynamically calibrates frame-wise upsampling weights. A cross modality tying module further couples the video and text branches through a shared factor space so that both modalities remain better aligned during transfer. With only about `2.4%` extra trainable parameters per task, MV-Adapter matches or exceeds full fine-tuning on five benchmarks, including clear gains on MSR-VTT, LSMDC, and ActivityNet, while substantially reducing deployment storage and training memory.

## Problem & Motivation

Prior CLIP-based video-text retrieval systems usually fully fine-tune the entire pretrained model for each dataset. That works, but it creates large storage overhead because every downstream task requires a separate full model copy, and it can be unstable on relatively small retrieval datasets. The paper therefore formulates parameter-efficient video-text retrieval (PE-VTR): keep most CLIP weights frozen, adapt image-text pretraining to video-text retrieval, preserve cross-modal alignment, and still learn temporal video structure with negligible parameter overhead.

## Method

- **CLIP transfer setup**: use a CLIP dual encoder with vision encoder `E_V` and text encoder `E_T`; sample frames `(I_1, ..., I_|v|)`, encode frame-level `[CLS]` features, mean-pool them into `e_v = (1 / |v|) \sum_k E_V(I_k)`, encode text as `e_t = E_T(t)`, and rank by cosine similarity `` `sim(v, t) = (e_v^T e_t) / (||e_v|| ||e_t||)` ``.
- **Basic adapter branch**: place a bottleneck branch after each transformer FFN in both modalities with `` `A_basic(x) = s · TRM(x W_down) W_up` ``, where `` `W_down ∈ R^(d × d')` ``, `` `W_up ∈ R^(d' × d)` ``, `TRM` is a lightweight transformer, and the adapted output is added back to the frozen backbone.
- **Temporal adaptation**: in the video branch, downsample frame `[CLS]` and patch tokens, aggregate a temporal sequence with a learnable `[CC]` token, run a lightweight transformer over frames, then calibrate per-frame upsampling weights from global and local temporal context.
- **Temporal calibration MLP**: for frame `i`, form `` `α^i = concat(x_[CC], x_[CLS]^i + x_patch^i_bar)` `` and compute `` `α_cal^i = FC2(ReLU(FC1(α^i)))` `` with layer sizes `` `2d' -> d'/σ -> d'` ``; use `` `(W_up-cal^i)_c = α_cal^i ⊙ (W_up)_c` `` so each frame receives dynamic upsample weights.
- **Cross modality tying (CMT)**: share a factor `` `f_C ∈ R^(1 × d^m)` `` between video and text branches, project it with modality-specific matrices `` `M_S^(T/V)` ``, and calibrate downsample weights via `` `(W_down-cal^(T/V))_c = β_cal^(T/V) ⊙ (W_down)_c` `` so the two modalities stay better aligned in a shared embedding space.
- **Complexity and efficiency**: the added tunable parameter complexity is dominated by `` `O(2 d d' + k (d')^2)` ``, enabling reuse of one shared pretrained model plus small task-specific adapters instead of storing a full fine-tuned model per dataset.
- **Implementation details**: default backbone is CLIP `ViT-B/16`; use Adam with learning rate `5e-6`, middle dimension `d' = 64`, shrinkage factor `` `σ = 4` ``, scaling factor `` `s = 0.1` ``, `12` video frames and `32` caption tokens for MSR-VTT/MSVD/LSMDC, `32` frames and `64` tokens for DiDemo/ActivityNet, `5` epochs on shorter datasets, `15` epochs on longer datasets, and batch size `128` except `64` on DiDemo/ActivityNet.

## Key Results

- **MSR-VTT**: with `2.39%` tunable parameters, MV-Adapter reaches text-to-video `R@Sum = 202.1` and video-to-text `R@Sum = 205.9`, versus full fine-tuning at `200.2` and `202.4`.
- **MSVD**: MV-Adapter gets text-to-video `214.8` and video-to-text `261.2` `R@Sum`, essentially matching or slightly improving on full fine-tuning (`216.2` / `259.3`) with the same `2.39%` trainable-parameter budget.
- **LSMDC**: MV-Adapter substantially beats full fine-tuning, improving text-to-video `R@Sum` from `112.9` to `120.3` and video-to-text from `113.2` to `118.8` with `2.42%` tunable parameters.
- **DiDemo and ActivityNet**: performance remains close to or better than full fine-tuning; on ActivityNet, MV-Adapter reaches text-to-video `203.1` vs. `201.5`, while video-to-text is essentially tied (`205.2` vs. `205.3`).
- **Ablations on MSR-VTT**: adding temporal modeling lifts T2V/V2T `R@1` from `42.9/43.4` to `45.0/46.0`; adding temporal calibration further improves to `45.9/46.6`; enabling CMT raises `R@Sum` from `200.9/204.4` to `202.1/205.9`.
- **Efficiency**: supporting five tasks needs about `112%` of the pretrained model's storage, compared with `500%` for full fine-tuning, and training uses roughly `60%` of the GPU memory of full fine-tuning.

## Limitations

- The model uses a fixed number of uniformly sampled frames, so it can miss fine-grained motions in long videos when critical events fall between sampled frames.
- The retrieval model does not use audio, and the authors explicitly note failures when captions depend on spoken or acoustic content.
- Gains over full fine-tuning are not uniform on every benchmark; on DiDemo and parts of MSVD/ActivityNet the method is closer to parity than to a large improvement.
- The approach is designed around CLIP-like frozen dual encoders and retrieval objectives, so its applicability to stronger cross-encoder or generative multimodal settings is not demonstrated.
