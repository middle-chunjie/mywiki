## Summary

Syntriever proposes a two-stage recipe for training dense retrievers from black-box LLM outputs without requiring teacher token probabilities. In stage 1, the model distills LLM knowledge through synthetic chain-of-thought query expansions, synthetic positive passages, synthetic hard negatives, and a relabeling step that turns hallucinated positives into useful negatives. It then optimizes a modified soft-nearest-neighbor objective that clusters multiple relevant views of each query. In stage 2, the retriever is aligned to LLM passage preferences via pairwise comparisons over top-`K` retrieved passages and a partial Plackett-Luce objective that preserves separation from in-batch negatives. Across BeIR-style benchmarks, the method consistently improves 125M-scale retrievers and establishes new best results on many settings.

## Problem & Motivation

Retriever distillation from LLMs had largely depended on teacher-side probabilities, as in RePlug-style approaches, but modern black-box APIs often expose only generated text. The paper asks whether a retriever can still absorb LLM knowledge using synthetic passages and preference judgments alone. This matters for retrieval-augmented generation because retriever quality directly affects factuality and coverage, yet supervised labels are scarce and standard contrastive training underuses the richer query decompositions, alternative contexts, and fine-grained ranking preferences that strong LLMs can provide.

## Method

- **Retrieval setup**: the retriever scores a query-passage pair with `s_tau(q, p) = sim(E(q), E(p)) / tau`, where `sim` is cosine similarity and `tau = 0.05`.
- **Stage 1, query augmentation**: for each query `q_i`, an LLM generates a chain-of-thought expansion `q_i^cot = M(P_cot(q_i))`; the resulting sub-query sequence is treated as an additional positive view.
- **Stage 1, synthetic passages**: the LLM generates a synthetic positive `p_i^+ = M(P_+(q_i))` and a plausible but irrelevant hard negative `p_i^- = M(P_-(q_i))`, alongside the dataset's labeled positive `p_i`.
- **Self-verification and relabeling**: the LLM re-checks each synthetic positive with `hat(p_i) = M(P_Relabel(q_i, p_i^+))`; if the passage is judged hallucinated, Syntriever reuses it as another hard negative instead of discarding it.
- **Distillation loss**: Syntriever replaces single-positive InfoNCE with a modified soft-nearest-neighbor objective `L_distill(q_i)` that attracts `p_i`, `p_i^+`, and `q_i^cot` toward `q_i`, while repelling in-batch negatives and synthetic hard negatives `p_j^-`.
- **Stage 2, preference alignment**: after distillation, the retriever retrieves top-`K` passages `hat(C_i) = Retrieval(q_i, Z, K, E_hat)` with default `K = 5`; it samples `N = K(K-1)/2 = 10` passage pairs and queries the LLM for preferences `(c_i^+, c_i^-)`.
- **Partial Plackett-Luce ranking**: the alignment loss `L_align(q_i)` models `c_i^+ ≻ c_i^- ≻` in-batch negatives by marginalizing a Plackett-Luce ranking distribution, which regularizes preference learning with the same contrastive structure used during distillation.
- **Implementation details**: the default base encoder is E5 at roughly `125M` parameters; optimization uses Adam with learning rate `2e-5`, cosine schedule, `1000` warmup steps, weight decay `0.01`, batch size `60` in stage 1 and `100` in stage 2, on a single `A100 80GB`.

## Key Results

- **Supervised BeIR fine-tuning**: Syntriever reaches `50.3` nDCG@10 on MSMARCO, `70.2` on HotpotQA, `41.9` on FiQA, `60.3` on FEVER, `80.5` on SciFact, `43.3` on NFCorpus, and `52.1` on NQ.
- **Relative gains over the runner-up**: the paper reports improvements of up to `18.6%` on MSMARCO, `8.3%` on NFCorpus, `5.9%` on HotpotQA, `4.0%` on NQ, `2.5%` on FiQA, and `1.8%` on SciFact.
- **Zero-shot transfer**: Syntriever is best on `8/15` zero-shot datasets and second-best on `1/15`, including `75.3` on TREC-COVID, `88.9` on Quora, and `62.2` on NQ.
- **Ablation**: adding all three components (`q^cot`, synthetic `p^{+,-}`, and alignment) improves nDCG@10 from `42.4 -> 50.3` on MSMARCO, `63.4 -> 70.2` on HotpotQA, `37.9 -> 41.9` on FiQA, `73.7 -> 80.5` on SciFact, and `35.8 -> 43.3` on NFCorpus.
- **Self-verification matters**: relabeling hallucinated positives improves HotpotQA `67.4 -> 70.2`, FiQA `40.8 -> 41.9`, and SciFact `79.7 -> 80.5`.
- **Preference model comparison**: partial Plackett-Luce clearly beats Bradley-Terry on harder datasets, e.g. FiQA `41.9 vs. 35.7` and NFCorpus `43.3 vs. 36.1`, while staying competitive on SciFact (`80.5 vs. 79.8`).

## Limitations

- The method requires substantial black-box LLM inference for both synthetic data generation and pairwise alignment feedback, so training cost is higher than ordinary retriever fine-tuning.
- The main experiments focus on `125M`-scale encoders and BeIR-style benchmarks; the paper does not establish how the recipe scales to larger retrievers, different corpora sizes, or multilingual settings.
- Alignment cost grows with the number of retrieved passages and sampled passage pairs; the paper observes a trade-off between larger `K` and computational overhead.
- Synthetic relevance remains hard to measure precisely: even after self-verification, positive passages can still be indirect or noisy, and the gains from relabeling vary noticeably by dataset.
