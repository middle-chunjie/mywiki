## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2021; re-verify against recent literature.

RocketQA studies dense passage retrieval for open-domain question answering and argues that standard dual-encoder training underuses negatives, mislabels many hard negatives, and lacks enough labeled questions. The method preserves efficient independent question and passage encoders, but strengthens training with three additions: cross-batch negatives to enlarge the candidate set, a cross-encoder to denoise top-ranked hard negatives, and pseudo-labeled unlabeled questions for data augmentation. These components are organized into a four-step pipeline that alternates dual-encoder and cross-encoder training. On MS MARCO, RocketQA reaches `37.0` MRR@10 and `97.9` R@1000; on Natural Questions it reaches `74.0` R@5 and `88.5` R@100, while also improving end-to-end QA exact match to `42.8` with a retrained reader.

## Problem & Motivation

Dense passage retrieval had become a strong alternative to sparse term matching for open-domain QA, but plain dual-encoder training still diverged from deployment conditions. Training usually saw only a small in-batch candidate set instead of millions of passages, top-ranked hard negatives often contained unlabeled positives, and available supervised QA datasets were still limited relative to real search traffic. RocketQA targets these three bottlenecks jointly so that a dual-encoder retriever can remain efficient at inference time while learning from more negatives, cleaner negatives, and more training questions.

## Method

- **Dual-encoder retriever**: learn separate question and passage encoders `E_q(.)` and `E_p(.)`, score candidates with `sim(q, p) = E_q(q) · E_p(p)`, and use the first token representation (for example `[CLS]`) as the embedding.
- **Training objective**: optimize the positive passage against `m` negatives with `L(q_i, p_i^+, {p_i,j^-}) = -log(exp(sim(q_i, p_i^+)) / (exp(sim(q_i, p_i^+)) + Σ_j exp(sim(q_i, p_i,j^-))))`.
- **Cross-batch negatives**: if training uses `A` GPUs and `B` questions per GPU, each question can reuse positives from all devices and see `A × B - 1` negatives instead of only `B - 1`; the implementation uses FleetX differentiable all-gather.
- **Denoised hard negatives**: retrieve top passages with `M_D^(0)`, rescore them with a cross-encoder `M_C`, and keep only passages predicted negative with high confidence; in implementation, cross-encoder scores `< 0.1` are treated as negatives.
- **Data augmentation**: use the same cross-encoder to pseudo-label unlabeled questions `Q_U`, keep passages scored `> 0.9` as positives and `< 0.1` as negatives, then train `M_D^(2)` on both `D_L` and pseudo-labeled `D_U`.
- **Four-step pipeline**: `STEP 1` train `M_D^(0)` with cross-batch negatives; `STEP 2` train `M_C` on labeled questions plus top-`k` negatives from `M_D^(0)`; `STEP 3` train `M_D^(1)` with denoised hard negatives; `STEP 4` train `M_D^(2)` with augmented pseudo-labeled data.
- **Modeling and retrieval stack**: the dual-encoder is initialized from `ERNIE 2.0 base`, the cross-encoder from `ERNIE 2.0 large`, and passage search uses FAISS `IndexFlatIP` for exact maximum inner-product retrieval.
- **Key hyperparameters**: dual-encoder learning rate `3e-5`, cross-encoder learning rate `1e-5`, warmup ratio `0.1`, max question length `32`, max passage length `128`, and dual-encoder epochs `40/10/10` on MS MARCO and `30/30/30` on NQ across the main stages.
- **Training ratios and hardware**: cross-encoder positive:negative ratios are `1:4` on MS MARCO and `1:1` on NQ; dual-encoder hard-negative ratios in later stages are also `1:4` and `1:1`; experiments run on up to `8` NVIDIA Tesla V100 GPUs with PaddlePaddle.

## Key Results

- **MS MARCO retrieval**: RocketQA achieves `37.0` MRR@10, `85.5` R@50, and `97.9` R@1000, outperforming ANCE (`33.0` MRR@10, `95.9` R@1000), ME-BERT (`33.8` MRR@10), and docTTTTTquery (`27.7` MRR@10, `94.7` R@1000).
- **Natural Questions retrieval**: RocketQA reaches `74.0` R@5, `82.7` R@20, and `88.5` R@100, beating DPR on reported recall (`78.4` R@20, `85.4` R@100) and slightly exceeding ANCE at `R@100` (`87.5`).
- **Ablation on MS MARCO**: in-batch negatives score `32.39` MRR@10, cross-batch negatives raise this to `33.32`, naive hard negatives collapse to `26.03`, denoised hard negatives recover to `36.38`, and adding augmentation yields `37.02`.
- **Noise analysis**: manual inspection of top retrieved unlabeled passages finds about `70%` are actually positives or highly relevant, motivating denoising; the selected pseudo labels exceed `90%` accuracy in manual evaluation.
- **End-to-end QA**: on NQ, RocketQA plus the released DPR reader reaches `42.0` EM, and RocketQA plus a retrained DPR reader reaches `42.8` EM, compared with DPR's `41.5`.

## Limitations

- The method is substantially more complex than a plain dual-encoder baseline because it requires alternating training of `M_D^(0)`, `M_C`, `M_D^(1)`, and `M_D^(2)` plus pseudo-label generation.
- Denoising and augmentation depend on cross-encoder score calibration; thresholds like `< 0.1` and `> 0.9` are heuristic and validated only by limited manual checking.
- Experiments are restricted to two English open-domain QA benchmarks, so it is unclear how well the recipe transfers to multilingual retrieval, other domains, or non-QA dense retrieval tasks.
- The paper uses 2021-era encoder PLMs and exact inner-product retrieval; it does not study later retrieval paradigms such as late interaction, instruction-tuned retrievers, or generative retriever-reader coupling.
