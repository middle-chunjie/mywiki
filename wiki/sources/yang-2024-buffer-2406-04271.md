## Summary

The paper proposes Buffer of Thoughts (BoT), a prompting-time reasoning framework that stores reusable high-level solution patterns as thought templates in a meta-buffer rather than rebuilding a reasoning strategy for each query. BoT first runs a problem distiller to extract key variables, constraints, and a normalized task description, then retrieves the most relevant template by embedding similarity, instantiates that template into a task-specific reasoning program or procedure, and finally updates the meta-buffer through a buffer manager that distills reusable templates from solved examples. Across 10 reasoning-intensive tasks, the method improves both accuracy and stability over single-query and multi-query baselines while reducing the average cost of multi-query prompting to roughly `12%`.

## Problem & Motivation

Existing LLM reasoning methods face a trade-off between generality and cost. Single-query prompting methods such as CoT or few-shot prompting often depend on manually designed exemplars or assumptions about the desired reasoning trace, which limits transfer across tasks. Multi-query methods such as ToT and GoT can explore richer reasoning structures, but they are computationally expensive because they recursively expand and evaluate multiple candidate paths. The paper argues that both families underuse reusable high-level structure from past solved tasks. BoT targets this gap by storing abstract thought templates that can be retrieved and instantiated for analogous future problems, with the goal of improving accuracy, efficiency, and robustness simultaneously.

## Method

- **Overall pipeline**: given an input task `x`, BoT applies a problem distiller, retrieves a high-level template from a meta-buffer, instantiates it into a task-specific solution process, and then optionally updates the buffer with a newly distilled template.
- **Problem distiller**: BoT prepends a meta prompt `φ` and computes distilled task information as `x_d = LLM(φ(x))`. The distiller extracts key variables, objectives, and constraints, then rewrites the original problem into a clearer normalized form for downstream reasoning.
- **Abstraction step**: the distiller explicitly translates concrete scenarios into higher-level structures, such as recasting word problems into simpler multi-step calculations or state-update procedures, to make template retrieval easier.
- **Meta-buffer design**: the meta-buffer stores tuples `(T_i, D_{T_i}, C_k)` where `T_i` is a thought template, `D_{T_i}` its description, and `C_k` one of six template categories: text comprehension, creative language generation, commonsense reasoning, mathematical reasoning, code programming, or application scheduling.
- **Template retrieval**: for a distilled task `x_d`, BoT selects template index `j = argmax_i Sim(f(x_d), {f(D_{T_i})})` subject to a similarity threshold `δ`. The paper recommends `δ = 0.5-0.7`; if no template clears the threshold, the task is treated as new.
- **Instantiated reasoning**: when a template is retrieved, the instantiated reasoner solves the task with `S_x = LLM_instantiation(x_d, T_j)`. The appendix defines three reasoning structures: prompt-based, procedure-based, and programming-based, chosen according to task type.
- **Fallback for unseen tasks**: if retrieval fails, BoT falls back to three predefined coarse-grained templates so the model can still run instantiated reasoning instead of reverting to an unconstrained prompt.
- **Buffer manager**: after solving a task, the system distills a reusable template `T_new = LLM_distill(x_d, S_x)` using a three-part prompt covering core task summarization, solution-step description, and a general answer template.
- **Distillation supervision**: the buffer manager uses both in-task and cross-task in-context exemplars so template distillation generalizes beyond one benchmark or surface form.
- **Dynamic update rule**: BoT only inserts a new template if `max Sim(f(D_{T_new}), {f(D_{T_i})}) < δ`, which is intended to keep the meta-buffer lightweight while still absorbing novel reasoning patterns.
- **Experimental setup**: the main experiments use `GPT-4` as the base model and compare against standard prompting, single-query baselines (`CoT`, Expert Prompting, `PAL`), and multi-query baselines (`ToT`, `GoT`, Meta Prompting) on `10` reasoning-intensive tasks.

## Key Results

- On Game of 24, BoT reaches `82.4`, beating `ToT` (`74.0`), `GoT` (`73.2`), and Meta Prompting (`67.0`).
- On Geometric Shapes, BoT scores `93.6`, compared with Meta Prompting at `78.2` and `CoT` at `69.2`, a reported `20%` gain over Meta Prompting.
- On Checkmate-in-One, BoT reaches `86.4`, versus `57.2` for Meta Prompting and `51.4` for GoT, a reported `51%` gain over Meta Prompting.
- On Multi-Step Arithmetic and Word Sorting, BoT achieves `99.8` and `100.0`, respectively, the best scores in the comparison table.
- On MGSM, Penguins, and Date Understanding, BoT obtains `89.2`, `94.7`, and `88.2`, consistently outperforming the strongest baselines listed.
- The paper states that BoT uses only `12%` of the average cost of multi-query prompting methods such as tree/graph-based search and meta-prompting.
- In the robustness evaluation, BoT improves average success rate by roughly `10%` over the second-best method across repeated subsamples of `1000` examples.
- In model-size analysis, `Llama3-8B + BoT` is reported to potentially surpass a plain `Llama3-70B` baseline on some difficult tasks.

## Limitations

- The method is less effective on tasks requiring open-ended human-like creativity, where fixed high-level templates may be less informative.
- Template quality depends on the model used to initialize and expand the meta-buffer; weaker models may distill lower-quality templates.
- The retrieval subsystem is described at a high level with embedding similarity, but the paper does not give much implementation detail about the embedding model, indexing strategy, or sensitivity to retrieval errors.
- Evaluation focuses on prompt-level reasoning benchmarks rather than interactive agent settings with external tools, long-horizon planning, or real-world retrieval noise.
- The framework introduces several prompt stages and a persistent template store, so operational simplicity is lower than plain single-query prompting even if inference cost is reduced relative to multi-query search.
