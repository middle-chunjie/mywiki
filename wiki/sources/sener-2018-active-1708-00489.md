## Summary

The paper argues that batch active learning for CNNs should be treated as a geometric coverage problem rather than an uncertainty-ranking problem. Sener and Savarese define active learning as unlabeled core-set selection, derive a bound that links population loss to the labeled subset's covering radius, and show that minimizing this surrogate reduces to a `k`-center objective over deep feature embeddings. They pair a greedy `2`-approximation with a robust mixed-integer refinement that allows a small outlier budget. Experiments on CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and SVHN, in both fully supervised and weakly supervised settings, show consistent gains over random sampling, uncertainty-based baselines, DBAL, BMDR, and clustering baselines, especially when the feature space is improved by semi-supervised training.

## Problem & Motivation

The paper studies pool-based active learning for CNN image classifiers under realistic batch budgets. Classical active learning heuristics often assume one-point-at-a-time acquisition, but that is impractical for deep networks because single queried examples have negligible effect and each round requires expensive retraining. The authors argue that batch acquisition makes uncertainty-based selection correlated and redundant, so the real objective is to choose a labeled subset whose geometry represents the unlabeled pool well enough that a model trained on it generalizes to the whole dataset.

## Method

- **Batch active learning objective**: given an initial labeled pool `s^0` and budget `b`, choose `s^1` to minimize `E_{(x,y) ~ p_Z}[l(x, y; A_{s^0 U s^1})]`, then repeat myopically over rounds.
- **Risk decomposition**: the population loss is upper-bounded by training error, generalization error, and a core-set discrepancy term measuring the gap between empirical loss on the selected subset and empirical loss on the full dataset.
- **Core-set reformulation**: they redefine active learning as minimizing `|(1/n) Σ_i l(x_i, y_i; A_s) - (1/|s|) Σ_{j in s} l(x_j, y_j; A_s)|`, where `s = s^0 U s^1`.
- **Theorem 1**: if the selected set is a `δ_s`-cover, the loss is bounded by `δ_s (λ^l + λ^η L C) + sqrt(L^2 log(1/γ) / (2n))` under Lipschitz and bounded-loss assumptions plus zero training error on selected points.
- **CNN-specific lemma**: for a CNN with `n_c` convolutional layers, `n_fc` fully connected layers, ReLU/max-pool nonlinearities, and `C` classes, the `l_2` loss to the softmax output is `(sqrt(C - 1) / C) α^(n_c + n_fc)`-Lipschitz with respect to the input.
- **Optimization target**: minimizing the covering radius becomes the `k`-center problem `min_{|s^1| <= b} max_i min_{j in s^0 U s^1} Δ(x_i, x_j)`, where `Δ` is the `l_2` distance between activations of the final fully connected layer.
- **Greedy solver**: Algorithm 1 iteratively adds the unlabeled point farthest from the current selected set, yielding a `2`-approximation to the optimal `k`-center solution.
- **Robust refinement via MIP**: Algorithm 2 binary-searches over `δ` and checks feasibility with a mixed-integer program that uses binary center variables `u_i`, assignment variables `ω_{i,j}`, and outlier indicators `ξ_{i,j}` with outlier budget `Ξ = 1e-4 * n`.
- **Implementation details**: the experiments use [[vgg-16]] features, [[ladder-networks]] in the weakly supervised regime, He initialization, RMSProp with learning rate `1e-3`, [[tensorflow]] training, and retraining from scratch after each acquisition round.

## Key Results

- Evaluated on `3` classification tasks: CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and SVHN, covering both fully supervised and weakly supervised active learning.
- Each experiment averages over `5` random initial labeled pools, and the proposed method beats all reported baselines across the plotted accuracy-vs-label-budget curves; the margin is especially large in the weakly supervised setting.
- The authors note the method is weaker on `CIFAR-100` than on `CIFAR-10` and `SVHN`, consistent with their bound scaling with the number of classes `C`.
- In the qualitative t-SNE analysis, the visualization starts from `1000` labeled images and queries another `1000`; the proposed method spreads selected points across the feature space, while uncertainty-based querying leaves large regions uncovered.
- For a `50k`-image problem with `|s^0| = 10k` and `b = 5k`, the reported runtime is `104.2 s` to build the distance matrix, `2 s` for greedy `2`-OPT, `7.5 s` per MIP iteration, `244.03 s` total MIP time, and `360.23 s` end-to-end.
- Solving the refined MIP gives a small but meaningful accuracy improvement over the plain greedy `2`-OPT solution, while greedy alone still remains better than the competing baselines.

## Limitations

- The theory assumes zero training error on the selected subset, Lipschitz regression functions, and an `l_2` loss to softmax outputs; the actual experiments use cross-entropy, so the guarantee does not directly cover the trained objective.
- The method is geometric and label-agnostic after feature extraction, so it does not incorporate uncertainty in a principled way and may miss genuinely ambiguous but well-covered points.
- The optimal refinement requires solving a mixed-integer program and retraining CNNs from scratch each round, which increases computational cost despite the practical runtimes reported.
- Experimental evidence is restricted to image classification, and the extracted markdown does not include tabulated accuracy numbers for the main curves, only qualitative and runtime summaries.
