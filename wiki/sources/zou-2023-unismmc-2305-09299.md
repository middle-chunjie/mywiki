## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature in multimodal learning.

UniS-MMC proposes a multimodal contrastive learning framework for image-text classification that addresses a key weakness of prior methods: treating all modalities as equally reliable. The method first runs auxiliary unimodal prediction tasks to generate weak supervision labels indicating per-sample modality effectiveness. These labels redefine contrastive pairs into three categories — positive (both modalities correct), semi-positive (one correct), and negative (both wrong) — and the contrastive loss aligns ineffective unimodal representations toward effective ones. Combined with early-fusion aggregation, UniS-MMC achieves 94.7% accuracy on UPMC-Food-101 and consistently best results across all text sources on N24News, outperforming both supervised and unsupervised multimodal contrastive baselines.

## Problem & Motivation

Aggregation-based multimodal fusion (concatenation, early/late fusion) ignores inter-modality relationships and treats every modality equally regardless of sample-level noise or quality. Existing contrastive-based alignment methods (unsupervised and supervised) improve cross-modal correspondence but still assign identical roles to both modalities, suffer from sensor/label noise in in-the-wild datasets, and risk collapsing complementary multimodal information into a single shared space. The question is how to leverage modality quality differences within the contrastive framework to produce more trustworthy multimodal representations.

## Method

- **Architecture overview**: Two unimodal encoders `f_{θ_a}` and `f_{θ_b}` extract representations `r_a` and `r_b`. Parameter sharing between unimodal and multimodal classification branches follows a multi-task learning (MTL) framework.
- **Modality encoders**: Pretrained ViT-Base for images; pretrained BERT-Base or RoBERTa-Base for text (both datasets). Classifiers are 3-layer fully-connected networks with ReLU activations.
- **Unimodality check**: Each modality's representation is passed to its own unimodal classifier `g_{φ_m}` to produce unimodal prediction `ŷ_m`. If `ŷ_m == y`, the modality is "effective"; otherwise "ineffective".
- **Contrastive pair taxonomy** (Table 1):
  - Positive pair `P`: both `ŷ_a == y` and `ŷ_b == y` → pull closer, mutual update
  - Negative pair `N`: both `ŷ_a ≠ y` and `ŷ_b ≠ y` → push apart (encourages complementary information)
  - Semi-positive pair `S`: exactly one correct → align the ineffective representation toward the effective one; **gradient for the effective-side representation is detached** from the semi-positive update (GAN-discriminator-style detach)
- **Bimodal contrastive loss** (Eq. 2):
  `L_{b-mmc} = -log[ Σ_{n ∈ P,S} exp(cos(r_a^n, r_b^n)/τ) / Σ_{n ∈ B} exp(cos(r_a^n, r_b^n)/τ) ]`
  where cosine similarity is `cos(r_a^n, r_b^n) = (r_a^n · r_b^n) / (‖r_a^n‖ · ‖r_b^n‖)`, temperature `τ = 0.07`.
- **Multi-modal extension** (Eq. 3): `L_{mmc} = Σ_i Σ_{j>i} L_{b-mmc}(m_i, m_j)`
- **Total objective** (Eq. 5): `L_{UniS-MMC} = L_{uni} + L_{multi} + λ·L_{mmc}`, with loss coefficient `λ = 0.1`.
- **Fusion**: concatenation `r_c = r_1 ⊕ r_2 ⊕ ... ⊕ r_m`; multimodal classifier then predicts from `r_c`.
- **Hyperparameters**: batch size 32, batch gradient 128, Adam optimizer with ReduceLROnPlateau; learning rate `2e-5` (Food-101) / `1e-4` (N24News); weight decay `1e-4`; trained on V100/A100 GPUs.

## Key Results

- **UPMC-Food-101**: UniS-MMC achieves **94.7% ± 0.1** accuracy (ViT + BERT), surpassing SupMMC (94.2%), UnSupMMC (94.1%), ME (94.6%), and AggMM (93.7%).
- **N24News (BERT)**: Headline 80.2%, Caption 77.5%, Abstract 83.2% — best on all three text sources, +1.6/+0.7/+2.4 percentage points over AggMM.
- **N24News (RoBERTa)**: Headline 80.3%, Caption 78.1%, Abstract 84.2% — also best on all three, +1.4/+0.3/+0.7 percentage points.
- **Ablation** (Table 4): Adding `L_uni` alone gives +0.8/+0.5/+1.7 pp (BERT, headline/caption/abstract); adding `C_Semi` on top gives further improvement; `C_Neg` adds the final increment. All three components jointly explain the final gain.
- **Unimodal vs. multimodal** (Table 3): Image-only 54.1% (N24News), text-only 72.1%–78.3%; all multimodal configurations substantially exceed both unimodal baselines.
- **t-SNE visualization**: UniS-MMC preserves bimodal sub-cluster structure (two distribution clouds per class) while sharpening class boundaries, unlike SupMMC/UnSupMMC which collapse both modalities into a single cloud and create inter-class contamination.

## Limitations

- Applicable primarily to tasks that rely on the quantity and quality of joint multimodal representations (e.g., classification). The paper explicitly notes it is **not suitable for cross-modal retrieval**, which requires direct cross-modal correspondence rather than effectiveness-guided alignment.
- The contrastive pair assignment depends entirely on per-sample unimodal prediction accuracy, which may be unreliable early in training or in highly imbalanced settings.
- Evaluated only on image-text datasets; audio, video, and other modality combinations are not explored.
- The gradient detachment for semi-positive pairs borrows GAN intuition but is not theoretically justified in the contrastive learning literature.
