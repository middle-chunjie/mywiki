## Summary

MPO proposes a portable way to improve LLM-agent planning without retraining every downstream agent. The core idea is to train a lightweight meta planner that emits abstract meta plans, then optimize those plans using downstream task feedback rather than human preference labels or manually written procedures. Starting from GPT-4o-generated seed plans distilled from expert trajectories, MPO scores each sampled plan by the success rate of an agent executing tasks under that plan, uses the best-vs-worst sampled plans as DPO preferences, and learns a stronger planner. Across ScienceWorld, ALFWorld, and WebShop, the resulting meta plans improve average reward and success rate for both untrained and already-trained agents, while also improving transfer to unseen scenarios and larger models.

## Problem & Motivation

LLM agents often rely on implicit planning during action generation, which makes them vulnerable to planning hallucinations and brittle long-horizon behavior. Existing fixes either retrain the agent for each framework and backbone, or inject explicit knowledge that is expensive to author and hard to quality-control. The paper targets a middle ground: reusable high-level guidance that is abstract enough to transfer across tasks and agents, but still optimizable from interaction outcomes. The motivation is therefore twofold: improve planning quality with explicit but lightweight guidance, and make that guidance portable so new agents can benefit without another task-specific training cycle.

## Method

- **Task formulation**: an agent trajectory is written as `e = (u, a_1, o_1, ..., a_n)` with policy `\pi_\theta(e | u) = \prod_t \pi_\theta(a_t | u, a_1, o_1, ..., o_{t-1})`; after inserting a meta plan `p`, the conditioned policy becomes `\pi_\theta(e | u, p)`.
- **Meta plan abstraction**: MPO treats a meta plan as high-level natural-language guidance that omits environment-specific details while preserving a reusable action strategy.
- **Seed data construction**: GPT-4o converts task instruction `u` plus golden trajectory `e` into a plan `p`, then the authors manually filter plans that are incorrect, too detailed, or non-standard, yielding `\mathcal{D}_s = {(u, p)}_{i=1}^{|\mathcal{D}_s|}`.
- **SFT initialization**: the meta planner `\pi_g` is initialized with autoregressive loss `\mathcal{L}_{SFT} = -\mathbb{E}_{(u,p)\sim \mathcal{D}_s}[\log \pi_g(p|u)]`.
- **Monte Carlo quality evaluation**: for each sampled meta plan, the downstream agent rolls out `N` trajectories `\{e^{(i)}\}_{i=1}^N \sim \pi_\theta(e|u,p)`, and plan quality is estimated as `Q(p) = (1/N)\sum_i r(u, e^{(i)})`.
- **Preference construction**: the SFT-initialized planner samples `M` candidate plans per task; MPO keeps the highest-scoring plan `p_w` and lowest-scoring plan `p_l` and skips tasks where all plans tie.
- **DPO optimization**: the planner is refined on `\mathcal{D}_c = {(u, p_w, p_l)}_{i=1}^{|\mathcal{D}_c|}` with DPO loss using reference model `\pi_ref` and `\beta = 0.1`.
- **Training setup**: the meta planner backbone is `[[llama-3.1-8b-instruct]]`; SFT uses batch size `32`, learning rate `1e-5`, cosine schedule, and `3` epochs. DPO also uses batch size `32`, learning rate `1e-5`, warmup `3%`, cosine schedule, and `3` epochs.
- **Sampling hyperparameters**: main experiments set `M = 5` meta plans per task, `N = 5` execution trajectories per plan, and generation temperature `0.7` for both planner and agent sampling.
- **Benchmarks and systems**: MPO is trained/evaluated on ScienceWorld (`1483/194/241` train/seen/unseen), ALFWorld (`3321/140/134`), and WebShop (`1624/200/-`), implemented with `[[vllm]]` and Llama-Factory on `8` NVIDIA A100 80GB GPUs.

## Key Results

- Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct without extra agent training rises from average reward `39.5` to `55.5` with MPO, an absolute gain of `+16.0` and a relative gain of about `+40.5%`.
- GPT-4o improves from `68.3` to `76.8` average reward, while Llama-3.1-70B-Instruct improves from `70.9` to `79.5`.
- MPO also helps trained agents: Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct-ETO increases from `75.5` to `79.7`, exceeding the paper's strongest explicit-guidance baseline WKM (`76.2`) by `+3.5`.
- On unseen ALFWorld success rate, GPT-4o improves from `83.6` to `93.3` (`+9.7`), and GPT-4o-mini improves from `41.0` to `79.9` (`+38.9`).
- In the ablation study, GPT-4o with MPO reaches `67.8/93.3/66.3` on ScienceWorld unseen, ALFWorld unseen, and WebShop, beating SFT (`59.5/91.0/63.1`), direct GPT meta planning (`60.2/91.3/64.2`), and reject-sampling RFT (`61.8/89.6/65.5`).
- Meta plans work best when inserted into the task instruction: for GPT-4o on ScienceWorld/ALFWorld, instruction insertion scores `67.8/93.3`, versus `65.3/85.1` in the thought channel and `67.6/91.8` in observations.
- Sampling analysis shows quality degrades when `M` or `N` is too small, with `N` more influential; gains saturate quickly, motivating the paper's `N = M = 5` default.

## Limitations

- The available `paper.md` omits the author-affiliation header, which limits reliable author disambiguation for common Chinese romanized names in this staging ingest.
- MPO trains one meta planner per task family rather than one unified planner across diverse environments, so cross-task consolidation remains unresolved.
- The quality estimator depends on repeated agent-environment rollouts; although cheaper than retraining every target agent, it still incurs non-trivial sampling cost.
- The method uses Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct as the meta-planner backbone throughout, so the paper does not establish whether the same tradeoffs hold for much smaller or architecturally different planners.
- Contrastive preference construction relies on simple sampling plus Monte Carlo success estimates; the authors explicitly leave more efficient search procedures such as MCTS to future work.
