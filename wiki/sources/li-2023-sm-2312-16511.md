## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

This paper proposes S2M, a pipeline that converts single-turn machine reading comprehension data into multi-turn conversational question answering data. Starting from SQuAD 2.0 contexts and QA pairs, it uses an RGX-based generator to produce candidate QA pairs, an OpenIE6-derived knowledge graph plus triple-join heuristics to assemble coherent sequences, and a reverse question rewriting model trained on R-CANARD to turn self-contained questions into conversational follow-ups. The resulting synthetic dataset contains 19K dialogs and 342K questions, far smaller than SIMSEEK yet more effective for QuAC. Across RoBERTa, ELECTRA, and DeBERTa backbones, S2M sharply improves unsupervised QuAC performance and yields state-of-the-art leaderboard results after finetuning, showing that single-turn resources can be repurposed into higher-quality CQA supervision.

## Problem & Motivation

Conversational question answering needs multi-turn supervision, but real CQA datasets are relatively small and expensive to annotate. Existing augmentation routes are unsatisfying: directly mixing in single-turn reading-comprehension datasets suffers from distribution shift, while synthetic CQA generation methods that only condition on context do not exploit the existing single-turn QA pairs already present in resources such as SQuAD 2.0. The paper targets this gap by asking whether single-turn QA corpora can be systematically converted into coherent, conversational training data instead of being used only as mismatched auxiliary supervision.

## Method

- **Task setup**: conversational QA answers turn `t` from context `C = [s_1, ..., s_N]` and history `H_{<t} = [(q_1, a_1), ..., (q_{t-1}, a_{t-1})]`; span extraction uses start/end logits `p^s = softmax(r^s)` and `p^e = softmax(r^e)` over context tokens.
- **Candidate QA generation**: S2M reuses RGX to generate many single-turn QA pairs for each context and scores them with `S_l = -(\log P(I_{st}) + \log P(I_{ed}))`, where `I_{st}` and `I_{ed}` are answer start/end positions.
- **Difficulty-noise filtering**: the paper applies expectation-maximization to split generated QA pairs into four score bands (`low`, `relatively low`, `medium`, `high`), then drops the two extremes to avoid trivial or overly noisy questions.
- **Redundancy merging**: an Improved Union Search algorithm groups QA pairs whose wording overlaps by more than half of their total words and keeps the representative with the intermediate credit score.
- **Knowledge graph construction**: OpenIE6 extracts triples from each sentence; the Triple Join Algorithm links triples when subject/object strings are equal or contained, when an otherwise isolated triple should attach to an adjacent triple, or when adjacent sentences lack another connection and must be bridged by their terminal/initial triples.
- **Sequential reassembly**: generated and existing QA pairs are aligned to graph nodes by triple matching, then the method traverses the graph from the root and replaces continuous masked nodes with matched QA pairs to produce ordered multi-turn conversations.
- **Reverse rewriting**: the paper constructs `R-CANARD` from CANARD and trains a conversational question rewriting model to maximize `p_q^{CQR}(q_t^c | C, H_{<t}, q_t)`, turning self-contained questions into follow-up questions with pronouns and omitted shared context.
- **Generation / training details**: conversations are generated up to `12` turns and stop early on discontinuity or after more than `3` unanswerable questions; PLMs are trained first on synthetic conversations and then on QuAC with `max_question_len = 128`, `max_answer_len = 64`, document stride `128`, batch size `16`, seed `42`, beam size `5`, Adam learning rates `1e-6 / 1e-5 / 1e-5` for RoBERTa / ELECTRA / DeBERTa, and `4 x A100` GPUs for about `12` hours.

## Key Results

- **Synthetic dataset size**: S2M contains `19K` dialogs and `342K` questions, versus `213K` dialogs and `2.1M` questions for SIMSEEK.
- **Unsupervised QuAC gains**: training only on S2M raises QuAC dev F1 from `12.6` to `48.0` for RoBERTa, from `11.1` to `58.7` for ELECTRA, and from `13.2` to `59.2` for DeBERTa.
- **Supervised QuAC gains**: after additional QuAC finetuning, S2M improves RoBERTa from `73.4` to `74.5`, ELECTRA from `74.5` to `75.6`, and DeBERTa from `74.7` to `76.4`; the best HEQ-D reaches `16.2`.
- **Comparison with prior synthetic data**: on QuAC dev under DeBERTa, S2M reaches `76.4 / 73.5 / 16.2` for `F1 / HEQ-Q / HEQ-D`, beating RGX (`74.9 / 72.1 / 14.1`) and SIMSEEK (`76.2 / 73.5 / 15.4`).
- **Leaderboard result**: on the hidden QuAC test set, DEBERTA-S2M scores `76.3` F1, `73.6` HEQ-Q, and `17.9` HEQ-D, surpassing CDQ-DEBERTA at `75.8 / 73.1 / 15.9`.
- **Conversation statistics**: compared with SIMSEEK, S2M yields longer questions (`9.6` vs `8.3` tokens), longer answers (`15.3` vs `14.6`), much higher question-answer lexical overlap (`33.4` vs `7.3`), and stronger historical coherence (`10.8` vs `8.6`).

## Limitations

- The pipeline depends on OpenIE6 extraction quality and hand-designed triple-joining heuristics; extraction errors can directly distort graph structure and downstream QA ordering.
- Validation is concentrated on the `SQuAD 2.0 -> QuAC` conversion setting, so cross-domain robustness beyond Wikipedia-style reading comprehension remains unclear.
- The paper still needs a separately trained reverse question rewriter and does not jointly optimize generation, graph assembly, and rewriting end to end.
- Supervised improvements after QuAC finetuning are meaningful but modest compared with the very large unsupervised jump, suggesting synthetic data does not remove the need for real conversational supervision.
- The paper claims first-place leaderboard status at submission time in 2022; because this is a 2023 preprint in a fast-moving NLP area, the comparative state of the art should be rechecked.
