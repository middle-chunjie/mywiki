## Summary

STaRK introduces a benchmark for retrieval over semi-structured knowledge bases (SKBs) that combine free-text attributes with graph relations. It covers three domains, STARK-AMAZON, STARK-MAG, and STARK-PRIME, and pairs large synthesized query sets with a smaller human-written test set. The main technical contribution is a four-step pipeline that samples relational templates, extracts textual requirements from a sampled gold entity with LLMs, fuses both into natural user-like queries, and filters additional answers with LLM verification so the final ground-truth set satisfies both textual and relational constraints. Across sparse, dense, multivector, and LLM-reranked baselines, the benchmark shows that current retrieval systems still struggle when multi-hop relational reasoning and long textual evidence must be handled jointly.

## Problem & Motivation

Prior retrieval work mostly separated unstructured text retrieval from structured SQL or knowledge-graph question answering, leaving a gap for realistic queries that simultaneously mention textual properties and relational constraints. The paper targets semi-structured knowledge bases, where each answer entity must satisfy graph-side conditions and document-side requirements at the same time. This matters for applications such as product search, academic paper search, and precision medicine, especially when the underlying knowledge base is private and cannot be replaced by open-web retrieval. The benchmark is motivated by the need to measure how well LLM-era retrievers actually handle this mixed setting.

## Method

- **Task formulation**: define an SKB as a graph `G = (V, E)` plus associated documents `D = ⋃_{i ∈ V} D_i`; given query `Q`, retrieve an answer set `A ⊆ V` such that each `i ∈ A` satisfies both the relational constraints in `G` and the textual constraints in `D_i`.
- **Benchmark domains**: construct three SKBs with both text and relations: STARK-AMAZON (`1,035,542` entities, `9,443,802` relations, `592,067,882` tokens), STARK-MAG (`1,872,968` entities, `39,802,116` relations, `212,602,571` tokens), and STARK-PRIME (`129,375` entities, `8,100,498` relations, `31,844,769` tokens).
- **Synthesized benchmark generation**: use a four-step pipeline: `1)` sample expert-designed relational templates and instantiate them with seed entities, `2)` sample a gold answer and extract short textual properties from its document with LLMs, `3)` fuse relational and textual requirements into natural queries with two LLM passes, and `4)` filter additional answers by verifying which candidates satisfy all extracted textual properties.
- **LLM pipeline details**: the synthesis stages use task-specific LLMs such as `gpt-3.5-turbo-16k`, `claude-2.0`, `gpt-4-0125-preview`, `claude-2.1`, and `claude-instant-1.2`; the filtering step keeps only candidates that satisfy every required property, while gold-answer verification rates reach `86.6%`, `98.9%`, and `92.3%` on AMAZON, MAG, and PRIME.
- **Human validation and human-authored data**: analyze query diversity with Shannon entropy `H(X) = -∑ p(x_i) log p(x_i)` and type-token ratio `TTR = V / N`; run human evaluation with `63` participants, then collect `263` human-generated queries from `31` participants (`22` native English speakers) and manually verify their answers.
- **Evaluation protocol**: compare sparse retrieval (BM25), small dense retrievers (DPR, ANCE, QAGNN), LLM-based dense retrievers (`ada-002`, `voyage-large-2-instruct`, `LLM2Vec`, `GritLM-7b`), multivector retrievers (`multi-ada-002`, `ColBERTv2`), and LLM rerankers (Claude3 and GPT-4). Rerankers rescore top-`k` ada-002 candidates with `k = 20` on synthesized queries and `k = 10` on human queries using satisfaction scores in `[0, 1]`.
- **Metrics and systems considerations**: report `Hit@1`, `Hit@5`, `Recall@20`, and `MRR`, and measure latency on `1 × NVIDIA A100-SXM4-80GB` to expose the accuracy-latency trade-off of more capable reranking systems.

## Key Results

- The benchmark contains synthesized query sets of `9,100` (AMAZON), `13,323` (MAG), and `11,204` (PRIME) queries, plus `81`, `84`, and `98` human-generated queries respectively.
- Query diversity is high across domains: Shannon entropy is `10.39` / `10.25` / `9.63`, and type-token ratio is `0.179` / `0.180` / `0.143` for AMAZON, MAG, and PRIME.
- Human evaluation reports non-negative rates of `94.1%` for naturalness, `85.3%` for diversity, and `89.4%` for practicality on average.
- BM25 is a strong baseline on the full synthesized test sets, outperforming ANCE across all three domains; for example, on STARK-MAG it reaches `Hit@1 = 25.85` and `MRR = 34.91` versus `21.96` and `29.14` for ANCE.
- Among dense and multivector retrievers, `GritLM-7b` is strongest on STARK-MAG and STARK-PRIME full tests (`Hit@1 = 37.90` and `15.57`), while `ColBERTv2` gives the best STARK-AMAZON full-test `Hit@1 = 46.10` and `MRR = 55.51`.
- LLM rerankers improve top-rank quality on the random synthesized sample but remain far from solved: GPT-4 reranking reaches `Hit@1 = 40.90` on STARK-MAG and only `18.28` on STARK-PRIME, with `Recall@20` still below `60` on all three datasets.
- Latency is a major trade-off: average runtime is `1.40s` for DPR, `1.65s` for QAGNN, `2.83s` for ada-002, `2.97s` for multi-ada-002, `26.33s` for the Claude3 reranker, and `25.05s` for the GPT-4 reranker.

## Limitations

- The benchmark only covers textual and relational information; the paper explicitly leaves images, videos, and speech for future multimodal extensions.
- Synthesized queries are generated and filtered with LLMs, so benchmark quality depends on those model judgments even though the authors add human evaluation and manual filtering for the human-written subset.
- The human-generated portion is relatively small (`263` queries from `31` participants), and the paper notes that human queries still contain distinctive expressions not fully captured by the synthesized data.
- Privacy remains a concern when transferring the benchmarking setup to domains with real user or private knowledge-base data, even with anonymization efforts.
