## Summary

AGRaME studies how to rank text at a finer granularity than the unit used for indexing, without building a separate dense index for each granularity. Starting from multi-vector late-interaction retrieval, the paper shows that token-level embeddings already permit sentence- and proposition-level scoring, but vanilla ColBERTv2 underperforms when passage-level encodings are reused for finer ranking. The proposed fix is a multi-granular contrastive objective that adds sentence-level supervision on top of standard passage-level distillation, plus a distinct query marker for fine-grained scoring. Across open-domain QA, proposition retrieval, and citation addition for retrieval-augmented generation, AGRaME improves fine-grained ranking while largely preserving coarse passage-level performance, and its PropCite application beats prompt-based citation generation.

## Problem & Motivation

Existing dense ranking pipelines usually bind retrieval granularity to index granularity: passage encoders rank passages, sentence encoders rank sentences, and finer attribution often needs a separate encoder or index. This is limiting for open-domain QA and attribution, where the relevant evidence is often a sentence or proposition inside a larger passage. The paper argues that multi-vector retrieval should, in principle, support this flexibility because token-level embeddings expose sub-span interactions. The problem is that standard training teaches the model to identify the right passage, not the right sub-unit within a passage, so finer-grained ranking quality degrades unless the training signal is adapted.

## Method

- **Base late-interaction scorer**: AGRaME starts from ColBERTv2, encoding query and passage tokens separately and scoring them with `S_CB(q, p) = sum_i max_j Q_i^T P_j`, where MaxSim aggregates the best passage-token match for each query token.
- **Sentence scoring inside a passage**: with passage-level encoding unchanged, the model scores a sentence `s_j^{p_i}` by restricting MaxSim to the token vectors belonging to that sentence: `S_CB(q, s_j^{p_i}) = sum_i max_r Q'_i^T P_{jr}`.
- **Granularity-specific query marker**: passage ranking uses the default query marker `m_q`, while sentence-level ranking uses a new marker `m'_q` so the query encoder can switch to a more discriminative notion of relevance for in-passage ranking.
- **Cross-passage sentence ranking**: because the sentence loss only teaches discrimination within a passage, final sentence ranking across passages uses `Score(q, s_j^{p_i}) = S_CB(q, s_j^{p_i}) + alpha * S_CB(q, p_i)`.
- **Passage-level distillation loss**: for each query and `(k + 1)`-way passage set `[p] = {p^+, p_1^-, ..., p_k^-}`, the student matches a cross-encoder distribution with `L_psg(q, [p]) = KL(D_CE(q, [p]) || D_CB(q, [p]))`.
- **Sentence-level teacher and loss**: a second cross-encoder `CE'` marks one sentence inside a passage with delimiters and produces soft sentence relevance scores; AGRaME minimizes `L_s(q, p_i) = KL(D_CE'(q, [s^{p_i}]) || D_CB(q, [s^{p_i}]))`.
- **Multi-granular training objective**: sentence losses are weighted by passage relevance and added to passage distillation: `L_sent(q, [p]) = sum_i Softmax(S_CE(q, p_i)) * L_s(q, p_i)`, `L(q, [p]) = L_psg(q, [p]) + L_sent(q, [p])`.
- **Experimental setup details**: OpenQA reranking uses top-`100` Contriever-retrieved passages; proposition retrieval evaluates sentence encodings with proposition token masks on PropSegmEnt; PropCite segments generated sentences into propositions with a T5-based segmenter and scores input context passages for `K in {5, 10}` retrieved passages.

## Key Results

- **Motivating gap on Natural Questions**: vanilla ColBERTv2 sentence ranking drops from `P@1/R@5 = 31.6/56.3` with sentence-level encoding to `27.4/48.8` with passage-level encoding, despite passage context being potentially richer.
- **Open-domain QA gains**: with passage-level encoding, AGRaME improves sentence-level `P@1` over ColBERTv2 from `27.9 -> 36.8` on Natural Questions, `43.5 -> 48.9` on TriviaQA, `27.6 -> 33.2` on WebQuestions, and `39.2 -> 43.8` on EntityQuestions, while passage-level scores remain nearly unchanged.
- **Cross-domain robustness**: on RobustQA, average sentence-level `P@1` rises from `21.4` for passage-encoded ColBERTv2 to `24.2` for AGRaME, while average passage-level `P@1` is essentially preserved (`33.8` vs `33.6`).
- **Marker ablation**: using the new query marker at both training and inference gives the best sentence-level `P@1`, e.g. `36.8` vs `35.9` on NQ and `48.9` vs `47.6` on TriviaQA compared with training/inference that both reuse the default marker.
- **Proposition retrieval**: on PropSegmEnt, AGRaME reaches proposition-level `P@1/R@5 = 47.7/74.7`, beating ColBERTv2 (`46.9/74.2`) and the stronger SubEncoder baseline (`41.0/72.2` for the ST5 version).
- **Citation addition**: PropCite improves over prompt-based citation generation, e.g. on ASQA with Mistral 7B and `K = 5`, precision/recall moves from `64.9/69.5` to `65.7/74.2`; on Qwen1.5 4B the jump is larger, from `26.9/21.3` to `48.9/54.5`.

## Limitations

- The sentence-level supervision is only intra-passage, so sentence ranking across passages still requires adding the passage score as an extra term.
- The method depends on auxiliary teacher models: a passage cross-encoder for distillation and a second sentence-marking cross-encoder for fine-grained supervision.
- Proposition-level gains are modest because the training objective is still sentence-level; the paper explicitly leaves proposition-level supervision as future work.
- Open-domain QA experiments rerank only the top-`100` Contriever results because of storage limits, so the paper does not show a full end-to-end first-stage retrieval system.
- PropCite depends on proposition segmentation and on the factual quality of the generated answer; when the generator hallucinates unsupported content, post-hoc citation quality necessarily degrades.
