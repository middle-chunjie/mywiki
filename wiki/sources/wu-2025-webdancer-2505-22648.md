## Summary

WebDancer presents an end-to-end recipe for training autonomous information-seeking web agents rather than relying only on prompting or shallow QA supervision. The pipeline combines large-scale synthetic deep-web QA construction, rejection-sampled ReAct trajectories, supervised fine-tuning for agent cold start, and on-policy reinforcement learning with DAPO. The system restricts actions to web search and page visits, then trains models to interleave thoughts, tool calls, and observations over multi-step trajectories. On GAIA and WebWalkerQA, WebDancer substantially improves over vanilla ReAct across Qwen-2.5-7B, Qwen-2.5-32B, and QwQ-32B backbones, with the best QwQ-32B variant reaching 51.5 average Pass@1 on GAIA and 47.9 on WebWalkerQA. The paper's main contribution is a data-and-training recipe for building stronger deep-research-style agents from scratch.

## Problem & Motivation

The paper argues that existing information-seeking agents are bottlenecked by shallow datasets, weak long-horizon supervision, and training pipelines that do not fully exploit tool-augmented reasoning. Prompt-only systems cannot reliably internalize multi-step search behavior, while earlier SFT or RL setups often operate on limited benchmarks or simple trajectories that do not match real web complexity. WebDancer targets this gap by building harder QA data, sampling better trajectories, and combining cold-start imitation with on-policy RL so the model can generalize to dynamic, open-web environments.

## Method

- **Data synthesis**: builds two synthetic QA corpora for deep information seeking: `60K` CRAWLQA instances from recursively crawled knowledgeable sites and `40K` E2HQA instances that iteratively rewrite a simple question into a harder one via `R_n = \pi(S(C_n))`.
- **Agent interface**: uses a ReAct-style loop with history `\mathcal{H}_t = (\tau_0, \alpha_0, o_0, \ldots, \tau_{t-1}, \alpha_{t-1}, o_{t-1})`, where action names satisfy `\alpha^m \in \{search, visit, answer\}`.
- **Tool schema**: search parameters are `query` and `filter_year`; visit parameters are `goal` and `url_link`. Search observations expose Top-10 titles/snippets, while visit observations return evidence and summaries produced by summarizer `M_s = Qwen-2.5-72B`.
- **Trajectory sampling**: generates short-CoT traces with GPT-4o and long-CoT traces with QwQ-Plus, then applies rejection sampling up to `N = 5` times per QA pair.
- **Trajectory filtering**: keeps only traces that pass validity control, correctness verification, and quality assessment, emphasizing instruction compliance, answer correctness, non-redundancy, goal alignment, and logical reasoning.
- **SFT cold start**: reformats trajectories with `<think>...</think>`, `<tool_call>...</tool_call>`, `<tool_response>...</tool_response>`, and `<answer>...</answer>`, then optimizes `L = -(1 / \sum \mathbb{I}[x_i \neq o]) \sum \mathbb{I}[x_i \neq o] \log \pi_\theta(x_i \mid tc, x_{<i})`, masking observation tokens from the loss.
- **RL stage**: continues with on-policy DAPO, optimizing clipped policy ratios over model-generated tokens only; the reward is `R(\hat{y}, y) = 0.1 * score_format + 0.9 * score_answer`.
- **Implementation details**: training uses ChatML-style multi-turn formatting, `16` rollouts per RL prompt, inference `temperature = 0.6`, `top_p = 0.95`, LRM repetition penalty `1.1`, and RL rollout sampling `temperature = 1.0`, `top_p = 1.0`.

## Key Results

- On **GAIA**, WebDancer improves Qwen-2.5-7B from `18.4` to `31.0` average Pass@1 and Qwen-2.5-32B from `31.0` to `40.7`.
- On **WebWalkerQA**, WebDancer improves Qwen-2.5-7B from `24.2` to `36.0` average Pass@1 and Qwen-2.5-32B from `31.9` to `38.4`.
- The best **QwQ-32B + WebDancer** variant reaches `51.5` average Pass@1 on GAIA (`61.5 / 50.0 / 25.0` across Levels 1/2/3), versus `37.8` for vanilla ReAct.
- The same best variant reaches `47.9` average Pass@1 on WebWalkerQA (`52.5 / 59.6 / 35.4` across Easy/Medium/Hard), versus `24.1` for vanilla ReAct.
- On harder BrowseComp benchmarks, WebDancer reports `3.8 / 7.9` on BrowseComp-en and `18.0 / 31.5` on BrowseComp-zh under Pass@1 / Pass@3.
- In analysis, the best model attains `64.1%` Pass@3 on GAIA and `62.0%` Pass@3 on WebWalkerQA; direct RL without cold-start is much weaker, with only `5%` Pass@3 on GAIA in the cited ablation.

## Limitations

- The evaluation focus is still narrow: the main benchmarks are short-answer information-seeking tasks, and the paper explicitly leaves document-level research and long-form generation to future work.
- RL gains on strong reasoning backbones are modest in some settings; the authors attribute this partly to sparse rewards over very long trajectories.
- The approach depends on synthetic QA generation and LLM-based judging, so label noise and reward-model errors remain plausible failure modes.
- Agent performance is hard to stabilize because the real web environment is non-stationary; even memorization-style fine-tuning on `69` GAIA trajectories only yields `37.4%` greedy accuracy on the same split.
