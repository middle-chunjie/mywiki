## Summary

D2Skill is an agentic reinforcement learning framework that couples policy optimization with a dynamic dual-granularity skill bank built entirely from training-time experience. The core idea is to store both task skills for high-level guidance and step skills for local correction, then update their utility by contrasting paired baseline and skill-injected rollouts sampled under the same policy. Reflection over failed and successful trajectories continuously expands the bank, while utility-aware retrieval and pruning keep it compact. On ALFWORLD and WEBSHOP, D2Skill consistently improves over GRPO and usually beats SkillRL despite avoiding validation-time privileged information, delivering roughly `10-20` point success-rate gains with only modest wall-clock overhead.

## Problem & Motivation

Agentic RL in textual environments is long-horizon, partially observable, and sparse-reward. A fixed interaction history is not a sufficient statistic of latent state, so credit assignment becomes hard and exploration is inefficient. Prior memory- or skill-based approaches help, but they mostly extract trajectory-level guidance and often lack a principled mechanism for evaluating, retrieving, and pruning accumulated skills as training progresses. The paper therefore targets two gaps simultaneously: finer-grained reusable experience that can correct local errors during interaction, and a dynamic memory-management scheme that keeps the skill repository useful rather than letting it drift into redundancy.

## Method

- **History-augmented decision process**: the agent acts on `x_t = (\tau_g, \mathcal{H}_t^L, o_t, \mathcal{A}_t^{adm})`, where the recent history window `\mathcal{H}_t^L` is augmented with retrieved skills to form `\tilde{x}_t = (x_t, m_t)`.
- **Dual-granularity bank**: D2Skill separates reusable experience into task skills for high-level task guidance and step skills for fine-grained correction, with retrieval keys `k_m = g` for task skills and `k_m = (g, o_j)` for step skills.
- **Paired rollout design**: for each task, a rollout group of size `N` is split into baseline and skill groups of size `N/2`; only the skill group retrieves skills, so the framework can compare matched rollouts under the same policy.
- **Hindsight utility signals**: task-skill utility uses `\Delta_g^{task} = \bar{Y}_g^{skill} - \bar{Y}_g^{base}`, while step-skill credit uses `c_i = Y_i - \bar{Y}_g^{base}`; both are folded into exponential-moving-average updates with coefficients `\beta_task` and `\beta_step`.
- **Intrinsic reward shaping**: skill-injected trajectories receive `R_i^{int} = \lambda (Y_i - \bar{Y}_g^{base})`, so policy optimization rewards improvements over the paired baseline rather than absolute return alone.
- **Policy objective**: the method plugs the shaped returns into group-normalized GRPO, using `\tilde{R}_i = R_i + R_i^{int}` for skill rollouts and the usual clipped objective `\min(r_i A_i, clip(r_i, 1-\epsilon, 1+\epsilon) A_i) - \beta D_{KL}`.
- **Reflection-driven generation**: when `\bar{Y}_g^{skill} < \tau_ref`, the system reflects on one failed trajectory and, when available, one successful trajectory to generate at most one task skill and one step skill per task group.
- **Two-stage retrieval**: candidate skills are first filtered by cosine similarity with threshold `\tau_sim`, then ranked by `score(m) = \alpha \hat{sim}(m,q) + (1-\alpha)(u_m + \eta \sqrt{\log(1+N_r)/(1+n_m)})`, which balances semantic matching with utility and exploration.
- **Bank management**: each pool has capacity `N_max`; if exceeded, skills are pruned by ascending `evict(m) = u_m + \eta \sqrt{\log(1+N_r)/(1+n_m)}`, while newly created skills are protected for `T_prot` steps.

## Key Results

- On ALFWORLD with `Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct`, D2Skill reaches `90.6` overall success with Gemini-3-Flash skills, beating GRPO by `15.6` points (`75.0 -> 90.6`) and SkillRL by `1.5` points (`89.1 -> 90.6`).
- On WEBSHOP with `Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct`, the best D2Skill variant reaches `91.1` score / `80.5%` success, while the O3-based variant reaches `90.1` / `84.4%`; both outperform GRPO at `86.0` / `72.6%`.
- On ALFWORLD with `Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507`, D2Skill improves overall success from `53.9` with GRPO to `69.6` and `72.7`, i.e. gains of `15.7` and `18.8` points.
- With a teacher-initialized `Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507 + SFT` policy, D2Skill reaches `92.2` ALFWORLD success after `40` RL steps versus `83.6` for GRPO at the same budget, and `95.3` after `120` steps versus `92.9`.
- Ablations on ALFWORLD show the full model at `72.7` validation success, versus `62.7` without task skills, `60.2` without step skills, and `57.8` without skill management.
- Training cost on `8 x H100` is `25.6h` for D2Skill, compared with `20.8h` for GRPO and `49.2h` for SkillRL, so the added memory machinery costs about `1.2x` over GRPO but is much cheaper than SkillRL.

## Limitations

- The empirical study is limited to two benchmarks, ALFWORLD and WEBSHOP, so generalization to broader agentic environments is still unverified.
- The framework depends on an external reflector model such as Gemini or O3 for skill generation, which adds system complexity and an additional external dependency.
- Several control knobs (`\tau_ref`, `\tau_sim`, `\alpha`, `\eta`, `N_max`, `T_prot`) shape retrieval and pruning behavior, so practical performance may be sensitive to tuning choices not deeply explored in the paper.
- The smaller `Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507` model remains ineffective on WEBSHOP, and those results are omitted, indicating that D2Skill does not fully overcome weak backbone limitations.
