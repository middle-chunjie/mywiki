## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

This paper introduces Active Retrieval-Augmented Generation and instantiates it with FLARE, a forward-looking inference-time method for long-form generation. Instead of retrieving once from the original prompt or retrieving passively at fixed intervals, FLARE first predicts the next sentence, checks token confidence, and retrieves only when low-probability spans suggest missing knowledge. The predicted sentence is then used directly, masked, or converted into explicit questions to retrieve evidence for regenerating the sentence. Evaluated with `text-davinci-003` on 2WikiMultihopQA, StrategyQA, ASQA, and WikiAsp, FLARE consistently beats single-time and passive multi-time retrieval baselines, with especially large gains on multi-hop QA, showing that retrieval timing and future-aware query formulation materially affect factual long-form generation.

## Problem & Motivation

Single-time retrieval-augmented generation works well when the information need is explicit in the input, but it is brittle for long-form generation, where later sentences often require facts not obvious from the initial query. Passive multi-time methods retrieve from previous tokens or at fixed intervals, which can miss future intent or retrieve unnecessarily. The paper asks whether a generic inference-time method can decide both when retrieval is needed and what to retrieve based on anticipated future content, thereby reducing hallucination while avoiding unnecessary retrieval noise.

## Method

- **General framework**: active retrieval-augmented generation interleaves retrieval and generation with query function `q_t = qry(x, y_<t)` and step output `y_t = LM([D_{q_t}, x, y_<t])`; initial retrieval uses the input `x`, while previous retrieved documents are dropped to control context length.
- **FLARE-direct generation loop**: first predict a temporary next sentence `s_hat_t = LM([x, y_<t])`; if every token probability is at least `theta`, accept `s_hat_t`, otherwise retrieve and regenerate `s_t = LM([D_{q_t}, x, y_<t])`.
- **Active retrieval trigger**: retrieval is invoked when any token in `s_hat_t` has probability below threshold `theta in [0, 1]`, operationalizing the assumption that low confidence reflects missing knowledge; task-specific settings include `theta = 0.8` for 2WikiMultihopQA, ASQA, and WikiAsp, and `theta = 0.4` for StrategyQA.
- **Query formulation**: the next sentence is turned into a retrieval query either implicitly with masked low-confidence spans `mask(s_hat_t)` or explicitly by generating questions `qgen(s_hat_t)` for spans below threshold `beta`; the paper uses `beta = 0.4` throughout and finds masking best on 2WikiMultihopQA while explicit questions work similarly on ASQA-hint and WikiAsp.
- **FLARE-instruct variant**: a second variant prompts the LM to emit `[Search(query)]` inline during generation; implementation boosts the logit of `"["` by `+2.0`, then suppresses it immediately after a retrieval call to prevent repeated search markers.
- **Base model and sentence unit**: all experiments use `text-davinci-003`; each iteration generates `64` tokens, applies the NLTK sentence tokenizer, keeps the first sentence as the semantic unit, and discards the rest before the next step.
- **Retrieval backends and prompting**: Wikipedia-based tasks use BM25 over the Wikipedia dump, while WikiAsp uses Bing over the open web; top-`k` is `2` for 2WikiMultihopQA, `3` for StrategyQA and ASQA, and `5` for WikiAsp, with `8/6/8/4` few-shot exemplars respectively.
- **Task coverage**: the method is evaluated on [[multihop-question-answering]], [[commonsense-reasoning]], [[open-domain-question-answering]], and open-domain summarization settings to test generality beyond a single benchmark family.

## Key Results

- **2WikiMultihopQA**: FLARE-direct reaches `51.0 EM / 59.7 F1`, beating no retrieval (`28.2 / 36.8`), single-time retrieval (`39.4 / 48.8`), previous-window (`43.2 / 52.3`), previous-sentence (`39.0 / 49.2`), question decomposition (`47.8 / 56.4`), and FLARE-instruct (`42.4 / 49.8`).
- **StrategyQA**: FLARE improves exact match to `77.3`, above no retrieval (`72.9`), single-time retrieval (`68.6`), previous-window (`71.2`), and previous-sentence (`71.0`).
- **ASQA / ASQA-hint**: FLARE reaches `41.3 EM / 28.2 D-F1 / 31.1 DR` on ASQA and `46.2 EM / 36.7 D-F1 / 37.2 DR` on ASQA-hint, outperforming all listed baselines in both settings.
- **WikiAsp**: FLARE gets `53.4 UniEval / 18.9 E-F1 / 27.6 ROUGE-L`, modestly but consistently above single-time retrieval (`52.4 / 17.4 / 26.9`) and passive multi-time retrieval.
- **Forward-looking ablation**: retrieving with the next sentence beats the previous sentence on 2WikiMultihopQA (`48.8 EM` vs `39.0`) and ASQA-hint (`45.9 EM` vs `42.5`), directly supporting the future-intent hypothesis.
- **Active retrieval ablation**: best 2WikiMultihopQA masking performance occurs at `beta = 0.4` with `0.510 EM / 0.597 F1`; retrieval rates around `40%-80%` of sentences are usually sufficient, while overly frequent retrieval can hurt StrategyQA.

## Limitations

- The method does not deliver clear gains on every long-form task: the paper reports limited improvements on Wizard of Wikipedia and ELI5.
- Short-output tasks may not need repeated retrieval, while open-ended long-form QA remains hard to ground and evaluate even with retrieval.
- The approach increases inference cost because retrieval and generation are interleaved across multiple LM calls rather than done once.
- A naive implementation recomputes prior activations after each retrieval because cached generation states are not preserved across retrieval-conditioned updates.
- The paper relies on black-box GPT-3.5 APIs and off-the-shelf retrievers, so it does not study training-time architectural changes for more efficient active information integration.
