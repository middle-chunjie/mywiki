## Summary

The paper introduces Rank1, a reranker that explicitly uses [[test-time-compute]] by generating a reasoning trace before emitting a final relevance judgment for each query-passage pair. The training recipe distills more than `635k` DeepSeek R1 traces on MS MARCO into smaller Qwen-based models, then filters them down to `386,336` higher-quality examples. The resulting 7B, 14B, and 32B models substantially outperform prior pointwise rerankers on reasoning-heavy and instruction-following benchmarks, including BRIGHT, NevIR, and mFollowIR, while also exposing benchmark saturation in older datasets such as TREC DL19 and parts of BEIR. The work argues that explainable reasoning and extra inference-time tokens can define a new family of search rerankers, even if latency and calibration remain open issues.

## Problem & Motivation

The paper targets a gap between recent reasoning language models and standard IR rerankers. Existing pointwise rerankers usually emit a scalar relevance score without using additional inference-time deliberation, which limits their ability to handle reasoning-intensive retrieval, negation, and instruction-conditioned search. The authors argue that if a reranker can inspect both the query and each candidate passage while producing an auditable chain of reasoning, it can support more complex retrieval use cases and provide explanations that are useful for users and [[retrieval-augmented-generation]] systems.

The motivation is also empirical: traditional reranking benchmarks appear increasingly saturated for strong models, so the authors want a model that improves on harder tasks rather than merely optimizing legacy qrels.

## Method

- **Teacher-generated supervision**: prompt [[deepseek-r1]] on MS MARCO query-passage pairs to produce a reasoning trace plus a final relevance label, using `temperature = 0.3` and `max_output_tokens = 1000`.
- **Data construction**: initial generation mixes four equally weighted subsets: MS MARCO positives, Tevatron negatives, mT5 hard negatives ranked `1-5`, and mT5 hard negatives ranked `5-10`.
- **Quality filtering**: after observing heavy noise in mined negatives, filter examples where R1 disagrees with trusted labels and run a self-filtering pass with a 24B Rank1 variant. The final training set contains `386,336` examples: `136k` MS MARCO positives, `154k` Tevatron negatives, and `96k` mT5 negatives.
- **Student models**: fine-tune Qwen 2.5 base models at `7B`, `14B`, and `32B` parameters so each model learns to emit an explicit [[reasoning-trace]] before the relevance decision.
- **Optimization**: use LoRA over all parameters with `rank = 32`, `alpha = 64`, learning rate `1e-4`, and effective batch size `128`; train for up to `2` epochs or `3` days, with early stopping on BRIGHT Biology and [[nevir]].
- **Serving setup**: inference uses [[vllm]] on `1 x H100 80GB`, and the authors also quantize models to int4 with AutoAWQ to lower memory without losing most of the gains.
- **Evaluation protocol**: test mainly in a [[pointwise-ranking]] setup over top-`100` candidates from first-stage retrievers, then compare against MonoT5, RankLLaMA, mT5, and instruction-following baselines on BRIGHT, NevIR, [[mfollowir]], TREC DL19, and BEIR.

## Key Results

- **BRIGHT reasoning benchmark**: Rank1 reaches average nDCG@10 of `27.5` (`7B`), `28.7` (`14B`), and `29.4` (`32B`), versus `16.8` for MonoT5-3B and `13.7` for RankLLaMA-13B.
- **NevIR negation benchmark**: Rank1 scores `65.1`, `67.5`, and `70.1` pairwise accuracy, compared with `50.6` for MonoT5-3B and `43.2` for RankLLaMA-13B; the `32B` model matches RankGPT-4o at `70.1`.
- **mFollowIR multilingual / instruction-following**: average multilingual nDCG@20 reaches `0.586`, `0.594`, and `0.610`, beating mT5-13B at `0.517`; the best p-MRR improvement is `+10.1`.
- **TREC DL19 after relabeling**: fixed nDCG@10 becomes `78.6`, `77.6`, and `80.1`, exceeding RankLLaMA-7B / 13B at `76.9`, while also surfacing many more unjudged documents.
- **Quantization**: int4-compressed models retain most of their gains; quantized `32B` shrinks from `65.5 GB` to `19.3 GB` while keeping `77.2` on SciFact and `69.9` on NevIR.
- **Ablation**: removing the reasoning chain drops BRIGHT average from `27.5` to `17.5` on the `7B` model, indicating that the trace itself provides much of the performance boost.

## Limitations

- The model is slower than classification-head rerankers because test-time reasoning consumes extra tokens and compute.
- The paper reports that simple budget forcing from `s1` did not improve performance, so the claimed benefits come from distilled reasoning rather than successful inference-time scaling at evaluation.
- Training data is derived entirely from English MS MARCO pairs, which limits direct evidence for multilingual robustness despite good transfer to [[mfollowir]].
- The model can overthink and become overly strict about relevance, as illustrated by failure cases on TREC-COVID-style queries.
- Claims about benchmark saturation rely partly on targeted manual relabeling (`295` DL19 labels) rather than a full re-pooling of old benchmarks.
- BEIR results are mixed and often below strong baselines, so the approach is not uniformly better on traditional retrieval collections.
