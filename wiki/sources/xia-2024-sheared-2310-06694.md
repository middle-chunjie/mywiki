## Summary

The paper proposes LLM-Shearing, a two-stage recipe for building competitive small decoder-only language models by pruning a stronger pretrained model instead of training from scratch. Starting from LLaMA2-7B, the method combines targeted structured pruning, which searches for subnetworks that exactly match a desired dense target shape, with dynamic batch loading, which continuously reweights pretraining domains according to residual loss against domain-specific reference losses. The resulting Sheared-LLaMA-1.3B and 2.7B models are continued-pretrained on RedPajama for only `50B` tokens after a `0.4B`-token pruning phase, yet they outperform comparable open models such as Pythia, INCITE, and OpenLLaMA on a broad downstream suite. The central claim is that structured pruning plus limited recovery training can be substantially more compute-efficient than full pretraining.

## Problem & Motivation

Training even billion-parameter general-purpose LLMs from scratch remains expensive because modern small models still consume hundreds of billions to trillions of tokens. Pure pruning methods can reduce parameter count and inference cost, but existing structured pruning approaches often produce irregular architectures and suffer large capability losses unless substantial recovery training is added. The paper asks whether one can start from a strong open model, prune it into a dense target architecture aligned with an existing efficient design, and then recover performance with much less compute than end-to-end pretraining.

## Method

- **Two-stage pipeline**: start from `\mathcal{M}_S =` LLaMA2-7B and first prune into a smaller dense model `\mathcal{M}_T`, then run continued pre-training with the standard LM objective to recover lost capability.
- **Targeted structured pruning**: learn mask variables over layers, hidden dimensions, heads, and FFN intermediate dimensions, with masks `z^{layer} \in \mathbb{R}^{L_S}`, `z^{hidden} \in \mathbb{R}^{d_S}`, `z^{head} \in \mathbb{R}^{H_S}` per layer, and `z^{int} \in \mathbb{R}^{m_S}` per layer.
- **Constrained objective**: optimize `` `\mathcal{L}_{prune}(\theta,z,\lambda,\phi)=\mathcal{L}(\theta,z)+\sum_j \tilde{\mathcal{L}}^{head}_j+\sum_j \tilde{\mathcal{L}}^{int}_j+\tilde{\mathcal{L}}^{layer}+\tilde{\mathcal{L}}^{hidden}` `` so the selected subnetwork matches the requested dense architecture rather than only a sparsity level.
- **Shape constraints**: for each substructure, the paper adds Lagrangian penalties such as `` `\tilde{\mathcal{L}}^{head}=\lambda^{head}(\sum z^{head}-H_T)+\phi^{head}(\sum z^{head}-H_T)^2` ``; mask variables are parameterized with hard-concrete distributions so discrete keep/prune decisions can be learned.
- **Target architectures**: the `1.3B` model follows the Pythia-1.4B shape (`24` layers, hidden `2048`, intermediate `5504`, `16` heads), while the `2.7B` model follows INCITE-Base-3B-like depth/width (`32` layers, hidden `2560`, intermediate `6912`, `20` heads). The source LLaMA2-7B has `32` layers, hidden `4096`, intermediate `11008`, and `32` heads.
- **Dynamic batch loading**: every `m` steps, compute per-domain residual loss `` `\Delta_t[i]=\max(\ell_t[i]-\ell_{ref}[i],0)` ``, then update sampling weights with `` `\alpha_t=w_{t-m}\cdot \exp(\Delta_t)` `` and `` `w_t=\alpha_t / \sum_i \alpha_t[i]` `` so domains furthest from their reference losses receive more data.
- **Reference losses**: fit per-domain scaling laws of the form `` `L(N,D)=E + A/N^\alpha + B/D^\beta` `` using LLaMA2 checkpoints, then use the predicted loss of hypothetical `1.3B` or `2.7B` LLaMA2 models as the default training target for each RedPajama domain.
- **Training setup**: prune on RedPajama for `0.4B` tokens and continue pre-training for `50B` tokens with sequence length `4096`; pruning uses LR `1e-4` for weights and `1.0` for `z,\phi,\lambda`, warmup `10%`, batch size `131K` tokens, and eval interval `50`; continued pre-training uses LR `1e-4`, warmup `3%`, batch size `1M` tokens, eval interval `400`, and up to `16` Nvidia A100 `80GB` GPUs.

## Key Results

- **Compute efficiency**: Sheared-LLaMA uses `50B` continued-pretraining tokens, about `5%` of OpenLLaMA's `1T` pretraining budget; the abstract frames this as roughly `3%` of training compute versus training comparable models from scratch.
- **1.3B model wins over same-scale baselines**: Sheared-LLaMA-1.3B reaches average score `51.0`, beating OPT-1.3B (`48.2`) and Pythia-1.4B (`48.9`) on the 11-task evaluation suite.
- **2.7B model beats larger-budget open models**: Sheared-LLaMA-2.7B reaches average `56.7`, surpassing INCITE-Base-3B (`54.7`), OpenLLaMA-3B-v1 (`55.1`), and OpenLLaMA-3B-v2 (`55.7`) while training on far fewer tokens.
- **Representative task gains**: Sheared-LLaMA-2.7B scores `70.8` on HellaSwag versus `67.6` for OpenLLaMA-3B-v2 and `64.8` for INCITE-Base-3B, and `41.2` on ARC-C versus `39.0` and `40.2` respectively.
- **Dynamic batch loading changes domain mix sharply**: cumulative RedPajama usage shifts from `15.0%` to `49.2%` on C4 and from `4.5%` to `9.1%` on Books, while GitHub drops from `4.5%` to `0.8%`, yielding better downstream curves than the original mixture.
- **Throughput tradeoff**: compared with CoFiPruning at similar sparsity, targeted pruning yields slightly worse immediate perplexity but better inference throughput, e.g. `58` vs `51` tokens/s for the `1.3B` setting and `43` vs `37` for the `3B` setting on A100.

## Limitations

- The method depends on access to a strong source LLM and an open pretraining corpus that meaningfully overlaps with target domains; unsupported domains are hard to recover.
- Experiments only prune from `7B`-scale sources, so claims about larger-scale transfer remain unverified.
- Math and coding performance remain limited because the dynamic objective tracks reference losses derived from the chosen data mixture; the paper explicitly notes weaker results when ArXiv and GitHub exposure is low.
- The pruning stage is about `5x` slower than ordinary LM training, so compute savings rely on keeping pruning budget small and recovering performance during later continued pre-training.
