## Summary

Retro* is a pointwise LLM reranker for reasoning-intensive document retrieval, where relevant evidence may be only indirectly connected to the query. The core design combines rubric-based relevance scoring, which asks the model to output an interpretable `0-100` absolute relevance score for each query-document pair, with test-time score integration over multiple sampled reasoning trajectories. The paper further trains the model with a two-stage recipe: supervised fine-tuning on teacher-generated rubric-following trajectories, then reinforcement learning with a composite reward that jointly improves per-document score stability and cross-document discrimination. On BRIGHT, Retro* reaches state-of-the-art nDCG@10, improves further under mean-score@16, and retains the efficiency advantage of pointwise parallel inference over listwise and setwise rerankers.

## Problem & Motivation

The paper targets retrieval settings in which lexical or shallow semantic matching is insufficient because the useful document may support the task only through an implicit theorem, design pattern, or abstract conceptual bridge. Prior reasoning-enhanced rerankers improve ranking quality, but the authors argue they still have three gaps: they usually return only relative order rather than an interpretable absolute relevance estimate, they underuse test-time scaling because they rely on a single trajectory, and listwise or setwise formulations introduce sequential latency when the candidate set grows. Retro* is proposed to make relevance measurement, reranking quality, and inference scalability work together inside one pointwise framework.

## Method

- **Rubric-based relevance scoring**: define a rubric `\Gamma` with a task-specific relevance definition plus 5 score bands over `0-100`; for each query-document pair, Retro* outputs a reasoning trajectory and integer score, formalized as ``Retro*(\Gamma, q, d) -> (y, s)``.
- **Absolute score semantics**: the scoring bands are explicitly interpreted as `80-100`, `60-80`, `40-60`, `20-40`, and `0-20`, so the score is intended to be directly usable for both filtering and reranking rather than only pairwise comparison.
- **Test-time scaling by score integration**: sample `K` trajectories for each `(q, d)` pair and aggregate their scores with ``\bar{s} = \sum_i w_i s_i / \sum_i w_i``; in the main evaluation the paper uses uniform weights, i.e. mean-score@`K`.
- **SFT warm-up stage**: for each training pair, a teacher model `\mathbb{T}` generates `K = 8` rubric-following trajectories; the trajectory whose score is closest to the integrated score is retained for SFT, with an explicit length-control instruction that keeps reasoning within `512` tokens.
- **Intra-document RL reward**: roll out `N = 8` trajectories per `(q, d)`; assign `+1` to the score closest to `\bar{s}`, `-1` to the furthest one, and `0` otherwise, subject to a disagreement threshold ``\tau = 20`` so trivial agreement cases are skipped.
- **Inter-document RL reward**: for one positive document `d+` and one negative document `d-`, reward each sampled trajectory by the fraction of opposite-label trajectories it correctly dominates, encouraging ``s(d+) > s(d-)`` across the sampled group.
- **Composite reward and optimizer**: combine the two objectives as ``R(y, s) = \alpha R_intra + (1 - \alpha) R_inter`` with ``\alpha = 0.75`` and optimize the policy with [[group-relative-policy-optimization]].
- **Training hyperparameters**: SFT uses learning rate ``1e-5``, `1` epoch, warmup ratio ``0.1``, cosine schedule, per-device batch size `1`, and gradient accumulation `8`; RL uses learning rate ``1e-6``, `1` epoch, batch size `256`, micro-batch size `8`, KL coefficient ``0.005``, and temperature ``1.0``.
- **Evaluation protocol**: Retro* reranks the top-`100` documents returned by the first-stage retriever and reports nDCG@10 on [[bright]], with additional robustness checks on BM25, ReasonIR, and BEIR.

## Key Results

- On BRIGHT with BGE-Reasoner-Embed top-`100` candidates, Retro* (7B) reaches `36.6` nDCG@10 and Retro* (32B) reaches `38.5`, outperforming ReasonRank by `+3.1` and `+1.9` points respectively.
- Test-time scaling with mean-score@`16` raises BRIGHT performance to `38.7` for 7B and `40.6` for 32B; the scaled 7B model (`38.7`) slightly exceeds the unscaled 32B model (`38.5`).
- With alternative first-stage retrievers, Retro* remains strong: on BM25 candidates it gets `35.3` / `37.0` (7B @1 / @16) and `36.6` / `38.5` (32B @1 / @16); on ReasonIR candidates it gets `36.8` / `38.4` and `37.4` / `39.5`.
- Scaling trends are monotonic: under single-sample inference, BRIGHT average nDCG@10 increases from `32.4` (3B) to `36.6` (7B) to `38.5` (32B).
- Ablations on Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct show `22.9` without training, `30.1` with only SFT, `35.1` with only RL, and `36.6` with full SFT+RL; using only intra-reward gives `33.2`, while only inter-reward gives `30.8`.
- On BEIR, Retro* (7B) reaches `55.8` nDCG@10 and improves to `56.8` with mean-score@`16`, indicating the method generalizes beyond BRIGHT-style reasoning-intensive retrieval.

## Limitations

- The method is still a reranker over the first-stage top-`100` candidates; if the retriever misses critical evidence, Retro* cannot recover it.
- Test-time scaling materially improves accuracy, but sampling `K` trajectories per document increases inference compute even if pointwise evaluation parallelizes well.
- The absolute `0-100` scores are rubric-dependent, so calibration quality may vary across tasks and requires a well-specified relevance definition.
- Training is resource-intensive: SFT and RL are run on H100 clusters, and the warm-up stage depends on a strong teacher model (`Qwen3-235B-A22B`) for trajectory generation.
- Evaluation emphasizes BRIGHT and selected BEIR reranking settings rather than full end-to-end agentic RAG pipelines, so downstream task gains are inferred rather than directly measured.
