## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2020; re-verify against recent literature.

REALM introduces retrieval-augmented language-model pre-training that learns a dense retriever and a knowledge-augmented encoder jointly from masked language modeling, without supervised passage labels. For an input `x`, the model retrieves documents `z` from a large corpus and optimizes the marginal likelihood `p(y|x) = Σ_z p(y|z, x) p(z|x)`, so retrieval is rewarded when it improves prediction. To scale to more than 13 million Wikipedia passages, the paper uses inner-product retrieval, approximate top-`k` search with MIPS, and asynchronous index refreshes. After fine-tuning on open-domain QA, REALM reaches `39.2/40.2/46.8` exact match on NaturalQuestions-Open, WebQuestions, and CuratedTrec, substantially outperforming ORQA and even larger parametric baselines such as T5-11B.

## Problem & Motivation

Large pretrained language models can answer factual questions, but the knowledge is stored implicitly in parameters, which makes it hard to inspect, update, or scale without increasing model size. The paper targets a more modular alternative: retrieve textual evidence from an external corpus at prediction time, while still learning retrieval end-to-end during pre-training rather than relying on heuristic sparse retrievers or supervised retrieval labels. The downstream motivation is open-domain question answering, where a system must access facts scattered across millions of documents instead of reading a pre-selected passage.

## Method

- **Latent retrieve-then-predict formulation**: REALM models prediction as `p(y|x) = Σ_{z ∈ Z} p(y|z, x) p(z|x)`, where retrieved document `z` is a latent variable marginalized during both pre-training and fine-tuning.
- **Dense neural retriever**: the retrieval distribution is `p(z|x) = exp(f(x,z)) / Σ_{z'} exp(f(x,z'))` with relevance score `f(x,z) = Embed_input(x)^T Embed_doc(z)`. Both `Embed_input` and `Embed_doc` are BERT-style encoders over `[CLS] ... [SEP]` sequences, followed by learned projections.
- **Knowledge-augmented encoder for MLM**: for masked language modeling, the model concatenates input `x` with retrieved passage body `z_body` and predicts each mask with `p(y_j|z,x) ∝ exp(w_j^T BERT_MASK(j)(join_BERT(x, z_body)))`.
- **Open-QA fine-tuning as span extraction**: for QA, the answer probability sums over matching spans `s ∈ S(z,y)` using `p(y|z,x) ∝ Σ_s exp(MLP([h_start(s); h_end(s)]))`, assuming the answer appears as a contiguous span in a retrieved passage.
- **Approximate marginalization with MIPS**: since summing over all documents is infeasible, REALM marginalizes over the top-`k` documents under `p(z|x)`. Retrieval uses Maximum Inner Product Search over precomputed document embeddings, which makes lookup sub-linear in corpus size.
- **Asynchronous index refresh**: because updating `Embed_doc` makes the MIPS index stale, a background index-builder periodically re-embeds the corpus using a parameter snapshot `θ'` and swaps in a refreshed index. In analysis, roughly one refresh every `~500` steps is important for stable optimization.
- **Inductive biases for meaningful retrieval**: the paper masks salient entity/date spans instead of arbitrary tokens, adds an empty null document `∅` to the top-`k` set, excludes the trivial source document when `X = Z`, and warm-starts the retriever with the Inverse Cloze Task to avoid cold-start collapse.
- **Data and hyperparameters**: the knowledge corpus is the `2018-12-20` English Wikipedia snapshot, chunked into passages of up to `288` BERT wordpieces, yielding just over `13M` retrieval candidates. Pre-training runs for `200k` steps on `64` Cloud TPUs with batch size `512`, learning rate `3e-5`, and top-`8` retrievals (including `∅`); the document-embedding pass is parallelized over `16` TPUs. Fine-tuning inference uses top-`5` passages and fits on a single `12GB` GPU.

## Key Results

- **State of the art on three Open-QA benchmarks**: with `X = Wikipedia, Z = Wikipedia`, REALM achieves `39.2` EM on [[naturalquestions-open]], `40.2` on [[webquestions]], and `46.8` on [[curatedtrec]].
- **Better than the strongest direct baseline**: compared with [[orqa]] (`33.3 / 36.4 / 30.1`), REALM improves by `+5.9 / +3.8 / +16.7` EM on the same three datasets under matched fine-tuning settings.
- **Competitive against much larger parametric models**: REALM at `330M` parameters outperforms [[t5]]-11B on NaturalQuestions (`39.2` or `40.4` vs `34.5`) and WebQuestions (`40.2` or `40.7` vs `37.4`) while using roughly `30x` fewer parameters.
- **Pre-training improves both retriever and encoder**: on NQ development, full REALM reaches `38.2` EM and `38.5` zero-shot Recall@5, versus the ORQA baseline at `31.3` EM and `13.9` Recall@5.
- **Training tricks matter materially**: replacing salient-span masking with random uniform masks drops NQ dev EM from `38.2` to `32.3`, random span masks give `35.3`, and a `30x` staler MIPS index drops EM to `28.7`.
- **Retrieved evidence can sharply raise answer probability**: in the "Fermat prime" example, BERT assigns the correct token probability `1.1 × 10^-14`, whereas REALM's marginalized probability rises to `0.129` after retrieving relevant evidence.

## Limitations

- REALM is expensive to train and maintain: the reported setup needs `200k` pre-training steps on `64` TPUs plus a separate `16`-TPU document-embedding pipeline for index refreshes.
- Performance is sensitive to retrieval infrastructure and training heuristics; stale indexes and weaker masking strategies cause large degradations, so the method is not robust to sloppy systems engineering.
- Fine-tuning assumes the gold answer is a contiguous span in a retrieved passage, which restricts applicability relative to freer-form generative QA settings.
- Experiments focus on three English open-domain QA benchmarks over Wikipedia/CC-News; multilingual, multimodal, and non-QA downstream evaluations are left to future work.
- Even with explicit retrieval, the appendix shows that some predictions remain stale after swapping in a newer corpus because the encoder still memorizes part of the knowledge parametrically.
