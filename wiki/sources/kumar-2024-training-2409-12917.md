## Summary

The paper introduces SCoRe, a two-stage multi-turn reinforcement learning method for teaching a single [[large-language-model]] to revise its own answers without external feedback, teachers, or a separate refinement model. The central claim is that offline revision training fails because it faces both [[distribution-shift]] and [[behavior-collapse]]: the learner either overfits traces produced by another policy or learns to avoid meaningful edits altogether. SCoRe addresses this by optimizing self-generated two-turn rollouts on-policy, first forcing second-turn improvement while regularizing first-turn outputs toward the base model, then jointly optimizing both turns with [[reward-shaping]] that explicitly rewards corrective progress. Across Gemini-based math and coding experiments, the method produces the strongest intrinsic self-correction gains among compared baselines.

## Problem & Motivation

Intrinsic self-correction remains weak in modern LLMs: prompting alone often degrades answers, and prior fine-tuning pipelines either depend on external teachers, separate correction models, or offline traces that do not match the learner's own future mistakes. The paper asks how to train a single model to produce an initial answer and then improve it using only its own generations and a task-specific reward signal. The authors argue that naive [[supervised-fine-tuning]] and standard multi-turn RL both miss the target behavior because they optimize final correctness but do not sufficiently bias training toward learning the algorithmic strategy of correction itself.

## Method

- **Setting**: train a two-attempt policy `π_θ(· | [x, ŷ_{1:l}, p_{1:l}])` with `l = 1`, where turn 2 conditions on the original problem, the first response, and a self-correction instruction.
- **Objective**: optimize multi-turn reward `E[∑_{i=1}^{l+1} r̂(ŷ_i, y*)]` rather than only first-attempt correctness, with binary verifiers for math and coding.
- **Base RL formulation**: use REINFORCE with KL regularization against a reference model, i.e. optimize `r̂(y_t, y*) - β_1 D_KL(π_θ || π_ref)` as the single-turn building block.
- **Failure analysis before SCoRe**: offline STaR- and Pair-SFT-style training improves first responses but often fails to increase `Δ(t1, t2)` because it suffers from [[distribution-shift]] on self-generated errors and collapses to conservative editing behavior.
- **Stage I**: optimize second-attempt reward while constraining the first-attempt distribution to remain close to the base model with a stronger first-turn KL term `β_2`; this is meant to decouple turn-1 and turn-2 behavior and preserve exploratory mistakes that can later be corrected.
- **Stage II**: jointly optimize both attempts with multi-turn RL starting from the Stage I initialization, so the model improves direct answering and revision under its own rollout distribution.
- **Reward shaping**: add a bonus on turn 2, `b̂(y_2 | y_1, y*) = α · (r̂(y_2, y*) - r̂(y_1, y*))`, to reward incorrect-to-correct transitions and penalize correct-to-incorrect revisions; the paper recommends `α > 1`, and uses `α = 10` in experiments.
- **Training details**: MATH uses Gemini 1.5 Flash with `Adam`, `lr = 5e-6`, `steps = 3000`, `batch size = 512`, `temperature = 1.0`, `β_1 = 0.01`, `β_2 = 0.1`; coding uses Gemini 1.0 Pro with `lr = 1e-5`, `steps = 1500`, `batch size = 128`, `β_2 = 0.25`.
- **Evaluation protocol**: math is trained on MATH and reported on MATH500; coding trains on MBPP and tests on HumanEval, with greedy decoding by default and an explicit self-correction prompt at turn 2.
- **Inference-time extension**: the paper also studies combining self-correction with [[self-consistency]]-style sampling under [[test-time-scaling]], comparing `2K` parallel samples against `K` samples each followed by one correction round.

## Key Results

- On MATH, SCoRe reaches `60.0%` Accuracy@t1 and `64.4%` Accuracy@t2, with `Δ(t1, t2) = +4.4%`, versus the base model's `52.6%`, `41.4%`, and `-11.2%`.
- Relative to Gemini 1.5 Flash, SCoRe improves self-correction delta on MATH by `15.6%` absolute and Accuracy@t2 by `23.0%` absolute.
- On MATH, SCoRe increases incorrect-to-correct transitions to `5.8%` and reduces correct-to-incorrect revisions to `1.4%`, compared with `4.6%` and `15.8%` for the base model.
- On HumanEval, SCoRe reaches `64.6%` Accuracy@t2 with `Δ(t1, t2) = +12.2%`, compared with `56.7%` and `+3.0%` for the base model, and outperforms Self-Refine (`52.5%`, `-1.2%`) and Pair-SFT (`54.3%`, `-1.8%`).
- On MBPP-R offline repair, SCoRe improves the base model from `47.3%` to `60.6%`.
- Under a `32`-solution inference budget, pure parallel sampling yields a `7.4%` gain, while parallel sampling plus one correction step yields a `10.5%` gain.
- Ablations show that removing multi-turn training drives `Δ(t1, t2)` to `-2.4%`; removing Stage I lowers it to `2.2%`; removing reward shaping lowers it to `2.6%`.

## Limitations

- Training covers only one explicit correction round; the paper notes that performance beyond two attempts plateaus and is not directly trained.
- The method assumes access to task-specific oracle reward functions during training, which may be unavailable or noisy for many open-ended domains.
- Evidence is limited to math and coding tasks on proprietary Gemini models, so transfer to broader domains, open-weight models, or non-binary rewards is not established.
- The approach introduces a two-stage RL pipeline with KL tuning and reward shaping, increasing training complexity relative to straightforward SFT baselines.
- The authors note that unifying Stage I and Stage II, and scaling to more than two attempts, remain open problems.
