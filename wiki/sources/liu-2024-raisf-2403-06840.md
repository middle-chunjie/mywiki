## Summary

RA-ISF proposes an iterative retrieval-augmented question answering framework that decides, for each question, whether the model already knows the answer, whether retrieved passages are actually relevant, and whether the problem should be decomposed into easier sub-questions. Instead of always appending retrieved text to the prompt, it chains three specialized sub-models: a self-knowledge classifier, a passage-relevance classifier, and a decomposition model. The system is trained from GPT-4-generated supervision and instantiated with Llama 2-7B sub-models over a Contriever-backed Wikipedia corpus. On five open-domain QA benchmarks, RA-ISF substantially outperforms direct prompting and standard RAG on GPT-3.5 and also improves Llama-2 13B, with especially large gains on multi-hop and retrieval-sensitive settings.

## Problem & Motivation

The paper starts from two concrete failures of standard retrieval-augmented generation. First, large language models store incomplete and stale knowledge in their parameters, so purely parametric answering is unreliable for knowledge-intensive queries. Second, naive RAG can degrade performance when the retriever returns irrelevant passages, because the model is forced to reason over noisy context. The authors therefore target a harder objective than ordinary retrieval: deciding when to trust internal knowledge, when retrieved evidence is useful, and when a complex question should be decomposed into simpler sub-problems so that both internal and external knowledge can be fused more effectively.

## Method

- **Overall pipeline**: RA-ISF takes a question `q_new` plus three sub-models `\mathcal{M}_{know}`, `\mathcal{M}_{rel}`, `\mathcal{M}_{decom}`, an answering LLM `\mathcal{M}`, retriever `\mathcal{R}`, and corpus `\mathcal{C}`.
- **Self-knowledge gating**: `\mathcal{M}_{know}(q_t)` predicts whether the model can answer from internal knowledge; if yes, the answer is produced directly as `\mathcal{A} = \arg\max_{a_i} P(a_i \mid q_{new})`.
- **Retrieval and relevance filtering**: when self-knowledge fails, the retriever returns `\mathcal{P} = {p_1, ..., p_k}` and `\mathcal{M}_{rel}` keeps only `\mathcal{P}_{rel}` judged relevant; if `|\mathcal{P}_{rel}| > 0`, the answer is generated as `\mathcal{A} = \arg\max_{a_i} P(a_i \mid q_{new}, \mathcal{P}_{rel})`.
- **Question decomposition fallback**: when all retrieved passages are irrelevant, `\mathcal{M}_{decom}` outputs sub-questions `Q_sub = {q_1, ..., q_n}`; each sub-question is solved recursively and the final answer is generated from `q_{new}`, `Q_sub`, and the collected sub-answers `\mathcal{A}_{sub}`.
- **Stopping rule**: if recursive depth exceeds `D_th`, the branch returns `"unknown"`; the implementation sets the default threshold to `D_th = 3`.
- **Training data construction**: the authors randomly sample `1000` prompts from each dataset and use GPT-4 to generate supervision. For `\mathcal{M}_{know}`, questions are split into `\mathcal{Q}_{know}` and `\mathcal{Q}_{unknow}` by comparing model answers with gold answers. For `\mathcal{M}_{rel}`, GPT-4 labels question-passage pairs as `relevant` or `irrelevant`. For `\mathcal{M}_{decom}`, GPT-4 generates sub-question sets.
- **Sub-model learning**: each sub-model is initialized from Llama 2-7B and trained with cross-entropy, `\min_{\mathcal{M}_{sub}} - \mathbb{E}_{(\mathcal{Q}, \mathcal{A}) \sim \mathcal{D}^{*}} \log P_{\mathcal{M}_{sub}}(\mathcal{A} \mid \mathcal{Q})`, using learning rate `5e-4`.
- **Retriever and corpus**: the retriever is Contriever-MS-MARCO over Wikipedia dated `2018-12-20`; articles are split into non-overlapping `100`-word fragments and retrieval length is set to `l = 64`.
- **Evaluation details**: inference uses greedy decoding throughout, and Exact Match is the reported metric.

## Key Results

- On **GPT-3.5**, RA-ISF reaches average exact match `55.0`, beating Direct (`41.8`), Least-to-most (`46.3`), IRCoT (`46.5`), RAG (`44.2`), and SKR (`47.6`).
- GPT-3.5 per-dataset scores are `40.2` on NQ, `76.1` on TriviaQA, `46.5` on HotpotQA, `75.9` on StrategyQA, and `36.1` on 2WikiMHQA.
- On **Llama-2 13B**, RA-ISF reaches average exact match `46.0`, surpassing RAG (`33.9`), REPLUG (`38.6`), and Self-RAG 13B (`44.1`); it wins on `4/5` datasets, though StrategyQA (`66.7`) is slightly below Self-RAG (`67.2`).
- The paper highlights large retrieval-sensitive gains for GPT-3.5, including `+11.2` over direct RAG on StrategyQA (`75.9` vs. `64.7`) and `+11.9` over direct RAG on TriviaQA (`76.1` vs. `64.2`).
- Ablations show every module matters: removing self-knowledge / passage-relevance / question-decomposition drops GPT-3.5 NQ to `37.9 / 35.8 / 34.6`, TriviaQA to `72.3 / 70.3 / 71.5`, and HotpotQA to `40.1 / 34.7 / 34.9`.
- Smaller intermediate models still work: replacing Llama 2-7B sub-models with T5-780M changes scores from `40.2/76.1/46.5/75.9/36.1` to `39.6/74.8/45.8/74.7/35.3`.
- Human and GPT-4 assessments report high sub-module reliability: human / GPT-4 agreement is `93.5 / 95.0` for `\mathcal{M}_{rel}` and `89.5 / 87.0` for `\mathcal{M}_{decom}`, while GPT-4 gives `97.0` for `\mathcal{M}_{know}`.

## Limitations

- The recursive problem-solving strategy can branch excessively, making inference inefficient when many sub-problems are explored without finding useful evidence.
- The effectiveness of decomposition depends on how the question is phrased; alternative formulations can change both the decomposition path and the final answer quality.
- Evaluation is restricted to open-domain QA benchmarks, so the method is not validated on domains like mathematics, symbolic reasoning, medicine, or law.
- The paper still acknowledges residual factual and reasoning errors from the underlying LLMs; RA-ISF mitigates hallucination but does not eliminate it.
