## Summary

R^2AG addresses the semantic gap between dense retrievers and large language models in standard retrieval-augmented generation, where retrieved documents are simply concatenated and the generator must infer document salience on its own. The method extracts three list-wise retrieval features from retriever embeddings, feeds them through a lightweight Transformer encoder called R2-Former, and projects the resulting retrieval information into the LLM input space through retrieval-aware prompting. Training combines an auxiliary query-document matching objective with language modeling, while allowing frozen retrievers and optionally frozen LLMs for low-resource deployment. Across Natural Questions, HotpotQA, MuSiQue, 2WikiMultiHopQA, and DuReader, the method consistently improves over standard RAG and several enhanced RAG baselines, while adding only about `0.8%` inference latency.

## Problem & Motivation

Standard RAG couples retrieval and generation through text concatenation alone: the retriever ranks documents, then the LLM receives the ranked list without the retrieval-side signals that explain why those documents were selected. The paper argues this creates a semantic gap between encoder-style retrievers and mostly decoder-only LLMs. As a result, the generator must separately filter irrelevant documents, infer cross-document relations, and overcome lost-in-the-middle effects in long contexts. The goal is therefore to expose retrieval-side structure directly to the generator without retraining the retriever and without destructive document compression.

## Method

- **Base formulation**: standard RAG is written as `\hat{y} = f_G(P(q, \mathcal{D}))`, where the prompt template `P` couples query `q` and ranked documents `\mathcal{D} = {d_1, ..., d_k}` only through text.
- **Retriever feature extraction**: encode query and documents as `x^q = f_R(q)` and `x_i^d = f_R(d_i)`. For each document, compute relevance `r_i = sim(x^q, x_i^d)`, precedent similarity `\gamma_i = sim(x_i^d, \sum_{j<i} w_j x_j^d)` with `w_j = exp(r_j) / \sum_{\ell=1}^k exp(r_\ell)`, and neighbor similarity `\zeta_i` from adjacent documents. The list-wise input is `input_i = {r_i, \gamma_i, \zeta_i}`.
- **R2-Former**: map each 3-dimensional feature vector into hidden size `h_1 = 256` with a linear layer, add trainable positional embeddings `p \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times h_1}`, then apply a Transformer encoder with `4` attention heads and dropout `0.1` to obtain retrieval information `H = f_att[f_{\to h_1}(input) + p]`.
- **Retrieval-aware prompting**: project retrieval information into the LLM embedding space by `E^R = f_{\to h_2}(H)`, where `h_2` matches the LLM token embedding dimension. The final prompt embeddings are `E = [E^q, e_1^R, E^{d_1}, ..., e_k^R, E^{d_k}]`, so each document receives a retrieval-information anchor in front of its tokens. The prompt template materializes these anchors with special `<R>` placeholders.
- **Training objective**: add a query-document matching head `\hat{s} = f_{\to 1}(H)` and optimize binary cross-entropy `L_QDM = -\sum_i s_i log \hat{s}_i + (1-s_i) log(1-\hat{s}_i)` jointly with language modeling, yielding `L = L_QDM + L_LM`. The framework supports training only R2-Former with a frozen LLM or jointly fine-tuning both modules.
- **Implementation details**: the retriever is a Sentence-Transformer fine-tuned BERT dual encoder (`bert-base-uncased` for English, `bert-base-chinese` for Chinese) with `768`-dimensional embeddings and cosine similarity. R2-Former uses learning rate `2e-4`, Adam, and greedy decoding is used for all LLM comparisons; English enhanced-RAG experiments use LLaMA2-7B or LongChat-7B-v1.5 as the foundation LLM, while DuReader uses Qwen1.5-0.5B.

## Key Results

- On NQ-10, R2AG reaches `0.6930` accuracy, up from `0.3898` for standard LLaMA2-7B RAG and slightly above ChatGPT at `0.6886`.
- On NQ-20 and NQ-30, R2AG scores `0.7062` and `0.6704` accuracy, improving over LongChat-7B-v1.5 standard RAG (`0.5782`, `0.5198`).
- On HotpotQA, R2AG improves the frozen LLaMA2-7B baseline from `0.2630`/`0.0852` to `0.6675` accuracy / `0.3605` F1.
- On MuSiQue and 2WikiMultiHopQA, R2AG raises frozen-baseline performance from `0.0546`/`0.0241` to `0.1864`/`0.1687`, and from `0.1205`/`0.0634` to `0.3342`/`0.3452`, respectively.
- On DuReader, R2AG improves Qwen1.5-0.5B from `0.1395` F1 / `0.1656` Rouge to `0.1510` / `0.1663`; the joint `R2AG+RAFT` variant reaches `0.2507` / `0.2734`.
- Ablations on NQ show all three retrieval features matter; removing neighbor similarity `\zeta` drops NQ-10 accuracy from `0.6930` to `0.6252` (`-9.78%`), and removing `L_QDM` lowers it to `0.6441` (`-7.07%`).
- The paper reports that adding retrieval information increases inference latency by only about `0.8%`.

## Limitations

- The method depends on dense encoder-style retriever representations; compatibility with sparse retrievers and cross-encoders is left open.
- Performance still depends strongly on the foundation LLM, and strong closed-source models such as GPT-4 remain better on most reported datasets.
- The feature design is hand-crafted around only three retrieval statistics (`r`, `\gamma`, `\zeta`), so the representation space may still be incomplete.
- Evaluation assumes retrieved sets usually contain relevant evidence; behavior when no relevant document is returned is not well studied.
- Experiments are concentrated on QA-style benchmarks, so generality to broader long-form or agentic generation settings is not established.
