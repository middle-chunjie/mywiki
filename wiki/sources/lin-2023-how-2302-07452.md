## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

The paper argues that the usual tradeoff between supervised and zero-shot dense retrieval is not a hard model-capacity limit of BERT-base bi-encoders, but a training-data problem. It reframes recent dense retriever improvements as data augmentation over queries and relevance labels, then shows that diverse supervision from multiple retrievers is more useful than relying on a single strong cross-encoder teacher. The resulting DRAGON recipe mixes large-scale cropped sentences and synthetic queries, and trains with progressive label augmentation from sparse, dense, and late-interaction teachers. On MS MARCO, BEIR, and LoTTE, DRAGON and DRAGON+ reach state-of-the-art or near-state-of-the-art effectiveness for BERT-base-sized dense retrievers while preserving standard dot-product retrieval efficiency.

## Problem & Motivation

Prior dense retrievers often improve either supervised retrieval accuracy or zero-shot transfer, but not both. Some work attributed this to insufficient model capacity and moved toward larger encoders or more expensive late-interaction architectures. This paper challenges that view and asks whether a standard BERT-base dense retriever can become more generalizable through better training data construction. The core motivation is to preserve the deployment advantages of simple bi-encoder retrieval while learning more diverse relevance signals than those provided by a single labeling pipeline or a single query type.

## Method

- **Dense retriever formulation**: uses a bi-encoder with dot-product scoring, `s(q, d) = e_{q[CLS]} · e_{d[CLS]}`, and trains with InfoNCE, `-log exp(s(q,d+)) / (exp(s(q,d+)) + Σ_j exp(s(q,d_j^-)))`.
- **Unified data-augmentation view**: organizes prior dense retrieval training recipes along two axes, query augmentation and relevance-label augmentation, so contrastive pretraining, pseudo-query generation, and distillation become comparable instances of one framework.
- **Query augmentation pool**: studies `28M` cropped sentences from the `8.8M`-passage MS MARCO corpus and `28M` synthetic queries sampled from the doct5query pool; the final DRAGON recipe mixes the two query types `50/50`.
- **Teacher-generated supervision**: retrieves ranking lists for each augmented query from multiple retrievers with different matching biases, including `uniCOIL`, `Contriever`, `GTR-XXL`, `ColBERTv2`, and `SPLADE++`, instead of relying on a cross-encoder only.
- **Positive / negative sampling**: for each chosen teacher ranking list, samples positives from the top `10` passages and hard negatives from ranks `46-50`, extending single-teacher dense-retrieval sampling to diverse supervision.
- **Progressive label augmentation**: rather than fusing all teachers at once, trains in stages and gradually exposes the student to more difficult supervision; the final trajectory is `uniCOIL -> Contriever -> GTR-XXL -> ColBERTv2 -> SPLADE++` with `20` epochs per stage.
- **Training setup**: initializes from `bert-base-uncased`, uses asymmetric dual encoders, in-batch negatives, `32` A100 GPUs, per-GPU batch size `64`, learning rate `3e-5`, max query length `32`, max passage length `128` for MS MARCO and `512` for BEIR.
- **Variants**: `DRAGON-S` uses cropped sentences only, `DRAGON-Q` uses synthetic queries only, `DRAGON` mixes both, and `DRAGON+` keeps the same recipe but switches initialization to `RetroMAE`.

## Key Results

- In the pilot study with cropped-sentence queries, progressive supervision improves BEIR-13 from `47.7` under uniform supervision to `49.3`, while supervised MS MARCO Dev remains competitive at `RR@10 = 36.6`.
- Query scale matters more than query naturalness: scaling cropped sentences from `0.8M` to `28M` produces large gains, and cropped sentences generalize better than human-like synthetic queries in zero-shot retrieval.
- On MS MARCO Dev, `DRAGON` reaches `RR@10 = 39.3` and `R@1K = 98.5`; `DRAGON+` reaches `RR@10 = 39.0` and `R@1K = 98.6`.
- On TREC DL, DRAGON-family models stay strong under graded relevance labels: `DRAGON` reaches `74.1` on DL2019 and `72.9` on DL2020 nDCG@10, while `DRAGON+` reaches `74.4` and `72.3`.
- On zero-shot BEIR, `DRAGON` reaches `49.4` averaged nDCG@10 on BEIR-13 and `46.6` across all `18` datasets; `DRAGON+` further improves BEIR-13 to `50.2` and matches `SPLADE++`.
- On LoTTE pooled evaluation, `DRAGON` reaches `Success@5 = 72.6` for search and `61.4` for forum queries; `DRAGON+` further improves search to `73.5`.
- Cross-encoder augmentation is not necessary in this setup: adding a CE teacher to `DRAGON-S` leaves MS MARCO Dev unchanged at `38.1` and slightly lowers BEIR-13 from `49.8` to `49.7`.

## Limitations

- The recipe is computationally heavy despite keeping inference simple: training uses `32` A100 GPUs, multi-stage supervision, and tens of millions of augmented queries.
- Augmentation is built from the MS MARCO corpus only, so the method is not a direct substitute for explicit target-corpus domain adaptation.
- The study is confined to text retrieval benchmarks; it does not test multimodal or generative retrieval settings.
- The paper shows cross-encoder supervision is suboptimal here, but does not fully explain when cross-encoders might still help under different teacher sets or corpora.
- Soft-label distillation across heterogeneous sparse/dense/multi-vector teachers remains unresolved because their scores are hard to normalize into a shared target space.
