## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023 in a volatile domain (LLM evaluation); re-verify against recent literature.

AGIEval is a bilingual (Chinese/English) benchmark built from 20 real, high-standard human examinations — including Gaokao, SAT, LSAT, GMAT, GRE, AMC/AIME, JEC-QA, and civil service tests — totalling 8,062 objective questions. Unlike artificially curated NLP benchmarks, AGIEval sources directly from exams taken by millions of real test-takers, providing human-level baseline scores. Evaluation of GPT-4, ChatGPT, and Text-Davinci-003 under zero-shot, few-shot, and chain-of-thought settings reveals that GPT-4 surpasses average human performance on SAT and LSAT but trails on complex reasoning tasks (LSAT-AR, Gaokao math, physics). Qualitative analysis along four dimensions — understanding, knowledge, reasoning, and calculation — exposes systematic weaknesses in multi-step logical deduction and domain-specific knowledge recall.

## Problem & Motivation

Standard NLP benchmarks (GLUE, SuperGLUE, SQuAD) rely on artificially curated datasets emphasising simpler textual understanding rather than real-world human reasoning. MMLU partially closes this gap but is English-only and uses unverified data sources. As foundation models approach human-level performance on many synthetic tasks, there is a need for a benchmark grounded in officially validated, high-stakes exams that simultaneously cover multiple cognitive skills (understanding, knowledge, reasoning, calculation) and multiple languages.

## Method

- **Benchmark construction**: 20 tasks from official exams partitioned into five categories — general college entrance (Gaokao 8 subjects, SAT-En, SAT-Math, GRE via AQuA-RAT), law school admission (LSAT-AR, LSAT-LR, LSAT-RC), lawyer qualification (JEC-QA-KD, JEC-QA-CA), math competition (AMC/AIME via MATH dataset, GRE/GMAT via AQuA-RAT), and civil service (LogiQA-En, LogiQA-Zh).
- Only **objective** formats retained (multiple-choice, fill-in-the-blank); subjective questions removed to enable automatic evaluation.
- **Evaluation metrics**: classification accuracy for multiple-choice; Exact Match (EM) and F1 for fill-in-the-blank.
- **Human baselines**: average (50th percentile) and top (1st percentile) human performance estimated by scaling official pass-rate statistics to 100-point scale.
- **Evaluation settings**: zero-shot, few-shot (`k=5` examples), zero-shot CoT, few-shot CoT. CoT elicited with `"Let's think step by step:"` prompt; few-shot CoT explanations sourced from existing rationales (AQuA-RAT, MATH), expert annotation (Gaokao, SAT), or ChatGPT-generated explanations (LSAT, LogiQA, JEC-QA).
- **Models**: GPT-4 (chat completion API, `temp=0`, `max_tokens=2048`), ChatGPT (chat completion / completion API), Text-Davinci-003 (completion API), Vicuna-13B (open-source). All via Azure OpenAI Service with greedy decoding.
- **Data contamination control**: Gaokao timestamped; uncontaminated 2022 subset evaluated separately.

## Key Results

- GPT-4 (zero-shot CoT): average **58.4%** across 20 tasks vs. human average 67% — best among tested models.
- GPT-4 surpasses human average on SAT-Math (`95.0%`), GK-En (`92.5%`), SAT-English (`85.9%`), LSAT-LR (`80.6%`), LSAT-RC (`85.1%`).
- GPT-4 lags human average on MATH (`47.7%` vs. avg 40% — close), GK-Math-Cloze (`15.3%` vs. 73%), GK-physics (`45.5%` vs. 71%), LSAT-AR (`34.4%` vs. 56% avg).
- Few-shot vs. zero-shot: marginal gains, suggesting modern LLMs' zero-shot capability approaches few-shot.
- CoT helps on English math (MATH, AQuA-RAT, SAT-Math) but hurts on Chinese math (GK-Math-Cloze) and Chinese logical tasks.
- Vicuna-13B substantially below all closed-source models despite claimed 90% ChatGPT parity on Open LLM Leaderboard.
- Data contamination analysis: minor accuracy drop on uncontaminated Gaokao 2022 subset (e.g., geography 76.9% → 73%), suggesting benchmark validity is not primarily driven by memorisation.

## Limitations

- Language-only version: multimodal content (diagrams, images in Gaokao physics/math) excluded; limits coverage of visual reasoning.
- Closed-source model opacity: API-only access, no access to training data cuts or internal reasoning.
- Human performance proxies: scaled from aggregate pass-rate statistics, not per-question annotations; may not be perfectly comparable.
- Few-shot demonstrations for some tasks rely on ChatGPT-generated rationales, introducing potential quality variance.
- Data contamination cannot be fully ruled out for non-Gaokao tasks lacking timestamps.
- Benchmark coverage biased toward Chinese and American educational contexts; other regions underrepresented.
