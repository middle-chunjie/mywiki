## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

ALCE introduces a reproducible benchmark for retrieval-grounded long-form generation with inline citations, targeting the practical problem that large language models are fluent but difficult to verify in information-seeking settings. The benchmark pairs each question with a large passage corpus and evaluates end-to-end systems that retrieve evidence, generate multi-statement answers, and cite supporting passages. It covers ASQA, QAMPARI, and ELI5, and scores systems on fluency, task correctness, and citation quality through automatic metrics centered on NLI-based support checks. Experiments with ChatGPT, GPT-4, and open-source models show that citation-grounded generation remains far from solved: retrieval is a major bottleneck, longer context helps only inconsistently, and many outputs remain only partially supported by their cited evidence.

## Problem & Motivation

LLMs are increasingly used for information seeking, but fluent answers without explicit evidence are hard to trust and easy to hallucinate. Prior work on citation-grounded or web-assisted generation often depended on proprietary search engines, closed systems, and expensive human evaluation, which made results difficult to reproduce and compare. This paper frames citation-grounded generation as an end-to-end benchmark problem: retrieve relevant passages, generate a multi-statement answer, and attach citations that actually support each statement. The goal is not only higher factual correctness, but also answer verifiability and faithful grounding in external evidence.

## Method

- **Task formalization**: given query `q` and corpus `D`, the system outputs statements `S = {s_1, ..., s_n}`; each statement `s_i` cites passages `C_i = {c_i,1, c_i,2, ...}` with at most `3` citations per statement. Statements are segmented by sentence boundaries, except QAMPARI where each list item is treated as one statement.
- **Benchmark construction**: ALCE samples `1,000` development examples from each of ASQA, QAMPARI, and ELI5. Corpora are split into `100`-word passages. ASQA and QAMPARI use Wikipedia (`21M` passages); ELI5 uses Sphere (`899M` passages).
- **Fluency metric**: fluency is measured with MAUVE on the concatenation of question and answer; references and model outputs are truncated to `100` words for stability.
- **Correctness metrics**: ASQA uses exact-match recall over gold short answers; QAMPARI uses list precision/recall with `recall-5` capped at `100%` once at least `5` correct answers are present; ELI5 uses claim recall, where InstructGPT `text-davinci-003` generates `3` sub-claims and TRUE checks entailment.
- **Citation quality metrics**: citation recall for statement `s_i` is `1[C_i != empty and φ(concat(C_i), s_i) = 1]`, where `φ` is an entailment model. Citation precision marks a citation `c_i,j` irrelevant if `φ(c_i,j, s_i) = 0` and `φ(concat(C_i \\ {c_i,j}), s_i) = 1`; precision is only credited when statement-level recall is already `1`.
- **Entailment backbone**: both ELI5 claim recall and citation verification use TRUE, a `T5-11B` NLI model. Premises are formatted as `Title: {TITLE}\n{TEXT}` and multiple cited passages are concatenated with newline separators.
- **Retrieval**: the paper uses off-the-shelf retrievers and always retrieves the top `100` passages. Wikipedia experiments use dense retrievers GTR and DPR; Sphere uses BM25.
- **Synthesis strategies**: Vanilla places top-`k` passages directly in context with `2` demonstrations; `k <= 3` for `2K` context models and `k <= 5` for `4K` context models. Summ and Snippet replace passages with ChatGPT-produced summaries or spans and filter passages labeled `irrelevant`; on ASQA, summaries reduce passage length by about `6x`.
- **Interactive variants**: Interact lets the model emit `Check`, `Output`, and `End` actions, with at most `3` checked passages; InlineSearch issues `Search: {query}` over the already retrieved top-`100` passages and removes shown passages after one action to save context.
- **Post-editing and decoding**: Rerank samples `n_sample = 4` candidate answers and selects the one with the best automatic citation recall. PostCite attaches citations to ClosedBook generations by matching each statement to the best top-`100` passage with GTR. Main experiments use `3` random-seed runs; ChatGPT and GPT-4 decode with temperature `0.5`, while open models use nucleus sampling with `top_p = 0.95`.

## Key Results

- **ASQA main baseline**: ChatGPT Vanilla (`5` passages) reaches MAUVE `66.6`, EM recall `40.4`, citation recall `73.6`, and citation precision `72.5`. Adding Rerank raises citation recall to `84.8` and precision to `81.6` while leaving correctness roughly unchanged (`40.2`).
- **QAMPARI trade-off**: ChatGPT Vanilla scores `20.8` recall-5, `20.8` answer precision, `20.5` citation recall, and `20.9` citation precision. Snippet (`10` passages) gives the best ChatGPT recall-5 at `24.5`, while ClosedBook reaches higher recall-5 (`32.9`) but only `10.0` citation recall.
- **ELI5 remains difficult**: ChatGPT Vanilla gets claim recall `12.0`, citation recall `51.1`, and citation precision `50.0`. GPT-4 with `20` passages improves to claim recall `18.3`, citation recall `48.5`, and citation precision `53.4`, while ClosedBook attains the best ChatGPT correctness (`18.6`) but poor citation quality (`15.5`/`15.5`).
- **Retrieval and context limits**: on ASQA, the top-`5` retrieved passages cover only `56.8%` of answers. On ELI5, BM25 recall is just `R@5 = 9.6` and `R@100 = 31.8`, showing retrieval itself is a hard bottleneck.
- **Longer context helps selectively**: on ASQA, GPT-4 improves from EM recall `41.3` with `5` passages to `44.4` with `20` passages, and citation precision from `75.6` to `76.5`. ChatGPT-16K does not benefit similarly: EM recall goes from `36.1` (`5` passages) to `36.1` (`20` passages), while citation precision drops from `76.5` to `73.5`.
- **Automatic metrics track human judgments well**: on ASQA, ChatGPT Vanilla gets human citation recall/precision `74.7/76.6` versus ALCE `75.3/74.4`. Reported Cohen's kappa is `0.698` for citation recall and `0.525` for citation precision, with overall accuracy `85.1%` and `77.6%` respectively against human labels.

## Limitations

- The benchmark evaluates 2023-era models, retrievers, and context windows; its absolute rankings may now be outdated in the fast-moving LLM and retrieval landscape.
- Citation quality depends on automatic NLI judgments from TRUE, which can miss partially supporting evidence and is explicitly acknowledged to be imperfect for borderline cases.
- The benchmark does not provide supervised training data for citations; it focuses on evaluation and prompting-time system design rather than learning citation behavior end-to-end.
- ClosedBook plus post-hoc citation shows that answer correctness and citation faithfulness can diverge sharply, so high factual utility does not imply good evidence attribution.
- Passage-level citations use fixed `100`-word chunks from curated corpora rather than full web pages, which improves reproducibility but only partially matches deployed search assistants.
