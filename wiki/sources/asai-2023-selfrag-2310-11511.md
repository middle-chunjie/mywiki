## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

SELF-RAG trains a language model to decide when retrieval is needed, generate grounded responses, and emit explicit self-critique tokens about relevance, support, and utility. Instead of always prepending a fixed number of passages as in standard retrieval-augmented generation, it expands the LM vocabulary with reflection tokens and supervises them using an offline critic distilled from GPT-4 judgments. At inference, the model performs adaptive retrieval, scores candidate segments with critique-token probabilities, and can trade off citation precision against fluency by adjusting decoding weights. Using Llama 2 7B and 13B backbones, the method improves open-domain QA, fact verification, and long-form cited generation, while substantially increasing citation precision and recall over conventional RAG baselines.

## Problem & Motivation

Standard RAG improves factuality by attaching retrieved documents to the prompt, but it usually retrieves a fixed number of passages whether retrieval is necessary or not. That hurts open-ended tasks, wastes compute, and can inject distracting or irrelevant context. Even when relevant evidence is retrieved, conventional RAG does not explicitly train the generator to judge whether passages are relevant, whether a claim is supported, or whether the resulting answer is actually useful.

SELF-RAG targets this gap by folding retrieval decisions and post-generation critique into the model itself. The core motivation is to keep the versatility of a general-purpose LM while making retrieval conditional, grounding more explicit, and citation quality controllable at inference time.

## Method

- **Reflection-token design**: extend the vocabulary with `Retrieve ∈ {Yes, No, Continue}`, `ISREL ∈ {Relevant, Irrelevant}`, `ISSUP ∈ {Fully supported, Partially supported, No support/Contradictory}`, and `ISUSE ∈ {1,2,3,4,5}`.
- **Sequence formulation**: generate outputs as segments `y = [y_1, ..., y_T]`; in the paper's experiments, one sentence is one segment. The model predicts both normal text and reflection tokens autoregressively.
- **Critic supervision**: build a critic model `C` by prompting GPT-4 to annotate reflection tokens, collecting roughly `4k-20k` supervised instances per token type. The final critic is initialized from `Llama 2 7B` and reaches over `90%` agreement with GPT-4 on most categories.
- **Generator training**: create an augmented corpus `D_gen` by inserting retrieved passages and critic-predicted tokens into `145,619` training examples (`~150k` in the main text) sampled from Open-Instruct plus knowledge-intensive datasets such as Wizard of Wikipedia, Natural Questions, FEVER, OpenBookQA, ARC-Easy, and ASQA.
- **Learning objective**: train the generator `M` with standard next-token prediction, `max_M E_(x,y,r ~ D_gen) log p_M(y,r | x)`, masking retrieved chunks from loss so the model learns to emit the output and reflection tokens rather than copy evidence tokens as targets.
- **Adaptive retrieval**: trigger retrieval when the normalized probability of `Retrieve=Yes` exceeds a threshold `δ`; default `δ = 0.2` for most tasks and `δ = 0` for ALCE/ASQA-style citation-heavy generation.
- **Retriever and evidence budget**: use `Contriever-MS MARCO` by default, retrieving up to `10` documents during setup and typically scoring the top `5` passages at inference; biographies and open-domain QA add another `5` web-search-derived Wikipedia snippets, while ASQA uses author-provided `GTR-XXL` top-5 documents.
- **Critique-guided decoding**: when retrieval is used, process `K` passages in parallel and score each segment with `f(y_t, d, Critique) = p(y_t | x, d, y_<t) + S(Critique)`, where `S(Critique) = sum_(G in {ISREL, ISSUP, ISUSE}) w^G s_t^G`.
- **Score shaping**: normalize desirable-token probabilities, e.g. `s_t^ISREL = p(Relevant) / (p(Relevant) + p(Irrelevant))`; for `ISSUP`, partially supported receives half weight; for `ISUSE`, the five labels are weighted as `{-1, -0.5, 0, 0.5, 1}`.
- **Inference defaults**: set weights `w^ISREL = 1.0`, `w^ISSUP = 1.0`, `w^ISUSE = 0.5`, use segment-level beam width `B = 2`, and greedy token decoding inside each segment.
- **Training setup**: train 7B and 13B generator variants for `3` epochs on `4 x A100 80GB`, with batch size `128`, peak learning rate `2e-5`, `3%` warmup, linear decay, `bf16`, DeepSpeed ZeRO stage 3, and FlashAttention; max length is `2048` for 7B and `1524` for 13B.

## Key Results

- `SELF-RAG 13B` is the best non-proprietary model across all six benchmark families reported in Table 2: `PopQA 55.8`, `TriviaQA 69.3`, `PubHealth 74.5`, `ARC 73.1`, `ASQA rouge 37.0`, `citation precision 70.3`, `citation recall 71.3`.
- `SELF-RAG 7B` is already strong: `PopQA 54.9`, `TriviaQA 66.4`, `PubHealth 72.4`, `ARC 67.3`, `Bio FactScore 81.2`, `ASQA MAUVE 74.3`, `citation precision 66.9`, `citation recall 67.8`.
- Against the strongest comparable 7B retrieval baseline `Llama2-FT 7B`, SELF-RAG 7B improves `PopQA` from `48.7 -> 54.9`, `PubHealth` from `64.3 -> 72.4`, `Bio FactScore` from `78.2 -> 81.2`, and `ASQA citation precision` from `5.0 -> 66.9`.
- On citation-heavy long-form QA, SELF-RAG closes much of the gap to proprietary systems and even exceeds `Ret-ChatGPT` on citation precision (`70.3` vs `65.1` for 13B), though not on recall (`71.3` vs `76.6`).
- Ablations with `50k` training instances show every component matters: removing the retriever drops `PopQA` from `45.5 -> 43.6`, removing the critic drops `ASQA em` from `32.1 -> 18.1`, and replacing critique-guided retrieval with `Retrieve top1` lowers `ASQA em` to `28.6`.
- Analysis of groundedness shows only about `2%` of SELF-RAG's correct open-QA answers are absent from retrieved evidence, versus `15%` for Alpaca, `18%` for `Llama2-chat 13B`, and `20%` for `Alpaca 30B`.

## Limitations

- The supervision pipeline depends on GPT-4-generated reflection labels, so reproducibility and labeling bias still partly inherit properties of a proprietary teacher model.
- Retrieval quality is bounded by the external retriever and Wikipedia-centric corpora; the authors explicitly note limited effectiveness of off-the-shelf retrievers on open-ended instruction-following.
- The model can still emit outputs that are only partially supported or unsupported by citations; the paper positions reflection tokens as mitigation, not a guarantee.
- Inference is more complex than vanilla generation because it requires retrieval, passage-parallel scoring, and segment-level beam search, so latency and systems complexity remain nontrivial.
- Results are centered on zero-shot evaluation over six tasks and a limited set of backbones (`Llama 2 7B/13B`), leaving broader transfer to newer models and domains untested.
