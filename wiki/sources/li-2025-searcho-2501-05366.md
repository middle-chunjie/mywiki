## Summary

Search-o1 augments [[large-reasoning-model]]s with on-demand web retrieval and document-side reasoning to address the knowledge gaps that appear in long o1-style inference. The framework uses [[qwq-32b-preview]] as the backbone model and interleaves reasoning with [[agentic-rag]]: when the model emits a search query, the system retrieves external documents, runs a separate [[reason-in-documents]] pass to distill the useful evidence, and injects the refined result back into the main reasoning chain. Experiments on GPQA, math benchmarks, LiveCodeBench, and six [[open-domain-question-answering]] datasets show consistent gains over direct reasoning and simpler retrieval baselines, especially on multi-hop and knowledge-intensive tasks. The paper is an early explicit attempt to couple LRMs with iterative [[web-search]] rather than one-shot [[retrieval-augmented-generation]].

## Problem & Motivation

The paper targets [[knowledge-insufficiency]] in long-form reasoning: o1-like models can sustain detailed stepwise reasoning, but once a chain hits a missing fact or specialized domain detail, the error can propagate through later steps. The authors show that uncertain expressions appear frequently in difficult GPQA reasoning traces and argue that one-shot RAG is mismatched to such tasks because different reasoning steps need different external facts. Their goal is therefore to let the model decide when to search, retrieve evidence multiple times inside a single reasoning session, and compress noisy documents before they disrupt the original chain of thought.

## Method

- **Task formulation**: the system maps instruction, question, and retrieved documents to reasoning and answer, formalized as `` `(I, q, D) -> (R, a)` `` with autoregressive factorization over the reasoning tokens `` `R_t` `` and answer tokens `` `a_t` ``.
- **Agentic retrieval trigger**: Search-o1 lets the backbone emit a search query `` `q_search^(i) ~ P(q_search^(i) | I, q, R^(i-1))` `` during reasoning; when the sequence contains `` `<|begin_search_query|> ... <|end_search_query|>` ``, generation pauses and retrieval is invoked.
- **Retrieval step**: the search backend returns `` `D^(i) = Search(q_search^(i))` `` with top-`k = 10` documents, using [[bing-web-search-api]] in region `` `US-EN` ``; URLs are converted into readable page text through [[jina-reader-api]].
- **Reason-in-Documents module**: instead of directly inserting long documents, the framework generates an intermediate analysis `` `r_docs^(i)` `` from previous reasoning, the current search query, and retrieved documents, then compresses that into refined knowledge `` `r_final^(i)` ``.
- **Knowledge injection**: the refined step `` `r_final^(i)` `` is inserted back into the main reasoning chain, effectively conditioning later reasoning on the accumulated refined evidence `` `{r_final^(j)}_{j <= i(t)}` `` rather than raw pages.
- **Batch inference**: the algorithm maintains unfinished sequences `` `S` `` and finished sequences `` `F` ``, batching both token generation and document-refinement calls so multiple questions can search and refine in parallel.
- **Backbone and decoding**: experiments use [[qwq-32b-preview]] with `` `max_tokens = 32768` ``, `` `temperature = 0.7` ``, `` `top_p = 0.8` ``, `` `top_k = 20` ``, and repetition penalty `` `1.05` ``.
- **Evaluation setup**: challenging reasoning tasks cover GPQA, MATH500, AMC2023, AIME2024, and LiveCodeBench; open-domain QA covers NQ, TriviaQA, HotpotQA, 2WikiMultihopQA, MuSiQue, and Bamboogle. Experiments run on `` `8 x NVIDIA A800-80GB` `` GPUs.

## Key Results

- On challenging reasoning tasks, Search-o1 reaches GPQA overall `` `63.6` ``, beating direct QwQ-32B `` `58.1` `` and RAgent-QwQ-32B `` `61.6` ``; subject scores are `` `77.9` `` in physics, `` `47.3` `` in chemistry, and `` `78.9` `` in biology.
- On math benchmarks, Search-o1 scores `` `86.4` `` on MATH500, `` `85.0` `` on AMC2023, and `` `56.7` `` on AIME2024.
- On LiveCodeBench, Search-o1 gets `` `33.0` `` overall; it notably improves the medium split to `` `32.4` `` versus `` `29.7` `` for direct QwQ-32B and `` `18.9` `` for RAgent-QwQ-32B.
- Averaged over the five reasoning datasets, the paper reports that Search-o1 exceeds RAgent-QwQ-32B by `` `4.7%` `` and direct QwQ-32B by `` `3.1%` ``.
- On the GPQA extended set, Search-o1 achieves `` `57.9` `` overall, above QwQ-32B `` `51.8` `` and RAG-QwQ-32B `` `54.6` ``; it reaches `` `68.7` `` in physics and `` `69.5` `` in biology.
- On open-domain QA, Search-o1 reports NQ `` `34.0/49.7` `` EM/F1, HotpotQA `` `45.2/57.3` ``, 2Wiki `` `58.0/71.4` ``, MuSiQue `` `16.6/28.2` ``, and Bamboogle `` `56.0/67.8` ``; the gains are strongest on multi-hop settings.

## Limitations

- The paper evaluates a single backbone ([[qwq-32b-preview]]) with a fixed search stack ([[bing-web-search-api]] plus [[jina-reader-api]]), so cross-backbone and cross-retriever generalization remains unclear.
- Benefits are concentrated in complex reasoning and multi-hop QA; for single-hop open-domain QA, Search-o1 does not dominate all baselines, and TriviaQA EM `` `63.4` `` is below RAG-QwQ-32B `` `65.6` ``.
- Even with retrieval, chemistry remains far below human chemists on GPQA extended (`` `40.7` `` vs. `` `72.6` ``), so domain-expert factual reasoning is still a weakness.
- The framework adds latency, token cost, and external dependency overhead because one question may trigger multiple search-and-refinement cycles and uses up to `` `32768` `` generation tokens.
