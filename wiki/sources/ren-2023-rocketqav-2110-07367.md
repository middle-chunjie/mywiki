## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

RocketQAv2 studies joint optimization of a dense retriever and a passage re-ranker instead of training the two stages separately or in alternating frozen-teacher loops. Its main idea is dynamic listwise distillation: the dual-encoder retriever and cross-encoder re-ranker score the same candidate list, convert scores into listwise relevance distributions, and minimize their divergence while still supervising the re-ranker with labeled positives and negatives. The paper also proposes hybrid data augmentation that mixes undenoised hard negatives with denoised instances filtered by RocketQA, aiming to better approximate collection-level difficulty. On MSMARCO and Natural Questions, the method improves both retrieval and reranking, and the authors position it as the first unified joint-training recipe for these two components.

## Problem & Motivation

Dense passage retrieval and passage re-ranking are usually deployed as a retrieve-then-rerank pipeline, but their training objectives are misaligned: the retriever is commonly optimized listwise with many negatives, while the re-ranker is often trained pointwise or pairwise. Prior work transfers signal from a stronger cross-encoder to a dual-encoder retriever, yet typically freezes one module and alternates optimization. The paper argues that this blocks mutual improvement because the retriever and re-ranker should adapt to each other's evolving relevance signals. It therefore seeks a unified training formulation that can jointly optimize both modules and expose them to richer hard-negative distributions.

## Method

- **Task setup**: the retriever is a [[dual-encoder-architecture]] with query encoder `E_Q(.)` and passage encoder `E_P(.)`, scoring passages by `s_de(q,p) = E_Q(q)^T E_P(p)`. The re-ranker is a [[cross-encoder]] that concatenates query and passage with `[SEP]`, then predicts `s_ce(q,p)` from the `[CLS]` representation.
- **Unified listwise view**: for each query `q`, both modules score the same candidate list `P_q = {p_{q,i}}_{1<=i<=m}` and normalize scores into listwise relevance distributions `\\tilde{s}_de(q,p)` and `\\tilde{s}_ce(q,p)` with a softmax over passages in `P_q`.
- **Dynamic listwise distillation**: the core loss matches the retriever and re-ranker distributions via `L_KL = \\sum_{q,p} \\tilde{s}_de(q,p) log(\\tilde{s}_de(q,p) / \\tilde{s}_ce(q,p))`, so the two modules continuously adapt rather than distilling from a frozen teacher.
- **Supervised reranker objective**: the re-ranker is additionally trained with listwise cross-entropy `L_sup = -(1/N) \\sum_{q,p^+} log( e^{s_ce(q,p^+)} / (e^{s_ce(q,p^+)} + \\sum_{p^-} e^{s_ce(q,p^-)} ) )`, and the final objective is `L_final = L_KL + L_sup`.
- **Hybrid data augmentation**: to build stronger candidate lists, [[rocketqa]] first retrieves top-`n` passages; the method mixes undenoised instances formed by random hard-negative sampling with denoised instances where the RocketQA re-ranker filters low-confidence negatives and keeps high-confidence positives.
- **Initialization and backbone**: both modules are initialized from RocketQA's pretrained retriever and re-ranker, and the implementations follow [[ernie-2-0]] base, a 12-layer BERT-like transformer with continual pretraining.
- **Training details**: optimization uses Adam with `lr = 1e-5`, trains up to `3` epochs with `batch_size = 96`, applies cross-encoder dropout `0.1`, and uses positive:hard-negative ratios of `1:127` on [[msmarco]] and `1:31` on [[natural-questions]]; experiments run on up to `32` NVIDIA Tesla V100 GPUs with `32 GB` RAM each.

## Key Results

- **MSMARCO retrieval**: RocketQAv2 retriever reaches `MRR@10 = 38.8`, `Recall@50 = 86.2`, and `Recall@1000 = 98.1`, outperforming RocketQA (`37.0 / 85.5 / 97.9`) and PAIR on `MRR@10` (`37.9`).
- **Natural Questions retrieval**: the retriever reports `Recall@5 = 75.1`, `Recall@20 = 83.7`, and `Recall@100 = 89.0`, improving over RocketQA's `74.0 / 82.7 / 88.5`.
- **MSMARCO reranking**: the RocketQAv2 re-ranker scores `MRR@10 = 41.9` when reranking top-`50` candidates from the RocketQAv2 retriever, `41.8` on top-`50` candidates from RocketQA, and `40.1` on top-`1000` BM25 candidates.
- **Ablations on joint training**: the retriever drops from `37.4` to `36.0` `MRR@10` under static distillation, to `36.3` under pointwise training, and to `36.3` without denoised instances; `R@50` drops from `84.9` to `84.5`, `83.9`, and `84.9` respectively.
- **Scaling signal**: increasing the number of instances per query, i.e. hard negatives, consistently improves both retrieval and reranking in the paper's analysis, while adding in-batch negatives does not produce additional gains.

## Limitations

- The method still depends on a staged retrieve-then-rerank pipeline at inference time; it is not a single end-to-end differentiable retriever-reader system.
- Joint training quality depends on candidate-list construction, especially the quality of hard negatives and denoising from the RocketQA teacher components.
- Experiments cover only [[msmarco]] and [[natural-questions]], so robustness to other domains, multilingual retrieval, and non-QA ranking settings is not established.
- The paper analyzes only ERNIE-2.0-base-scale models and does not quantify latency or memory tradeoffs of joint training relative to simpler distillation baselines.
- The paper is a 2023 preprint in a fast-moving IR/NLP area, so several architectural assumptions should be rechecked against later retrieval and reranking work.
