## Summary

Phi-4 is a 14B-parameter decoder-only [[large-language-model]] whose main contribution is a training recipe centered on data quality rather than architectural novelty. The report argues that large allocations of high-quality [[synthetic-data]], carefully filtered organic corpora, and a revised [[pretraining-data-mixture]] can push a small model beyond its teacher on STEM-heavy evaluation. The pipeline combines 10T-token pretraining, 4K-to-16K [[long-context-training]], 8B-token [[supervised-fine-tuning]], and two rounds of [[direct-preference-optimization]], including the paper's new [[pivotal-token-search]] method for token-level preference pair construction. Phi-4 reaches 84.8 MMLU, 56.1 GPQA, 80.4 MATH, and 82.6 HumanEval, while the authors openly note persistent weaknesses in strict instruction following, factual hallucination, and safety completeness.

## Problem & Motivation

The report targets a concrete scaling question: can a relatively small open-weight model close much of the gap to frontier systems without materially increasing parameter count? The authors argue that raw web-scale pretraining is no longer the only lever. Instead, they focus on higher-yield training tokens, especially reasoning-oriented synthetic corpora, stronger filtering of organic sources, better decontamination, and post-training methods that improve reasoning quality rather than just imitate a stronger teacher. The motivation is both scientific and practical: obtain high reasoning performance at substantially lower inference cost and latency than long-chain-of-thought or very large dense models.

## Method

- **Base model**: decoder-only transformer with `14B` parameters, default context length `4096`, later midtrained to `16K`; tokenizer switched to `tiktoken` with padded vocabulary size `100,352`; phi-4 uses full attention at `4K` instead of the `2K` sliding window used in phi-3-medium.
- **Pretraining horizon**: approximately `10T` tokens with linear warm-up and decay, peak learning rate `3e-4`, weight decay `0.1`, and global batch size `5760`.
- **Pretraining data mixture**: `40%` synthetic, `15%` web rewrites, `15%` filtered web, `20%` code, `10%` acquired sources; unique-token counts are roughly `290B`, `290B`, `1.3T`, `820B`, and `580B` respectively, implying repeated epochs over the higher-value synthetic clusters.
- **Synthetic-data pipeline**: seeds come from curated books, papers, code, forums, and Q&A; generation uses multi-agent prompting, rewrite-and-augment workflows, self-revision loops, question extraction from reasoning chains, and [[instruction-reversal]] for code-to-instruction synthesis.
- **Organic data processing**: the team emphasizes [[data-curation]] and [[data-decontamination]], including targeted acquisitions, small-classifier filtering of web dumps, multilingual quality filtering over `176` languages, custom parsers for TeX/PDF/Word/HTML, and benchmark decontamination against sets such as MMLU, GSM8K, GPQA, HumanEval, and MATH.
- **Midtraining for long context**: context increases from `4K` to `16K`; the midtraining mixture is `30%` newly curated long-context data plus `70%` recall tokens from pretraining; RoPE base frequency is raised to `250K`; maximum learning rate is reduced by `10x`; total midtraining budget is `250B` tokens.
- **SFT stage**: one round of [[supervised-fine-tuning]] on about `8B` chat-formatted tokens with learning rate `1e-6`, covering math, coding, reasoning, dialogue, identity, safety, and `40` languages.
- **DPO stage 1**: first round of [[direct-preference-optimization]] uses [[pivotal-token-search]] plus safety data; Table 7 reports `132,859` generic multiple-choice samples, `76,552` math, `16,080` Python, `21,806` multi-language code, and `3,000` unknown plus safety items.
- **Pivotal Token Search**: given query `Q` and completion `T_full = t_1, t_2, ...`, recursively split token spans until the success-probability gap `|p(success | prefix) - p(success | prefix + span)| < p_gap` or span length is `1`; convert pivotal single-token decisions into `(accepted, rejected)` DPO pairs at the exact divergence point.
- **DPO stage 2**: judge-guided DPO builds about `850k` pairs from GPT-4o, GPT-4t, and phi-4 outputs, labeled by GPT-4o on accuracy, style, and detail; Table 8 lists `532,000` accuracy-based pairs, `266,000` overall-comparison pairs, and `43,842` unknown plus safety examples.
- **Hallucination mitigation**: dedicated SFT and DPO data encourage refusal when the model lacks knowledge, explicitly trading benchmark F1 on SimpleQA for lower hallucination rates and better user-facing behavior.

## Key Results

- Academic benchmarks: `84.8` MMLU, `56.1` GPQA, `80.4` MATH, `82.6` HumanEval, `80.6` MGSM, `70.4` MMLU-Pro, `82.8` HumanEval+, `75.4` ArenaHard, `47.6` LiveBench, `63.0` IFEval, and `56.2` PhiBench.
- Teacher comparison: phi-4 exceeds GPT-4o on GPQA (`56.1` vs `50.6`) and MATH (`80.4` vs `74.6`), which the authors use as evidence that the recipe goes beyond simple distillation.
- In-class comparison: phi-4 beats Qwen-2.5-14B-Instruct on `9/12` reported benchmarks and substantially improves over phi-3-14B on GPQA (`31.2` to `56.1`) and MATH (`44.6` to `80.4`).
- Fresh-math validation: on the November 2024 AMC-10/12 tests, phi-4 scores above similar-size and some larger models, supporting the claim that MATH performance is not only contamination-driven.
- Pretraining ablations: a synthetic-only `13B` ablation improves HumanEval by `+12.1` and MMLU-Pro by `+4.0` relative to phi-3-medium but drops TriviaQA by `-14.8`, motivating a mixed recipe rather than pure synthetic training.
- Long-context HELMET results: at `16K`, phi-4 records `77.0` ICL F1, `36.0` document QA, and `40.5` summarization, while at `8K` it obtains `58.1` RAG and `65.3` rerank.
- Post-training ablation: pivotal-token DPO is especially useful on reasoning-heavy tasks, lifting GPQA from SFT-only `47.3` to stage-1 DPO `53.6`, while the final two-stage recipe reaches `56.1`.
- Safety metrics: on the internal RAI benchmark, phi-4 achieves grounding `4.619`, jailbreak defect rate `0.073`, and third-party content harm defect rate `0.121`, generally competitive with or better than comparable small open models.

## Limitations

- The paper is primarily a technical report on recipe and evaluation; many implementation details of data pipelines, filtering heuristics, and dataset composition remain high level and are not independently reproducible.
- Strict instruction following is a stated weakness: phi-4 underperforms on IFEval and can deviate from precise formatting or structural requirements.
- Factual hallucination remains unresolved, especially on rare-entity or knowledge lookup prompts; the authors explicitly say search augmentation would still be needed.
- Several evaluations rely on internal benchmarks, internal judge prompts, or LLM-as-judge protocols, which limits external auditability.
- Safety improvements are incomplete; the report notes remaining risks in bias, harmful content generation, and adversarial robustness despite post-training and red-teaming.
- The model is tuned mainly for single-turn helpful reasoning, so conversational efficiency and concise interaction quality can still suffer.
