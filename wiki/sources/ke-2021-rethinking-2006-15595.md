## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2021; re-verify against recent literature.

TUPE revisits positional encoding in BERT-style language pre-training and argues that adding word and positional embeddings at the input mixes heterogeneous signals inside attention. The method computes content and position correlations separately, using dedicated projections `U^Q, U^K` for positions and optionally adding relative bias `b_{j-i}`. It also resets position-only scores involving `[CLS]` with learnable `θ_1, θ_2` so sentence aggregation is less biased toward early tokens. On GLUE, TUPE-A and TUPE-R consistently outperform BERT baselines, and the `300k`-step TUPE checkpoints already match or exceed `1M`-step baselines, suggesting both a better inductive bias over order and faster pre-training convergence.

## Problem & Motivation

BERT-style encoders rely on positional information, but the standard absolute formulation adds positional embeddings directly to word embeddings before self-attention. The paper argues that this creates mixed word-to-position and position-to-word correlations that are both hard to interpret and empirically noisy. It further argues that `[CLS]` should not inherit the same positional treatment as ordinary tokens because its role is to summarize the entire sequence for sentence-level tasks. The goal is therefore to encode order information without entangling heterogeneous signals and without biasing `[CLS]` toward the first few positions.

## Method

- **Correlation decomposition**: expand absolute positional encoding in the first layer as `((w_i + p_i)W^Q)((w_j + p_j)W^K)^T / sqrt(d)`, yielding word-to-word, word-to-position, position-to-word, and position-to-position terms; TUPE removes the two mixed middle terms.
- **Untied absolute encoding**: compute attention scores as `α_ij = (x_i^l W^{Q,l})(x_j^l W^{K,l})^T / sqrt(2d) + (p_i U^Q)(p_j U^K)^T / sqrt(2d)`, where `U^Q, U^K ∈ R^{d×d}` are separate projections for position embeddings.
- **Untied relative variant**: TUPE-R adds a relative bias term, `α_ij = ... + b_{j-i}`, combining absolute position correlation with clipped relative position bias.
- **Untied `[CLS]` handling**: define position-only scores `v_ij` and apply `reset_θ(v, i, j)`, where non-`[CLS]` pairs keep `v_ij`, `[CLS] -> others` uses `θ_1`, and `others -> [CLS]` uses `θ_2`.
- **Multi-head / parameterization details**: absolute position embeddings `p_i` are shared across heads; each head keeps its own `U^Q`, `U^K`, relative bias `b_{j-i}`, and reset parameters. `U^Q` and `U^K` are shared across layers, adding about `1.18M` parameters (`2 × 768 × 768`), roughly `1%` of BERT-Base.
- **Training setup**: BERT-Base backbone with `12` layers, hidden size `768`, `12` heads, vocabulary `32,768`; pre-train on English Wikipedia + BookCorpus (`~16 GB`) for `1M` steps with batch size `256`, sequence length `512`, MLM rate `0.15`, Adam with `ε = 1e-6`, `β = (0.9, 0.999)`, peak learning rate `1e-4`, `10k` warmup, dropout `0.1`, weight decay `0.01`, clip norm `1.0`, and `16` V100 GPUs in mixed precision.

## Key Results

- TUPE-R reaches `85.04` average GLUE score, beating BERT-R at `83.66` by `+1.38`; TUPE-A reaches `84.47`, beating BERT-A at `82.96` by `+1.51`.
- TUPE-R improves especially on CoLA (`63.56` vs `55.43` for BERT-R), RTE (`73.56` vs `71.46`), and MRPC (`89.89` vs `89.26`).
- `300k`-step TUPE-R already gets `83.68` average GLUE, slightly above the full `1M`-step BERT-R baseline (`83.66`), indicating substantially faster convergence.
- Removing the `[CLS]` reset hurts low-resource tasks: TUPE-A gets `63.09` on CoLA and `71.61` on RTE, while TUPE-A-tie-cls drops to `59.46` and `69.54`.
- Appendix results show the pattern persists beyond BERT-Base: TUPE-Large reaches `87.39` vs `85.82` for BERT-Large, and ELECTRA-TUPE reaches `86.07` vs `85.38` for ELECTRA-Base.

## Limitations

- The central claim about noisy word-to-position and position-to-word interactions is supported mainly by visualization and ablation, not by a formal causal analysis of why those terms hurt optimization.
- Evaluation is concentrated on encoder-only language pre-training and GLUE-style downstream tasks; the paper does not test generation, multilingual transfer, or long-context settings.
- TUPE still depends on explicit absolute position embeddings and quadratic self-attention, so it does not address the broader scalability issues of Transformer encoders.
- The strongest evidence is against older BERT/RoBERTa-era baselines trained on `~16 GB` corpora, so the gains should be re-checked against newer encoder pre-training recipes.
