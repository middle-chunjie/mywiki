## Summary

Q-RAG proposes a resource-efficient multi-step retrieval framework that trains the retriever rather than the LLM. The method formulates chunk selection as a finite-horizon MDP whose state contains the query plus previously retrieved chunks, scores candidates with a factored value function `` `Q_theta(s, a^i) = <E_s(s), E_a(a^i, i)>` ``, and optimizes the embedder with soft value-based reinforcement learning and `lambda`-returns. A relative positional encoding over retrieved facts lets the agent reason about before/between/after relations in long narratives. Across Babilong, RULER, HotpotQA, and MuSiQue, Q-RAG reaches near-perfect needle-in-a-haystack retrieval up to `1M` tokens, competitive open-domain QA performance, and stronger long-context generalization than recurrent-transformer and LLM-agent baselines, while training on a single `A100-80GB` GPU.

## Problem & Motivation

Most RAG pipelines still rely on single-step retrieval, which is inadequate for questions whose evidence must be gathered sequentially across a long document or corpus. Prior multi-step systems typically either build expensive knowledge graphs over the full context, use brittle LLM-agent loops that compound retrieval noise across steps, or fine-tune the LLM itself, which is costly and impractical for large or proprietary models. Q-RAG targets this gap by moving the optimization burden from the generator to the retriever: it learns a compact retrieval policy directly in embedding space, aiming to preserve multi-step search quality while remaining cheap to train and easy to pair with arbitrary downstream LLMs.

## Method

- **Retrieval as an MDP**: the document is segmented into non-overlapping chunks `` `C = {c^(i)}_(i=1)^m` ``, the initial state is `` `s_0 = [q]` ``, transitions are deterministic `` `p(s_t, a_t) = ord([q, a_0, ..., a_t])` ``, and episodes stop either after `T` retrieval steps or a special Stop action.
- **Reward design**: with support-fact supervision, intermediate rewards are `0` and the terminal reward is `1` only if all gold support facts appear in the final retrieved state; the paper explicitly leaves LLM-based answer rewards for future work.
- **Factored Q-function**: Q-RAG uses separate state and action embedders, with `` `E_s(s_t; theta_1) in R^d` `` and `` `E_a(a^i, i; theta_2) in R^d` ``, and scores actions by inner product `` `Q_theta(s, a^i) = <E_s(s; theta_1), E_a(a^i, i; theta_2)>` ``.
- **Soft value-based RL**: chunk selection follows a Boltzmann policy `` `pi(a_t | s_t) propto exp((Q_theta(s_t, a_t) - q) / alpha)` `` with `` `q = max_a Q_theta(s_t, a)` ``; the entropy temperature `alpha` is annealed during training to balance exploration and exploitation.
- **TD training objective**: the method adapts PQN to this setting, adds target networks, and trains with `lambda`-returns plus the soft value estimate `` `V_theta'(s_t) = alpha log sum_a exp(Q_theta'(s_t, a) / alpha)` ``; the optimization loss is `` `L_Q = E[(Q_theta(s_t, a_t) - G_t^lambda)^2]` ``.
- **Temporal reasoning mechanism**: to capture narrative ordering, absolute chunk index `i` is replaced by relative position `` `rho_t(i) = j delta + l (i - b_j) / (b_(j+1) - b_j)` `` with `delta = 10` and `l = 9`, encoding whether a candidate lies before, between, or after already retrieved evidence.
- **Training recipe**: Appendix C reports `AdamW` with `` `lr = 1.5e-5` ``, `` `beta_1 = 0.9` ``, `` `beta_2 = 0.98` ``, `` `eps = 1e-6` ``, weight decay `` `5e-4` ``, warmup `1000` steps, gradient clipping `` `||g||_2 <= 2.0` ``, gradient accumulation `8`, effective batch size `96`, and core RL hyperparameters `` `gamma = 0.99` ``, `` `alpha = 0.05` ``, `` `lambda = 0.5` ``, `` `tau = 0.02` ``.
- **Backbones and inference**: the open-domain QA model uses `multilingual-e5-large`, the long-context model uses `facebook/contriever`, action representations are capped at `220` tokens, and Appendix B adds a beam-search planning variant that expands top-`k` actions by learned `Q` values without transformer reranking.

## Key Results

- **RULER NIAH**: Q-RAG reaches `100.0` average NIAH score at `4K`, `16K`, `32K`, and `128K` context length, and still scores `99.7` at `1M`; on the RULER multi-hop QA subtask it reports `67`, `67`, `67`, `62`, and `57` at `4K`, `16K`, `32K`, `128K`, and `1M`.
- **Open-domain QA**: on HotpotQA it achieves fact `F1/EM = 0.93/0.89` and answer `F1/EM = 0.76/0.59`; on out-of-distribution MuSiQue it reaches fact `F1/EM = 0.71/0.55` and answer `F1/EM = 0.52/0.37`, improving over Beam-Retriever's `0.40/0.27` answer `F1/EM` on MuSiQue.
- **Ablation on Babilong QA3**: the full model scores `97.1 +/- 0.08` at `32K`, `96.8 +/- 0.08` at `128K`, and `96.5 +/- 0.16` at `1M`; removing Soft-Q drops `32K` performance to `94.5 +/- 0.50`, and removing target networks drops it much further to `77.6 +/- 27.2`.
- **Generalization to extreme length**: the long-context retriever is trained on `4K` documents but maintains nearly perfect NIAH accuracy up to `1M` tokens and, according to the Babilong figures, remains competitive out to `10M` tokens.
- **Efficiency**: end-to-end training of a single model stays within `12` hours on one `A100-80GB`, while the paper contrasts this with recent RL-based retrievers that typically report training on roughly `8 x A100`.

## Limitations

- The reward function depends on gold support facts; the paper does not implement answer-based or LLM-judged rewards, so the training setup assumes structured supervision.
- The method requires pre-segmented, non-overlapping chunks and does not study chunk overlap, adaptive chunking, or end-to-end segmentation.
- Open-domain answer metrics are competitive rather than uniformly dominant: on HotpotQA answer `F1/EM`, Q-RAG is slightly below Beam-Retriever (`0.76/0.59` vs. `0.77/0.61`).
- The baseline set is heterogeneous because some comparison numbers are reproduced while others are only reported from prior papers, which weakens strict apples-to-apples claims.
- Q-RAG optimizes retrieval only; final answer generation still depends on an external LLM, so gains are not a complete end-to-end QA solution by themselves.
