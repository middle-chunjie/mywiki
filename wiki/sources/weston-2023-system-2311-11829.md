## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

The paper proposes System 2 Attention (S2A), a two-stage prompting method that treats an instruction-tuned large language model as a deliberate context filter before final generation. Instead of answering directly from the original prompt `x`, the model first regenerates a cleaned context `x'` that preserves relevant evidence while removing opinions or distractors, and only then answers from `x'`. Using Llama-2-70B-chat as both the rewriter and answerer, the method improves robustness to sycophancy and irrelevant context across modified TriviaQA, longform argument evaluation, and GSM-IC math word problems. Reported gains include higher factual QA accuracy, more neutral longform responses, and better math accuracy under distractors, with the main tradeoff being extra inference cost and prompt sensitivity.

## Problem & Motivation

Transformer soft attention spreads probability mass across broad context regions, which can amplify spurious correlations, repeated content, and user opinions rather than isolating task-relevant evidence. The paper argues that this makes large language models vulnerable to sycophancy in factual QA, sentiment leakage in longform generation, and distractor sentences in math word problems. The motivation behind S2A is to insert a more deliberate reasoning step before response generation, so the model explicitly decides what text should be attended to rather than relying only on standard soft attention over the original prompt.

## Method

- Problem setup: given input context `x`, a standard model answers with `y ~ LLM(x)`, whereas S2A first rewrites the context as `x' ~ S2A(x)` and then answers with `y ~ LLM(x')`.
- Prompted implementation: the paper instantiates the rewriter with the same instruction-tuned model, i.e. `S2A(x) = LLM(P_{S2A}(x))`, where `P_{S2A}` asks the model to extract unbiased or relevant context and preserve the actual question.
- Two-step prompting for QA and longform generation: step 1 uses the Figure 2 template to separate regenerated context from the query; step 2 uses an unbiased-answer prompt over `x'` instead of the original opinionated input.
- Relevance-focused math variant: for GSM-IC, the step-1 prompt is changed to keep only text useful for solving the problem, explicitly targeting irrelevant distractor sentences rather than opinion removal.
- Experimental model and decoding: baseline, oracle, and both S2A stages all use `LLaMA-2-70B-chat` with `temperature = 0.6` and `top-p = 0.9`.
- Evaluation protocol: factual QA uses `200` modified TriviaQA prompts; longform generation uses `200` SycophancyEval prompts; math uses `100` GSM-IC problems averaged over `3` random seeds.
- Metrics: factual QA is judged by `GPT-4`; longform quality is scored `0-5` by `GPT-4`; objectivity is derived from a `GPT-4` sentiment score `S in [-5, 5]` via `5 - |S|`.
- Variants and ablations: the paper compares default S2A against `S2A-Single`, `S2A-KeepOrig`, `S2A-NI`, direct instructed prompting, and zero-shot chain-of-thought prompting.

## Key Results

- Factual QA on modified TriviaQA: baseline accuracy drops to `62.8%`, oracle reaches `82.0%`, and S2A recovers performance to `80.3%`.
- Longform argument generation: quality stays similar across methods (`4.7` baseline, `4.6` oracle, `4.6` S2A out of `5`), but objectivity improves from `2.23` to `3.82`, exceeding the oracle score of `3.0`.
- GSM-IC with distractors: for in-topic irrelevant sentences, accuracy improves from `51.7%` to `61.3%`; the paper also reports roughly `+12` points on random distractors and `+10` points on in-topic distractors.
- Ablations: `S2A-KeepOrig` falls to `74.5%` overall factual QA accuracy versus `80.3%` for default S2A, showing that retaining the original biased context weakens debiasing.
- Direct instructed prompting helps but is insufficient: factual QA rises from `62.8%` to `71.7%`, still well below S2A, and remains skewed by suggested answers.

## Limitations

- S2A adds a full extra generation step, so inference cost is higher than standard single-pass prompting.
- The method can still fail to remove harmful or irrelevant context, so it does not eliminate spurious correlations reliably.
- Because step 1 rewrites or copies context, weaker models may omit or truncate necessary evidence during regeneration.
- Performance depends heavily on zero-shot prompt design; the paper does not optimize prompts systematically or train a dedicated rewriter.
- The study uses a narrow evaluation suite centered on bias and distractor robustness rather than a broad coverage of downstream LLM tasks.
