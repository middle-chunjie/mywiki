## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

This technical report introduces phi-1.5, a `1.3B`-parameter Transformer language model trained on `30B` unique tokens and `150B` total training tokens, with the central claim that carefully curated textbook-like synthetic data can close much of the capability gap to models `5-10x` larger. The model uses the phi-1 architecture and mixes phi-1 data with newly generated synthetic corpora targeting commonsense reasoning and world knowledge, while separate ablations probe filtered web data. Across commonsense, language understanding, math, and coding benchmarks, phi-1.5 is broadly competitive with `7B-13B` open models and substantially stronger than prior `1.3B` baselines, while also showing lower toxicity propensity than Llama2-7B and Falcon-7B. The paper argues that data quality, not only scale, is a primary driver of small-model capability.

## Problem & Motivation

The paper asks whether high-level LLM capability really requires ever-larger parameter counts and web-scale corpora, or whether better data can substitute for much of that scale. This matters economically because large models are expensive to train and deploy, scientifically because it clarifies what drives capability, and from a responsible-AI perspective because smaller models are easier to study and govern. The authors specifically target common-sense reasoning, an ability that is difficult for small models and poorly explained by fluent text generation alone. They also want a research-friendly model small enough for mechanistic interpretability, in-context learning analysis, and safety studies, while reducing dependence on noisy web data that may amplify toxicity and bias.

## Method

- **Backbone**: decoder-only [[transformer]] with `24` layers, `32` attention heads, head dimension `64`, rotary dimension `32`, and context length `2048`; phi-1.5 reuses the phi-1 architecture rather than introducing a new backbone.
- **Attention/training efficiency**: uses [[rotary-positional-embedding]] and [[flash-attention]] for faster training; tokenizer is inherited from CodeGen-Mono.
- **Training data for phi-1.5**: combines phi-1 training data (`7B` tokens) with roughly `20B` newly created textbook-like [[synthetic-data]] focused on common sense, science, daily activities, theory of mind, and general world knowledge; the only non-synthetic component is `6B` filtered code tokens inherited from phi-1.
- **Topic generation pipeline**: synthetic data creation starts from about `20K` seed topics and uses prompts designed to generate diverse instructional or explanatory material rather than raw web-style text.
- **Optimization**: trained from random initialization with constant learning rate `2e-4`, no warmup, weight decay `0.1`, Adam with betas `0.9, 0.98`, epsilon `1e-7`, `fp16`, and DeepSpeed ZeRO Stage `2`.
- **Batching / token budget**: batch size `2048`; total training is `150B` tokens, sampled `80%` from the new synthetic corpus and `20%` from phi-1 data, implying multiple passes over the `30B`-token dataset.
- **Web-data ablations**: phi-1.5-web-only is trained on `95B` filtered web/code tokens (`88B` Falcon RefinedWeb + `7B` code), while phi-1.5-web mixes data in roughly `40%` filtered web, `20%` phi-1 code, `40%` synthetic proportions to isolate the value of web data versus textbook-style data.
- **Evaluation setup**: reports mostly zero-shot accuracy via standard benchmark suites, with `2`-shot MMLU and pass@1 for coding tasks; prompt examples also show emergent [[chain-of-thought-prompting]] and limited instruction following despite no alignment stage.

## Key Results

- **Compute efficiency**: phi-1.5 needs about `1.5K` GPU hours on a single A100-80G, versus `>80K` GPU hours for Llama-7B; inference is reported as `<3 ms` per token and `3.5G` memory at context `2048`.
- **Commonsense benchmarks**: phi-1.5 scores `0.734` WinoGrande, `0.756` ARC-Easy, `0.444` ARC-Challenge, `0.758` BoolQ, and `0.526` SIQA, roughly matching Llama2-7B (`0.691/0.763/0.434/0.779/0.480`) and outperforming Falcon-7B on all five listed tasks.
- **Language understanding**: phi-1.5 reaches `0.766` PIQA, `0.476` HellaSwag, `0.376` MMLU, `0.372` OpenBookQA, and `0.72` SQuAD EM; phi-1.5-web improves some of these to `0.770`, `0.484`, `0.379`, `0.360`, and `0.74`.
- **Reasoning/coding**: phi-1.5 gets `40.2` GSM8K (via coding), `34.1` HumanEval, and `37.7` MBPP, beating all listed small/medium open models and even surpassing Llama-65B on HumanEval (`23.7`) and MBPP (`37.7` tie / exceed depending on rounding).
- **Web-data ablation**: phi-1.5-web-only already beats other `1.3B` baselines on commonsense tasks, but mixing synthetic and filtered web data is best overall; phi-1.5-web reaches `44.6` GSM8K, `41.4` HumanEval, and `43.5` MBPP.
- **Toxicity probe**: on `86` manually designed prompts, phi-1.5 records `47` pass, `34` fail, and `4` did-not-understand judgments, versus fewer than `20` passes for both Llama2-7B and Falcon-7B in the authors' setup.

## Limitations

- The paper is a preprint focused on one model family and internal data pipeline; many claims about data quality versus scale are suggestive rather than tightly controlled causal conclusions.
- phi-1.5 is still a base model without instruction tuning or RLHF, so instruction following is inconsistent and the paper explicitly shows generation mistakes even in simple coding explanations.
- Safety improvements are relative, not solved: the model still fails `34/86` adversarial toxicity prompts and remains susceptible to biased or harmful completions.
- Benchmark gains are uneven: web data still helps materially on GSM8K, HumanEval, and MBPP, so synthetic textbook-like data is not shown to dominate across all capability axes.
- The report gives high-level descriptions of data synthesis and filtering, but not enough procedural detail to fully reproduce the dataset construction process from the paper alone.
