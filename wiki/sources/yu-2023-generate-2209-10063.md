## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

The paper argues that knowledge-intensive NLP need not rely on retrieve-then-read pipelines over external corpora. It proposes GENREAD, a generate-then-read framework in which a large language model first synthesizes a background document for a question, then answers conditioned on that generated context. The key extension is a clustering-based prompting scheme that uses distinct in-context question-document demonstrations to generate multiple complementary documents rather than repetitive samples. Across open-domain QA, fact verification, and dialogue, GENREAD consistently beats direct close-book answering and is competitive with or better than BM25, Contriever, DPR, and Google-based zero-shot pipelines. With FiD readers in supervised settings, clustering prompts reach `71.6` EM on TriviaQA and `54.4` on WebQ, and hybrid retrieval plus generation performs even better.

## Problem & Motivation

Knowledge-intensive tasks usually depend on retrieve-then-read systems, but the paper argues that this design inherits three structural weaknesses: retrieved chunks are fixed and often noisy, dense two-tower retrievers model only shallow query-document interaction, and large-scale retrieval requires pre-encoding and indexing an external corpus. At the same time, directly asking a large language model to output an answer underuses its parametric knowledge. The paper's motivation is therefore to reframe the LLM as a context generator: generate a question-specific background document that is easier to read, more targeted than retrieved passages, and can expose knowledge already stored in the model parameters.

## Method

- **Core factorization**: direct answering uses `\hat{a} = argmax_a p(a|q,\theta)`, while GENREAD introduces latent documents `d` and models `p(a|q) = \sum_i p(a|d_i, q) p(d_i|q)`. In practice the paper approximates this with a MAP document `\hat{d} = argmax_d p(d|q)`.
- **Step 1: generate background**: prompt an LLM with an instruction such as `Generate a background document from Wikipedia to answer the given question.` to produce contextual text instead of retrieving from Wikipedia. Zero-shot experiments use greedy decoding for reproducibility; multi-document generation also compares nucleus sampling with `p = 0.95`.
- **Step 2: read generated context**: feed the generated passage and question to a reading-comprehension prompt such as `Passage: {background} ... Question: {query} ... The answer is`. This reuses the same LLM in zero-shot settings or a smaller supervised reader.
- **Zero-shot backbone**: unless otherwise specified, experiments use InstructGPT `text-davinci-002`; the paper also reports `text-davinci-003`, OPT, and Codex variants in appendices.
- **Supervised reader**: replace the reader with FiD using T5-770M (`FiD-l`) or T5-3B (`FiD-xl`) and read `10` generated documents per example for cost reasons rather than the `100` documents common in prior FiD setups.
- **Clustering-based prompting**: first generate one initial document per training question, encode each `[q_i, d_i]` pair with GPT-3 into a `12,288`-dimensional embedding, cluster embeddings with K-means into `K` groups, then sample `n = 5` question-document pairs from each cluster as in-context demonstrations to generate `K` diverse documents.
- **Diversity baselines**: compare clustering prompts against naive repeated prompting, nucleus sampling, and top human-authored prompts selected from `29` prompt variants written by `30` annotators on a `200`-example validation set.
- **Reader training details**: FiD uses AdamW, `2,000` warm-up steps, dropout `0.1`, weight decay `0.01`; T5-770M runs on `1 x A100` with batch `16`, while T5-3B runs on `8 x A100` with per-GPU batch `2` (global batch `16`).

## Key Results

- **Zero-shot QA gains over direct answering**: GENREAD with InstructGPT improves NQ from `20.9` to `28.0`, TriviaQA from `57.5` to `59.0`, and WebQ from `18.6` to `24.6` EM relative to InstructGPT without documents.
- **Zero-shot beyond QA**: FEVER improves from `77.6` to `80.4` accuracy, FM2 from `59.4` to `65.5`, and WoW from `15.4 / 13.8` to `15.8 / 14.2` F1 / Rouge-L when using generated context.
- **Competitive with retrieve-then-read**: on TriviaQA and WebQ, zero-shot GENREAD (`59.0`, `24.6`) beats BM25 + InstructGPT (`52.2`, `15.8`), Contriever + InstructGPT (`51.3`, `16.6`), and DPR + InstructGPT (`53.8`, `20.2`), and also exceeds Google + InstructGPT on WebQ (`20.4`).
- **Best supervised setting**: GENREAD (`FiD-xl`, clustering) reaches `71.6` on TriviaQA, `54.4` on WebQ, and `45.6` on NQ, averaging `57.1`; this beats the sampling variant (`69.6 / 52.6 / 42.6`) by `+2.2` EM on average.
- **Hybrid is strongest**: merging retrieved and generated documents further raises scores to `74.3` on TriviaQA, `56.2` on WebQ, and `54.0` on NQ, average `61.5`.
- **Retrieval-style recall**: clustering prompts improve Recall@10 over nucleus sampling on TriviaQA from `81.6` to `82.9`, WebQ from `71.4` to `73.3`, and NQ from `66.2` to `70.9`.
- **Answer coverage**: clustering prompts improve answer coverage over nucleus sampling from `19.6` to `20.4` on TriviaQA with aliases, from `74.5` to `76.5` without aliases, and from `59.8` to `62.1` on WebQ.

## Limitations

- Parametric knowledge goes stale: unlike retrieve-then-read systems, GENREAD cannot simply swap in newer documents for time-dependent questions or new domains.
- Generated documents can hallucinate, so better readability does not guarantee faithfulness.
- Multi-document generation still suffers from redundancy; even with clustering prompts, diversity scales worse than retrieval as the number of documents increases.
- The strongest supervised setting is computationally heavy, requiring FiD training on A100 GPUs and expensive LLM generation.
- NQ is especially affected by temporal drift and annotation artifacts, which the paper itself identifies as a source of underperformance relative to DPR-based pipelines.
