## Summary

⚠ Possibly outdated: published 2023; re-verify against recent literature.

RePlug proposes a retrieval-augmented inference framework for black-box language models whose parameters and hidden states are inaccessible. Instead of changing the LM architecture, it retrieves relevant documents for an input context, prepends each document separately, and ensembles next-token probabilities across multiple LM calls using retrieval-based weights. The paper also introduces RePlug LSR, which tunes the dense retriever by matching its retrieval distribution to an LM-induced document preference distribution. Across language modeling, MMLU, and open-domain QA, the method improves GPT, OPT, BLOOM, and Codex variants, including `175B`-scale black-box models. The main contribution is showing that retrieval can materially help API-only or otherwise frozen large language models without white-box adaptation.

## Problem & Motivation

Prior retrieval-augmented language models usually assume white-box access: they either modify LM internals, fine-tune LM parameters, or depend on hidden-state representations for datastore indexing. That assumption breaks for API-served or prohibitively large LMs such as GPT-3, Codex, OPT-175B, and BLOOM-176B. The paper targets a more realistic deployment regime where only token probabilities or outputs are exposed. The motivation is twofold: retrieved evidence can mitigate long-tail knowledge gaps and hallucination, and a plug-in retrieval module is much cheaper and more deployable than re-training or re-architecting the LM itself.

## Method

- **Document retrieval**: given input context `x`, a dense dual-encoder retriever embeds both query and document, scores each candidate with cosine similarity `s(d, x) = cos(E(d), E(x))`, and retrieves top-`k` documents from corpus `D` using a FAISS index over precomputed document embeddings.
- **RePlug inference**: for each retrieved document `d` in top-`k`, the model forms `d ∘ x`, runs the black-box LM independently, and computes the next-token distribution as `p(y | x, D') = Σ_d p(y | d ∘ x) · λ(d, x)`.
- **Ensemble weights**: retrieval weights are normalized by similarity, `λ(d, x) = exp(s(d, x)) / Σ_d exp(s(d, x))`, so more relevant documents contribute more strongly to the final LM prediction.
- **Retriever backbone**: RePlug uses Contriever as the off-the-shelf retriever and supports either frozen retrieval or retriever tuning while keeping the LM itself fixed.
- **RePlug LSR supervision**: the tuned variant constructs a retrieval likelihood `P_R(d | x) = exp(s(d, x) / γ) / Σ_d exp(s(d, x) / γ)` and an LM likelihood `Q(d | x, y) = exp(P_LM(y | d, x) / β) / Σ_d exp(P_LM(y | d, x) / β)`, then minimizes `KL(P_R || Q)` over a batch.
- **Index refresh**: because retriever parameters change during tuning, document embeddings and the FAISS index are recomputed asynchronously every `T` steps rather than after every update.
- **Training data for LSR**: queries are `800K` sequences of `256` tokens from The Pile, split into `128`-token context `x` and `128`-token continuation `y`; the external corpus contains `36M` documents of `128` tokens.
- **Training hyperparameters**: retrieve top `20` documents during retriever training, use temperature `0.1` for both retrieval and LM likelihoods, Adam with learning rate `2e-5`, batch size `64`, warmup ratio `0.1`, re-embed every `3k` steps, and train for `25k` steps.
- **Inference setup**: for language modeling, RePlug / RePlug LSR retrieve top `10` documents and ensemble them in parallel; for MMLU and open-domain QA, the question is used as the query and top `10` Wikipedia documents are prepended separately.

## Key Results

- Language modeling on 8 GPT-2 / GPT-3 variants: RePlug improves bits-per-byte by `4.7%` on average, while RePlug LSR improves by `7.7%` on average.
- GPT-3 Davinci (`175B`) on The Pile: BPB drops from `0.80` to `0.77` with RePlug and to `0.75` with RePlug LSR, a `6.3%` relative gain for the tuned retriever.
- MMLU 5-shot with Codex (`175B`): overall score rises from `68.3` to `71.4` with RePlug and `71.8` with RePlug LSR; the latter is a `5.1%` improvement over baseline.
- Open-domain QA, few-shot Codex: Natural Questions improves from `40.6` to `44.7` with RePlug and `45.5` with RePlug LSR; TriviaQA improves from `73.6` to `76.8` and `77.3`, respectively.
- Compared with few-shot Atlas on NQ, RePlug LSR reaches `45.5` vs. Atlas `42.4`; on TriviaQA, it reaches `77.3` vs. Atlas `74.5`.
- Analysis shows gains are not from ensembling alone: random-document ensembling degrades performance, whereas relevant retrieval improves monotonically as the number of ensembled documents increases.

## Limitations

- The method increases inference cost because the black-box LM must run once per retrieved document, so accuracy gains come with roughly `k` LM calls at inference.
- RePlug is limited by LM context length because each pass can only prepend one retrieved document plus the original context.
- The framework lacks interpretability: the paper explicitly notes it is unclear when predictions rely on retrieved evidence versus parametric knowledge.
- Open-domain QA few-shot results remain below fully fine-tuned retrieval models trained on full supervision, and the authors note dataset overlap can confound those comparisons.
