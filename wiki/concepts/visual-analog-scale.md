## Definition

**Visual Analog Scale** (视觉模拟量表) — a continuous response scale used to capture subjective judgments by letting participants place a score along an interval rather than choose from a small set of discrete options.

## Key Points

- PRISM records both stated preferences and response evaluations on continuous `0-100` or `1-100` style sliders while hiding numeric values from participants.
- The same fine-grained attributes appear in survey and conversation settings, enabling comparison between abstract and contextual preferences.
- Duplicate-context analysis suggests the scale contains non-trivial same-rater noise, with a mean duplicate difference of `5.8`, motivating a practical tie threshold.
- The paper treats VAS-based feedback as expressive but potentially vulnerable to measurement invariance and interpersonal comparability problems.
