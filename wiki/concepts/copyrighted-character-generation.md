## Definition

**Copyrighted Character Generation** (受版权保护角色生成) — the phenomenon where a generative model produces outputs recognizable as specific copyrighted fictional characters, whether or not the prompt names them explicitly.

## Key Points

- The paper treats copyrighted-character generation as distinct from verbatim memorization because characters can appear across many poses, styles, and scenes while still remaining recognizable.
- It operationalizes the phenomenon with the `DETECT` metric over a curated set of `50` characters from `18` studios and franchises.
- The study shows that both explicit names and indirect prompts such as descriptive keywords can trigger copyrighted-character generation.
- The authors position this as both a legal-risk problem and a technical safety problem for multimodal generation systems.
