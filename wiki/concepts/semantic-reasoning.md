## Definition

**Semantic Reasoning** (语义推理) — reasoning that depends on language understanding, world knowledge, or commonsense judgments rather than purely formal computation.

## Key Points

- The paper motivates CoC partly by arguing that many reasoning tasks mix semantic judgments with numeric or symbolic computation.
- Example semantic subtasks include deciding whether a phrase is sarcastic or whether an object should count as a fruit.
- CoC preserves semantic reasoning capacity by routing non-executable helper functions to the LMulator instead of failing outright.
- Empirically, the method roughly matches or slightly exceeds Chain-of-Thought on NLP-tagged BIG-Bench Hard tasks while being much stronger on algorithmic ones.
