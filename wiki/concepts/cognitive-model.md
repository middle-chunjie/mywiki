## Definition

**Cognitive Model** (认知模型) — a formal model intended to explain or predict human cognition and behavior, rather than only optimize task performance.

## Key Points

- The paper operationalizes a cognitive model as a system that predicts human choices across psychological tasks, not merely one that solves those tasks well.
- It shows that a frozen large language model plus a learned linear readout can outperform hand-crafted domain-specific cognitive baselines on two decision-making paradigms.
- The resulting model also supports participant-level variation through random effects, extending cognitive modeling from population averages to individual differences.
- The hold-out experiment argues that a useful cognitive model should transfer across tasks, not just fit a single paradigm in isolation.
