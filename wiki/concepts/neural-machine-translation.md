## Definition

**Neural Machine Translation** (神经机器翻译) — end-to-end machine translation that learns continuous sequence mappings from source to target text with neural encoder-decoder architectures.

## Key Points

- The survey treats NMT as the dominant MT paradigm but emphasizes that vanilla bilingual training is data-hungry for low-resource pairs.
- It contrasts recurrent attention architectures with newer [[transformer]] models, noting that the latter had become the main backbone in recent multilingual work.
- The paper organizes low-resource NMT into augmentation, unsupervised, semi-supervised, multilingual, transfer, and zero-shot families rather than viewing NMT as a single recipe.
- It frames method choice as a function of corpus size, monolingual availability, language similarity, and compute budget.
