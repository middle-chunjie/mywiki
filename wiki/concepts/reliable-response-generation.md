## Definition

**Reliable Response Generation** (可靠响应生成) — the branch of generative information retrieval that directly generates user-facing answers while improving factuality, timeliness, attribution, and personalization.

## Key Points

- The survey organizes this area into four routes: internal knowledge memorization, external knowledge augmentation, response generation with citation, and personal information assistance.
- Reliability interventions span model scaling, data curation, training objectives, prompt engineering, and decoding strategies.
- External augmentation includes both retrieval-based pipelines such as RAG and tool-using systems such as search-engine or API agents.
- The paper stresses that response quality must be judged beyond fluency, using factuality, citation quality, safety, and human evaluation.
- Open problems include real-time knowledge access, bias, privacy leakage, and when retrieval or tool use is worth its cost.
