## Definition

**Collaborative Filtering** (协同过滤) — a recommendation paradigm that infers preferences from historical interaction patterns rather than item content alone.

## Key Points

- The paper frames lawsuit history as implicit feedback suitable for collaborative filtering style modeling.
- Tensor factorization is presented as a model-based collaborative filtering approach over plaintiff, defendant, and patent interactions.
- Pure collaborative filtering struggles because litigation events are extremely sparse relative to the patent universe.
- CTF extends collaborative filtering by adding a content-based patent encoder to reduce sparsity.
