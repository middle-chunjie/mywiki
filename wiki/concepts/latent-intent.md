## Definition

**Latent Intent** (潜在意图) — an unobserved preference factor that explains why a user interacts with an item under a specific semantic or contextual motive.

## Key Points

- The paper assumes user-item interactions are generated by multiple intent factors rather than a single homogeneous preference signal.
- DCCF defines separate user-side and item-side intent variables and approximates preference as an expectation over their joint contribution.
- Learnable intent prototypes provide a global context that can connect semantically similar users or items even without direct graph adjacency.
- The number of intent prototypes `K` is a major control knob; the paper reports the best default performance at `K = 128`.
- Too many intent prototypes can create redundancy and noise, as shown by the weaker results at `K = 256`.
