## Definition

**Metacognition** (元认知) — awareness of one's own reasoning process, including when a current strategy is sufficient and when an external aid or different strategy is required.

## Key Points

- The paper uses human metacognition as the central analogy for deciding between parametric reasoning and tool invocation.
- SMART converts implicit metacognitive heuristics into explicit natural-language justifications attached to each reasoning step.
- These step-level justifications are treated as training signals that help the agent learn a calibrated boundary between internal knowledge and external assistance.
- The resulting agents are described as more interpretable because their tool decisions come with readable rationales rather than opaque triggers.
