## Definition

**Large Multimodal Model** (大规模多模态模型) — a foundation model that extends large language modeling with additional input or output modalities such as images while preserving broad task generality through prompting.

## Key Points

- The paper frames GPT-4V as an early large multimodal model that inherits GPT-4-class language competence while adding strong visual understanding.
- It emphasizes that LMM behavior should be studied across text-only, single-image, and interleaved multimodal inputs rather than through a single benchmark.
- The qualitative evidence suggests LMMs can cover scene text, documents, charts, coding, temporal understanding, and agent-like interaction within one system.
- The authors argue future LMMs should move beyond image understanding toward interleaved generation, additional modalities, and more open-ended learning from web or physical environments.
