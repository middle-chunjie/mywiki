## Definition

**Clue Words** (线索词) — lexical patterns in context that provide evidence about where a target concept is likely to appear and how it is expressed.

## Key Points

- [[fang-2021-guided]] defines clue words as words or phrases that help explain why a nearby span should be recognized as a concept.
- Examples in the paper include definitional or discourse markers such as "is called an" in textbooks and "we introduce/propose/study" in scholarly documents.
- Three experts manually developed clue-word sets for all three datasets, and the final inventory is their intersection.
- The total clue-word counts are `36` for CSEN, `95` for KP-20K, and `24` for MTB.
- Clue words act as compact, interpretable domain knowledge and are especially helpful when supervised labels are scarce.
