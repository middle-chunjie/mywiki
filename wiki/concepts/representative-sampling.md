## Definition

**Representative Sampling** (代表性采样) — a sampling strategy that aims to match the composition of a target population along chosen observable attributes so that empirical results better reflect that population.

## Key Points

- PRISM includes census-targeted UK and US sub-samples to move beyond convenience sampling in alignment studies.
- The paper uses representative samples as the baseline population in its welfare analysis and compares them with smaller or demographically restricted samples.
- Results show that larger representative samples tend to dominate smaller or subgroup-only samples in downstream welfare outcomes.
- The authors stress that representativeness is partial because it only covers measured attributes and only for two countries.
