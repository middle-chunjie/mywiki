## Definition

**Formal Verification** (形式化验证) - the use of mathematically precise models and specifications to check whether a system satisfies desired properties.

## Key Points

- UCLID5 is the paper's target language and is used to model and verify state-transition systems.
- The motivating tasks involve declarations, initialization blocks, transition relations, and logic-level modeling choices.
- The paper treats formal verification languages as especially hard text-to-code targets because they are both low-resource and semantically rigid.
- SPEAC improves syntactic access to formal verification code by routing generation through Python and automated repair.
- The evaluation focuses on whether the resulting generated models compile and whether they are semantically close to correct.
