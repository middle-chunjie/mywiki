## Definition

**Long-Horizon Decision Making** (长时程决策) — sequential problem solving in which success depends on many interdependent steps and delayed credit assignment.

## Key Points

- The paper targets interactive environments where errors made early in a trajectory can be difficult to repair later.
- Task skills provide high-level guidance across an episode, while step skills help with local corrections inside long trajectories.
- D2Skill's retrieval and hindsight signals are explicitly designed to improve exploration efficiency in these long-horizon settings.
- ALFWORLD and WEBSHOP are used as representative benchmarks of this regime.
