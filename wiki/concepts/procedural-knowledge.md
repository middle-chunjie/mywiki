## Definition

**Procedural Knowledge** (程序性知识) — actionable know-how about how to carry out a workflow or solve a class of tasks, rather than merely stating facts or definitions.

## Key Points

- SkillsBench argues that the main value of skills comes from encoding procedures, conventions, and stepwise heuristics that pretrained models often lack.
- The benchmark distinguishes procedural knowledge from factual retrieval, few-shot prompting, and standalone tool descriptions.
- Tasks in healthcare, manufacturing, and finance benefit most when skills provide domain-specific procedures that agents cannot reliably reconstruct from latent knowledge alone.
- Self-generated skills underperform curated ones, suggesting that models often know they need procedures but cannot author sufficiently precise procedural guidance themselves.
