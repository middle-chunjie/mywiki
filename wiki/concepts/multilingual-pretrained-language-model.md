## Definition

**Multilingual Pretrained Language Model** (多语预训练语言模型) — a language model pretrained on text from multiple languages and later adapted to downstream tasks such as translation.

## Key Points

- The survey identifies multilingual PLMs such as mBART as especially promising for low-resource translation because they inject cross-lingual priors before fine-tuning.
- It discusses PLMs both as input-representation builders and as initialization sources for semi-supervised, multilingual, and transfer-learning setups.
- The paper treats these models as a practical alternative to hand-crafted lexical resources when direct bilingual supervision is scarce.
- It also notes an open question: how well such models work when the underlying monolingual data for a truly low-resource language is itself very limited.
