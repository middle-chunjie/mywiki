## Definition

**Large Language Model** (大语言模型) — a neural language model trained on very large text corpora to predict or generate tokens and perform downstream tasks through prompting or fine-tuning.

## Key Points

- Phi-4 is positioned as a `14B` parameter decoder-only large language model whose gains come primarily from better data and post-training rather than from a new backbone.
- The report emphasizes that LLM capability can be improved through data quality, training curriculum, and post-training design, not only through parameter or token scaling.
- Phi-4 is optimized for reasoning-heavy assistance tasks and benchmarked against both similar-size instruct models and much larger frontier LLMs.
