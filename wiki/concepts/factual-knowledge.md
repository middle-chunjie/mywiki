## Definition

**Factual Knowledge** (事实性知识) — structured knowledge about entities and their attributes or relations, often representable as subject-relation-object triples.

## Key Points

- The paper operationalizes factual knowledge as triples `(subject, relation, object)` and probes it through QA.
- Different relation types exhibit different memorization behavior even when subjects are similarly popular.
- The authors distinguish factual knowledge from broader commonsense and leave cross-domain generalization open.
- Both parametric and non-parametric memory are evaluated as mechanisms for answering factual questions.
