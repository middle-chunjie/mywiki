## Definition

**Compositional Generalization** — the ability to solve new problems formed by novel combinations of familiar entities, relations, or operations.

## Key Points

- WebSailor motivates its graph-based QA generation partly through compositional generalization.
- Randomly sampled subgraphs expose the model to new combinations of known entities and relations rather than memorized templates.
- The paper argues that these novel compositions help train reasoning strategies for complex information landscapes with no predefined path.
- This framing supports the paper's emphasis on hard-to-reduce uncertainty instead of conventional step-by-step multi-hop chains.
