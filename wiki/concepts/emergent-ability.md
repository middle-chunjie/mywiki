## Definition

**Emergent Ability** (涌现能力) — a capability that appears absent in smaller models but present in larger ones, often operationalized as a sharp and seemingly unpredictable change in task performance with scale.

## Key Points

- [[schaeffer-2023-emergent-2304-15004]] argues that many reported emergent abilities are artifacts of metric choice rather than genuine discontinuities in model outputs.
- The paper separates two defining properties of emergence claims: sharpness and unpredictability with respect to model scale.
- It shows that nonlinear or discontinuous task metrics can transform smooth token-level improvements into abrupt sequence-level curves.
- Better test-set resolution can reveal that supposedly incapable smaller models already have non-zero, above-chance performance.
- The paper therefore reframes emergence as an evaluation question, not only a capability question.
