## Definition

**Temporal Question Answering** (时序问答) — question answering in which the correct answer depends on an explicit or implicit temporal context such as the current week, year, or event state.

## Key Points

- REALTIME QA defines temporal QA through naturally occurring news questions whose answers can change from week to week.
- The benchmark does not restrict itself to historical timestamped questions; it focuses on present-time information needs such as ongoing sports, politics, and breaking news.
- A small fraction of the dataset remains event-triggered but not strictly time-sensitive, showing that real-world temporal QA mixes dynamic and stable facts.
- The paper argues that strong temporal QA requires both answer generation and retrieval systems that can adapt to newly published evidence.
