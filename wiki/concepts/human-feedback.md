## Definition

**Human Feedback** (人类反馈) — evaluative signals produced by people about model behavior, such as ratings, comparisons, critiques, or natural-language explanations, used to study or optimize AI systems.

## Key Points

- PRISM expands human feedback beyond binary A/B choices by collecting scalar ratings, fine-grained attribute judgments, and open-text explanations.
- The dataset links each feedback event to participant profiles, letting researchers study how preferences vary with demographics, geography, and self-described values.
- The paper argues that alignment outcomes depend materially on who provides feedback and in what conversational context they provide it.
- Even when prompt context is nearly fixed, PRISM shows substantial disagreement across participants, reinforcing that human feedback is heterogeneous rather than universal.
