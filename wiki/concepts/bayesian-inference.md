## Definition

**Bayesian Inference** (贝叶斯推断) — the process of updating beliefs about hidden variables or hypotheses by combining prior beliefs with observed evidence.

## Key Points

- [[wang-2024-large-2301-11916]] interprets in-context learning as approximate Bayesian inference over a latent concept variable given the prompt.
- The quality of a demonstration is defined by how strongly it raises the posterior probability of the task-specific concept.
- The theory links the Bayes-optimal classifier to conditioning on the correct latent concept rather than on arbitrary example similarity.
- The paper uses posterior concentration as the criterion for when in-context learning matches Bayes-optimal behavior.
