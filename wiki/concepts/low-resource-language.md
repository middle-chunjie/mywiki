## Definition

**Low-Resource Language** (低资源语言) — a language whose NLP and MT development is constrained by limited digital corpora, annotations, tools, and community infrastructure.

## Key Points

- The survey grounds the notion of low-resource language in both data scarcity and missing language technology support, not just speaker population.
- It reuses a six-class taxonomy from Joshi et al. to organize `2485` languages by public resource availability.
- For MT, the paper stresses that low-resource status is pair-specific because it depends on available parallel corpora rather than monolingual resources alone.
- The authors note that practical low-resource NMT work often operates with less than `0.5M` parallel sentence pairs, though the cutoff is heuristic.
