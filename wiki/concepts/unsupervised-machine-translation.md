## Definition

**Unsupervised Machine Translation** (无监督机器翻译) — machine translation learned without direct parallel corpora, typically by combining monolingual data, shared representations, and iterative reconstruction or back-translation.

## Key Points

- The survey describes unsupervised NMT as a three-stage pattern involving initialization, back-translation, and discriminative or adversarial learning signals.
- It emphasizes that the method relies on strong shared input representations such as bilingual dictionaries or cross-lingual embeddings.
- For truly low-resource settings, the paper identifies two main bottlenecks: insufficient monolingual corpora and lack of reliable shared lexical resources.
- It also reports that unsupervised NMT degrades for distant scripts, domain mismatch, and extremely low-resource conditions.
