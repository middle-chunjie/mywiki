## Definition

**Bloom Taxonomy** (布鲁姆分类法) — a hierarchical categorization of cognitive processes used to classify questions by the type and complexity of reasoning they require.

## Key Points

- The paper uses the revised Bloom taxonomy to categorize automatically generated questions into levels such as remember, understand, apply, analyze, evaluate, and create.
- These categories are not only descriptive; they are paired with IRT information curves to see which cognitive levels best discriminate between RAG systems.
- On the StackExchange task, the paper reports that evaluating and understanding are more informative than simple remembering questions.
- The taxonomy gives practitioners a way to diagnose whether an exam overemphasizes shallow recall versus more discriminative forms of reasoning.
