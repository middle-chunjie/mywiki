## Definition

**Diffusion Bridge** (扩散桥) — a diffusion-inspired transport process that connects two arbitrary endpoint distributions instead of only mapping Gaussian noise to a data distribution.

## Key Points

- The survey identifies bridging distributions as a distinct research direction beyond standard Gaussian-to-data generation.
- It motivates diffusion bridges with applications such as image-to-image translation and cell-distribution transport.
- Rectified Flow, alpha-blending, and Schrödinger-bridge-style methods are cited as representative approaches in this area.
- The paper treats diffusion bridges as extending the denoising and stochastic-process viewpoint into general distribution transport.
