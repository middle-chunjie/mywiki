## Definition

**Cross-Lingual Embedding** (跨语言嵌入) — a shared vector representation space in which semantically related units from different languages are aligned.

## Key Points

- The survey presents cross-lingual embeddings as a key input representation for unsupervised and multilingual low-resource NMT.
- It argues that putting languages into a shared semantic space simplifies encoder learning and improves transfer from higher-resource languages.
- The paper contrasts embedding-based inputs with surface-form overlap methods such as BPE and transliteration, which can remain brittle for extremely low-resource languages.
- It also points to multilingual pretrained models as a newer way to obtain stronger shared cross-lingual representations.
