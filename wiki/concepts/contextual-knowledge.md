## Definition

**Contextual Knowledge** (上下文知识) — knowledge supplied to a model at inference time through prompts, dialogue history, retrieved documents, or tools rather than stored in its parameters.

## Key Points

- The survey includes user prompts, interactive dialogue, retrieved web documents, and tool outputs as forms of contextual knowledge.
- Contextual knowledge helps LLMs access updated or task-specific information after deployment.
- The same flexibility introduces conflict risk when context disagrees with [[parametric-knowledge]] or when multiple contextual sources contradict one another.
- Model responses are influenced not just by factuality but also by contextual coherence, logical structure, relevance, and ordering.
