## Definition

**Fine-Grained Taxonomy** (细粒度分类体系) — a classification scheme that decomposes a broad problem area into narrowly scoped categories to enable more precise analysis and control.

## Key Points

- The paper builds a `44`-class taxonomy of unsafe topics spanning `4` high-level domains.
- It explicitly replaces vague categories such as general illegal activity with more specific subcategories.
- The taxonomy is refined iteratively through GPT-4-assisted mapping plus manual author review of unmatched items.
- The authors argue that finer granularity improves both benchmark coverage and stakeholder customizability.
