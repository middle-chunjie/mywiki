## Definition

**Vertical Exploration** (纵向探索) — an information-seeking strategy that follows links deeper within a website to uncover evidence hidden below the top-level page.

## Key Points

- The paper contrasts vertical exploration with horizontal search, arguing that search engines often surface shallow content but miss deeply nested official information.
- WebWalker operationalizes vertical exploration by recursively selecting subpages from clickable button sets on each visited page.
- The critic memory is used to preserve useful evidence across multiple levels of traversal, which helps manage long-context noise.
- When combined with standard RAG, vertical exploration improves access to information needed for harder multi-source questions.
