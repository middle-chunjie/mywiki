## Definition

**Reflection** (反思) — an explicit reasoning behavior in which a model revisits its intermediate analysis to reassess assumptions, errors, or alternative approaches.

## Key Points

- The paper treats reflection as a hallmark of long-CoT reasoning traces distilled from strong teacher models.
- Reflection is often signaled by phrases such as "Alternatively" or "Wait", but the paper shows the keywords themselves are not the core factor.
- What matters is that reflective moves appear in the right structural context, connected to previous steps and later revisions.
- Fine-tuned models learn to produce reflective patterns even under LoRA, suggesting the behavior is structurally learnable.
