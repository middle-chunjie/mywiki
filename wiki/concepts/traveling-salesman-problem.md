## Definition

**Traveling Salesman Problem** (旅行商问题) — a combinatorial optimization problem that seeks the shortest tour visiting each node exactly once and returning to the start.

## Key Points

- The paper uses TSP as its discrete optimization case study for testing LLM-based optimization.
- OPRO starts from `5` random tours and proposes up to `8` new tours per step from the historical trajectory.
- Problem instances are generated by sampling node coordinates from `[-100, 100]`, and solution quality is measured by optimality gap against a Gurobi oracle.
- The experiments show strong results on small instances, but performance deteriorates quickly as the number of nodes increases.
