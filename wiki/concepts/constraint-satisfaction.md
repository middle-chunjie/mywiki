## Definition

**Constraint Satisfaction** (约束满足) — the problem of finding outputs that jointly satisfy a specified set of conditions over a candidate space.

## Key Points

- KITAB frames constrained information retrieval as a constraint-satisfaction problem where the author is fixed and additional lexical, temporal, or entity predicates must all hold.
- The paper studies both satisfiable and intentionally unsatisfiable constraints to separate filtering ability from raw memorization.
- Even with complete supporting context, GPT-4 and GPT-3.5 often fail to satisfy all constraints, showing that verification remains difficult after retrieval.
- The benchmark uses list-valued outputs rather than single answers, so constraint satisfaction includes both per-item correctness and full-set completeness.
