## Definition

**Super-Resolution** (超分辨率) — the task of reconstructing a higher-resolution image from a lower-resolution input, often under constraints of fidelity, realism, and texture consistency.

## Key Points

- ReFIR is evaluated on real-world super-resolution settings using CUFED5 and WR-SR with `x4` degradations generated by the second-order Real-ESRGAN pipeline.
- The method improves both fidelity metrics such as PSNR and SSIM and perceptual metrics such as LPIPS, NIQE, and FID over strong diffusion-based baselines.
- The paper positions retrieval-augmented restoration as distinct from earlier reference-based super-resolution because it operates on billion-parameter LRMs and requires no retraining.
- The reported gains suggest retrieved references are especially useful for recovering high-frequency scene-specific textures.
