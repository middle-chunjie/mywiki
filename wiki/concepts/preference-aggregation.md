## Definition

**Preference Aggregation** (偏好聚合) — the process of combining many individual judgments or ratings into a collective ranking, score, or decision over candidate options.

## Key Points

- PRISM treats model selection as a social-choice problem because each participant observes only a partial set of models and uses subjective rating scales.
- The paper compares aggregation styles such as mean score, win rate, Elo-style rankings, and Pairwise Rank Centrality.
- Frequency invariance and comparability across different tournaments are key desiderata motivating the chosen aggregation function.
- Welfare conclusions in the paper depend on how individual preferences are aggregated and who is included in the aggregation pool.
