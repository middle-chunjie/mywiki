## Definition

**General-Purpose Reasoning** (通用推理) — reasoning ability that transfers across heterogeneous task families without requiring narrowly tailored demonstrations for each one.

## Key Points

- The paper introduces cross-task prompting as a probe of whether CoC behaves like a more general reasoner.
- In this setting, the prompt examples come from different problem families and provide only structural hints rather than task-specific solution patterns.
- CoC still outperforms direct prompting and Chain-of-Thought under cross-task evaluation, though all methods degrade relative to single-task prompts.
- The authors interpret this as early evidence that code-structured reasoning can transfer across diverse problems more robustly than purely verbal reasoning traces.
