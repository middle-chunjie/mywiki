## Definition

**Compositional Reasoning** (组合推理) — reasoning that requires combining multiple operations, conditions, or evidence pieces rather than retrieving a single directly matching fact.

## Key Points

- LOFT uses SQL-style tasks such as Spider and SParC to probe compositional reasoning over serialized databases.
- The paper finds that long-context models are notably weaker on compositional reasoning than on retrieval or multi-hop RAG.
- Operator-level analysis shows averaging is harder than counting, and inequality conditions are harder than equality conditions.
- The authors frame SQL-like reasoning as a case study for whether long-context models can subsume interfaces to structured data more broadly.
